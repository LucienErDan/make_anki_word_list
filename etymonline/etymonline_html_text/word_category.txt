title="Origin and meaning of category" href="/word/category#etymonline_v_5485"><em>category</em> (n.)</a><section class="word__defination--2q7ZH undefined"><p>1580s, in Aristotle&#x27;s logic, &quot;a highest notion,&quot; from Middle French <span class="foreign">catégorie</span>, from Late Latin <span class="foreign">categoria</span>, from Greek <span class="foreign">kategoria</span> &quot;accusation, prediction, category,&quot; verbal noun from <span class="foreign">kategorein</span> &quot;to speak against; to accuse, assert, predicate,&quot; from <span <p>The verb&#x27;s original sense of &quot;accuse&quot; had weakened to &quot;assert, name&quot; by the time Aristotle applied <span class="foreign">kategoria</span> to his 10 classes of things that can be named. Sense of &quot;any very wide and distinctive class, any comprehensive class of persons or things&quot; is from 1660s.</p>
