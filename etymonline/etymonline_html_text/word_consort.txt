A <span class="foreign">prince consort</span> (1837) is a prince who is the husband of a queen but himself has no royal authority (the most notable being Prince Albert of Saxe-Coburg and Gotha, husband of Queen Victoria; the initial proposal in Parliament in 1840 was to call him <span class="foreign">king-consort</span>); <span class="foreign">queen consort</span> is attested from 1667.
