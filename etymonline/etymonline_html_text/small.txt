<blockquote>My sister ... is as white as a lilly, and as small as a wand. [Shakespeare, &quot;Two Gentlemen of Verona,&quot; 1591]</blockquote>
<p>Sense of &quot;not large, of little size&quot; developed in Old English. Of children, &quot;young,&quot; from mid-13c. Meaning &quot;inferior in degree or amount&quot; is from late 13c. Meaning &quot;trivial, unimportant&quot; is from mid-14c. Sense of &quot;having little property or trade&quot; is from 1746. That of &quot;characterized by littleness of mind or spirit, base, low, mean&quot; is from 1824. As an adverb by late 14c.</p>
