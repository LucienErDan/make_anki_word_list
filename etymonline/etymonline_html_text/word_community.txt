title="Origin and meaning of community" href="/word/community#etymonline_v_17249"><em>community</em> (n.)<i class="fa fa-image" style="margin-left:5px;color:#888"></i></a><section class="word__defination--2q7ZH undefined"><p>late 14c., &quot;a number of people associated together by the fact of residence in the same locality,&quot; also &quot;the common people&quot; (not the rulers or the clergy), from Old French <span class="foreign">comunité</span> &quot;community, commonness, everybody&quot; (Modern French <span class="foreign">communauté</span>), from Latin <span class="foreign">communitatem</span> (nominative <span class="foreign">communitas</span>) &quot;community, society, fellowship, friendly intercourse; courtesy, condescension, affability,&quot; from <span class="foreign">communis</span> &quot;common, public, general, shared by all or many&quot; (see <span class="crossreference">common</span> (adj.)).</p>
<p>Latin <span class="foreign">communitatem</span> &quot;was merely a noun of quality ... meaning &#x27;fellowship, community of relations or feelings&#x27; &quot; [OED], but in Medieval Latin it came to be used concretely to mean &quot;a society, a division of people.&quot; In English, the meaning &quot;common possession or enjoyment&quot; is from c. 1400. Sense of &quot;a society or association of persons having common interests or occupations&quot; also is from c. 1400.</p>
<p>An Old English word for &quot;community&quot; was <span class="foreign">gemænscipe</span> &quot;community, fellowship, union, common ownership,&quot; from <span class="foreign">mæne</span> &quot;common, public, general,&quot; and thus probably composed from the same PIE roots as <span class="foreign">communis</span>. Middle English also had <span class="foreign">commonty</span> (late 14c.) &quot;the common people; a community,&quot; also later meaning &quot;land held in common&quot; (c. 1600).</p>
<p><span class="foreign">Community service</span> as a criminal sentence is recorded from 1972, American English. <span class="foreign">Community college,</span> one offering post-secondary instruction geared to local needs and interests, is recorded from 1947, American English. <span class="foreign">Community chest</span> &quot;fund made up of individual donations to meet the needs of charity and social welfare in a community&quot; is from 1919, American English.</p>
<blockquote>The Community Chest is a device to consolidate all these separate [charitable] appeals, and go before the people once a year with a budget which appropriates to each organization the amount which it needs to make up the difference between its income from other sources, and its necessary expenses. By this means not only are the charities relieved of financial worry and adequately supported, but the public is spared the irritation of constant solicitation, which is all the more unbusinesslike because it is decentralized and not subject to outside disinterested scrutiny. [&quot;New Jersey Municipalities,&quot; December 1919]</blockquote></section></object><a data-more="true" class="word_4pc__footer--2yfj3" href="/word/community#etymonline_v_17249"><span>Relate entries &amp;
