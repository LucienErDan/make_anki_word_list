<p>The difficulty with the plural began in Old English, where the nominative plural was at first <span class="foreign">cild</span>, identical with the singular, then c.975 a plural form <span class="foreign">cildru</span> (genitive <span class="foreign">cildra</span>) arose, probably for clarity&#x27;s sake, only to be re-pluraled late 12c. as <span class="foreign">children</span>, which is thus a double plural. Middle English plural <span class="foreign">cildre</span> survives in Lancashire dialect <span class="foreign">childer</span> and in <span class="crossreference">Childermas</span>.</p>
