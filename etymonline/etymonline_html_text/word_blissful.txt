The Hyperboreans (Greek <span class="foreign">Hyperboreoi</span>) were an imagined northern people believed by the ancients to be distinguished by piety and happiness; their land being &quot;beyond&quot; (hence, out of reach of) the North Wind, it was thought to be a blissful paradise. <p>The word&#x27;s considerable sense development moved from &quot;happy&quot; to &quot;blessed&quot; to &quot;pious,&quot; to &quot;innocent&quot; (c. 1200), to &quot;harmless,&quot; to &quot;pitiable&quot; (late 13c.), &quot;weak&quot; (c. 1300), to &quot;feeble in mind, lacking in reason, foolish&quot; (1570s).
