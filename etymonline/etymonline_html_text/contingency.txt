<p>Meaning &quot;the prize in a contest of strength, skill, speed, etc.&quot; is by 1620s; plural <span class="foreign">stakes</span>, &quot;sum of money to be won in a (horse) race,&quot; is recorded by 1690s (compare <span class="crossreference">sweepstakes</span>). <p>In English frequently in plural, <span class="foreign">chances</span>. The word&#x27;s notions of &quot;opportunity&quot; and &quot;randomness&quot; are as old as the record of it in English and now all but crowd out its original notion of &quot;mere occurrence.&quot; Meaning &quot;fortuity, absence of any cause why an event should happen or turn out as it does, variability viewed as a real agent&quot; is from c. 1400.</p>
<p><span class="foreign"><strong>Main chance</strong></span> &quot;probability that offers greatest advantage,&quot; hence &quot;thing of most importance&quot; is from 1570s.
