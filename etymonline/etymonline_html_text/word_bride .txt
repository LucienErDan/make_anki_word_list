<p>Gothic cognate <span class="foreign">bruþs</span>, however, meant &quot;daughter-in-law,&quot; and the form of the word borrowed from Old High German into Medieval Latin (<span class="foreign">bruta</span>) and Old French (<span class="foreign">bruy</span>) had only this sense. On the same notion, some trace the word itself to the PIE verbal root <span class="crossreference">*bhreu-</span>, which forms words for cooking and brewing, as this likely was the daughter-in-law&#x27;s job.
