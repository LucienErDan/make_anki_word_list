From the purely physical sense come &quot;palace, residence of a sovereign&quot; (c. 1200), &quot;enclosed space connected with a building or buildings&quot; (early 14c.), and the sporting sense &quot;smooth, level plot of ground on which a ball game is played&quot; (1510s, originally of tennis). Also &quot;short arm of a public street, enclosed on three sides by buildings&quot; (1680s), formerly noted for poverty or as business districts.</p>
<p>From the notion of &quot;surroundings of a sovereign in his regal state&quot; (c. 1200) comes the legal meaning &quot;a tribunal for judicial investigation&quot; (c. 1300, early assemblies for justice were overseen by the sovereign personally), also &quot;hall or chamber where justice is administered&quot; (c. 1300). As an adjective, &quot;pertaining to a court,&quot; late 13c.</p></section></object><a data-more="true" class="word_4pc__footer--2yfj3" href="/word/court#etymonline_v_19182"><span>Relate entries &amp;
