<p>From 1708 as &quot;anything attached to a moving body that retards its progress.&quot; As the name of a device for retarding or stopping the rotation of wheels, 1795. Sense of &quot;annoying, boring person or thing&quot; is 1813, perhaps from the mechanical senses or the notion of something that must be dragged as an impediment.</p>

<p>Sense of &quot;women&#x27;s clothing worn by a man&quot; is by 1870, perhaps originally theater slang, from the sensation of long skirts trailing on the floor (another guess is Yiddish <span class="foreign">trogn</span> &quot;to wear,&quot; from German <span class="foreign">tragen</span>); <span class="foreign">drag queen</span> &quot;male transvestite or cross-dresser&quot; is from 1941.</p>

<p><span class="foreign">Drag</span> racing (1947), is said to be from thieves&#x27; slang <span class="foreign">drag</span> &quot;automobile&quot; (1935), perhaps ultimately from slang sense of &quot;wagon, buggy&quot; (1755), because a horse would drag it. By 1851 this was transferred to &quot;street,&quot; as in the phrase <span class="foreign">main drag</span> (which some propose as the source of the racing sense).</p>
