<p>A fanatical Muslim sect in the mountains of Lebanon at the time of the Crusades, under leadership of the &quot;Old Man of the Mountains&quot; (which translates Arabic <span class="foreign">shaik-al-jibal</span>, name applied to Hasan ibu-al-Sabbah), they had a reputation for murdering opposing leaders after intoxicating themselves by eating hashish. Their reputation has spread in Western Europe 12c.-13c.</p>

<p>The plural suffix <span class="foreign">-in</span> was mistaken in Europe for part of the word (compare <span class="crossreference">Bedouin</span>). Middle English had the word as <span class="foreign">hassais</span> (mid-14c.), from Old French <span class="foreign">hassasis</span>, <span class="foreign">assasis</span>, which is from the Arabic word.</p>

<blockquote>The generalization of the sect&#x27;s nickname to the meaning of any sort of assassin happened in Italian at the start of the 14th century. The word with the generalized meaning was often used in Italian in the 14th and 15th centuries.
