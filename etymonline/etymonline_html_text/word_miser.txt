and meaning of miser" href="/word/miser#etymonline_v_16267"><em>miser</em> (n.)</a><section class="word__defination--2q7ZH undefined"><p>1540s, &quot;miserable person, wretch,&quot; from Latin <span class="foreign">miser</span> (adj.) &quot;unhappy, wretched, pitiable, in distress,&quot; a word for which &quot;no acceptable PIE pedigree has been found&quot; [de Vaan]. The oldest English sense now is obsolete; the main modern meaning of &quot;money-hoarding person&quot; (&quot;one who in wealth conducts himself as one afflicted with poverty&quot; - Century Dictionary) is recorded by 1560s, from the presumed unhappiness of such people. The older sense is preserved in <span class="crossreference">miserable</span>, <span class="crossreference">misery</span>, etc.</p>

<p>Besides general wretchedness, the Latin word connoted also &quot;intense erotic love&quot; (compare slang <span class="foreign">got it bad</span> &quot;deeply infatuated&quot;) and hence was a favorite word of Catullus.
