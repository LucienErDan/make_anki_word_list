<p>From early 14c. as &quot;having a twofold character or relation,&quot; also &quot;consisting of two in a set together; being a pair, coupled.&quot; From mid-14c. as &quot;characterized by duplicity.&quot; The earliest recorded use in English is c. 1200, in <span class="foreign">double-feast</span> &quot;important Church festival.&quot;</p>
<p><span class="foreign">Double-chinned</span> is from late 14c.; <span class="foreign">double-jointed,</span> of persons, is by 1828. The baseball <span class="foreign">double play</span> is by 1866.</p>
