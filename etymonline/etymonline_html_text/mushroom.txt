title="Origin and meaning of mushroom" href="/word/mushroom#etymonline_v_19297"><em>mushroom</em> (n.)</a><section class="word__defination--2q7ZH undefined"><p>a word applied at first to almost any of the larger fungi but later to the agaricoid fungi and especially the edible varieties, mid-15c., <span class="foreign">muscheron</span>, <span class="foreign">musseroun</span> (attested 1327 as a <p>Barnhart says &quot;of uncertain origin.&quot; Klein calls it &quot;a word of pre-Latin origin, used in the North of France;&quot; OED says it usually is held to be a derivative of French <span class="foreign">mousse</span> &quot;moss&quot; (from Germanic), and Weekley agrees, saying it is properly &quot;applied to variety which grows in moss,&quot; but Klein says they have &quot;nothing in common.&quot; For the final <span class="foreign">-m</span> Weekley refers to <span class="crossreference">grogram</span>, <span class="crossreference">vellum</span>, <span class="crossreference">venom</span>. Modern spelling is from 1560s.</p>
