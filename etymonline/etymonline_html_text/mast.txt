<p>The single mast of an old ship was the boundary between the quarters of the officers and those of the crew, hence <span class="foreign">before the mast</span> &quot;serving as an ordinary seaman&quot; in the title of Dana&#x27;s book, etc.</p>
<blockquote>In all large vessels the masts are composed of several lengths, called <span class="foreign">lower mast</span>, <span class="foreign">topmast</span>, and <span class="foreign">topgallantmast</span>. The royalmast is now made in one piece with the topgallantmast. A mast consisting of a single length is called a <span class="foreign">pole-mast</span>.
