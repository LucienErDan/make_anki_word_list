<p>Middle English had two forms: <span class="foreign">cony, conny</span>, also <span class="foreign">coning, cunin, conyng</span>; Old French had <span class="foreign">conil</span> alongside <span class="foreign">conin</span>. <p><span class="crossreference">Rabbit</span> arose 14c. to mean the young of the species, but gradually pushed out the older word 19c., after British slang picked up <span class="foreign">coney</span> as a punning synonym for <span class="foreign">cunny</span> &quot;cunt&quot; (compare <span class="foreign">connyfogle</span> &quot;to deceive (a woman) in order to win sexual favors&quot;). <p>Association with &quot;cheating&quot; is from <span class="foreign">coney-catcher</span>, &quot;A term made famous by [Robert] Greene in 1591, and in great vogue for 60 years after&quot; [OED]</p>
