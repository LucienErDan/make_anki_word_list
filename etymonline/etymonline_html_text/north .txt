word_thumbnail__name--1khEg" title="Origin and meaning of north" href="/word/north#etymonline_v_9803"><em>north</em> </a><section class="word__defination--2q7ZH undefined"><p>Old English <span class="foreign">norð</span> &quot;northern&quot; (adj.), &quot;northwards&quot; (adv.), from Proto-Germanic <span class="foreign">*nurtha-</span> (source also of Old Norse <span class="foreign">norðr</span>, Old Saxon <span class="foreign">north</span>, Old Frisian <span class="foreign">north</span>, Middle Dutch <span class="foreign">nort</span>, Dutch <span class="foreign">noord</span>, German <span class="foreign">nord</span>), which is probably an IE word, but of uncertain origin.</p>
<p>It might be ultimately from PIE <span class="foreign">*ner-</span> (1) &quot;left,&quot; also &quot;below&quot; (source also of Sanskrit <span class="foreign">narakah</span> &quot;hell,&quot; Greek <span class="foreign">neretos</span> &quot;deeper, lower down,&quot; <span class="foreign">enerthen</span> &quot;from beneath,&quot; Oscan-Umbrian <span class="foreign">nertrak</span> &quot;left&quot;), as north is to the left when one faces the rising sun. The same notion apparently underlies Old Irish <span class="foreign">tuath</span> &quot;left; northern;&quot; Arabic <span class="foreign">shamal</span> &quot;left hand; north.&quot; Or perhaps the notion is that the sun is at its &quot;lowest&quot; point when in the north.</p>
<p>The usual word for &quot;north&quot; in the Romance languages ultimately is from English, for example Old French <span class="foreign">north</span> (Modern French <span class="foreign">nord</span>), borrowed from Old English <span class="foreign">norð</span>; Italian <span class="foreign">nord</span>, Spanish <span class="foreign">norte</span> are borrowed from French.</p>
<blockquote class="poetry">Ask where&#x27;s the North? At York &#x27;tis on the Tweed;</blockquote>
<blockquote class="poetry">In Scotland at the Orcades; and there</blockquote>
<blockquote class="poetry">At Greenland, Zembla, or the Lord knows where.</blockquote>
<blockquote class="poetry">[Pope, &quot;Essay on Man&quot;]</blockquote>
