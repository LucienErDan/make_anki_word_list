title="Origin and meaning of cowboy" href="/word/cowboy#etymonline_v_29007"><em>cowboy</em> (n.)</a><section class="word__defination--2q7ZH undefined"><p>1725, &quot;boy who tends to cows and drives them to and from pasture,&quot; from <span class="crossreference">cow</span> (n.) + <span class="crossreference">boy</span>.</p>
<p>American-English sense of &quot;man employed to have care of grazing cattle on the Great Plains for a stockman or ranch, doing his work on horseback&quot; is by 1849. Earlier it was an insulting name for a band of marauding loyalists in the neighborhood of New York during the Revolution (1775). In figurative use by 1942 for &quot;brash and reckless young man&quot; (as an adjective meaning &quot;reckless,&quot; from 1920s).</p>
<p>The oldest word for &quot;one whose occupation is the care of cattle&quot; is <span class="foreign"><strong>cowherd</strong></span> (late Old English). <span class="foreign"><strong>Cowhand</strong></span> is first attested 1852 in American English (see <span class="crossreference">hand</span> (n.)). <span class="foreign"><strong>Cowpoke</strong></span> (said to be 1881, not in popular use until 1940s) was said to be originally restricted to those who prodded cattle onto railroad cars with long poles. <span class="foreign"><strong>Cowboys and Indians</strong></span> as a children&#x27;s game (imitating movie serials, etc.) is by 1941.</p></section></object><a data-more="true" class="word_4pc__footer--2yfj3" href="/word/cowboy#etymonline_v_29007"><span>Relate entries &amp; more</span> <i class="fa fa-chevron-right" style="opacity:0"></i></a></div><div data-ad-container="true" class="adContainer--6CVz1 horizontal"><span class="adLabel--35hfi">Advertisement</span><ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-7093273770345366" style="display:block;width:100%" data-ad-format="fluid" data-ad-slot="1604734010" data-ad-layout-key="-fb+5w+4e-db+86"></ins></div><div class="word--C9UPa word_4pc--2SZw8"><object><a class="word__name--TTbAA word_thumbnail__name--1khEg" title="Origin and meaning of *gwou-" href="/word/*gwou-#etymonline_v_52797">*gwou- </a><section class="word__defination--2q7ZH undefined"><p>Proto-Indo-European root meaning &quot;ox, bull, cow,&quot; perhaps ultimately imitative of lowing; compare Sumerian <span class="foreign">gu</span>, Chinese <span class="foreign">ngu</span>, <span class="foreign">ngo</span> &quot;ox.&quot;</p>
