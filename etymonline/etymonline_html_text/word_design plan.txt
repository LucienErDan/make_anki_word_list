<p>In art, &quot;a drawing, especially an outline,&quot; 1630s. The artistic sense was taken into French as <span class="foreign">dessin</span> from Italian <span class="foreign">disegno</span>, from <span class="foreign">disegnare</span> &quot;to mark out,&quot; from Latin <span class="foreign">designare</span> &quot;mark out, devise, choose, designate, appoint&quot; (which is also ultimately the source of the English verb), from <span class="foreign">de</span> &quot;out&quot; (see <span class="crossreference">de-</span>) + <span class="foreign">signare</span> &quot;to mark,&quot; from <span class="foreign">signum</span> &quot;identifying mark, sign&quot; (see <span class="crossreference">sign</span> (n.)).</p>
<blockquote>[T]he artistic sense was taken into Fr. and gradually differentiated in spelling, so that in mod.F. <span class="foreign">dessein</span> is &#x27;purpose, plan&#x27;, <span class="foreign">dessin</span> &#x27;design in art&#x27;. Eng. on the contrary uses <span class="foreign">design</span>, conformed to the verb, in both senses. [OED]</blockquote>
<p>General (non-scheming) meaning &quot;a plan our outline&quot; is from 1590s. Meaning &quot;the practical application of artistic principles&quot; is from 1630s. Sense of &quot;artistic details that go to make up an edifice, artistic creation, or decorative work&quot; is from 1640s.</p></section></object><a data-more="true" class="word_4pc__footer--2yfj3" href="/word/design#etymonline_v_46757"><span>Relate entries &amp; more</span> <i class="fa fa-chevron-right" style="opacity:0"></i></a></div><div class="word--C9UPa word_4pc--2SZw8"><object><a class="word__name--TTbAA word_thumbnail__name--1khEg" title="Origin and meaning of design" href="/word/design#etymonline_v_5656"><em>design</em> (v.)</a><section class="word__defination--2q7ZH undefined"><p>late 14c., &quot;to make, shape,&quot; ultimately from Latin <span class="foreign">designare</span> &quot;mark out, point out; devise; choose, designate, appoint,&quot; from <span class="foreign">de</span> &quot;out&quot; (see <span class="crossreference">de-</span>) + <span class="foreign">signare</span> &quot;to mark,&quot; from <span class="foreign">signum</span> &quot;identifying mark, sign&quot; (see <span class="crossreference">sign</span> (n.)).</p>
<p>The Italian verb <span class="foreign">disegnare</span> in 16c. developed the senses &quot;to contrive, plot, intend,&quot; and &quot;to draw, paint, embroider, etc.&quot; French took both these senses from Italian, in different forms, and passed them on to English, which uses <span class="foreign">design</span> in all senses.</p>
<p>From 1540s as &quot;to plan or outline, form a scheme;&quot; from 1703 as &quot;to contrive for a purpose.&quot; Transitive sense of &quot;draw the outline or figure of,&quot; especially of a proposed work, is from 1630s; meaning &quot;plan and execute, fashion with artistic skill&quot; is from 1660s.
