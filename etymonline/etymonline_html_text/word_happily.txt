<p>Originally identical with <span class="crossreference">astronomy</span> and including scientific observation and description. The special sense of &quot;astronomy applied to prediction of events&quot; was divided into <span class="foreign">natural astrology</span> &quot;the calculation and foretelling of natural phenomenon&quot; (tides, eclipses, dates of Church festivals, etc.), and <span class="foreign">judicial astrology</span> &quot;the art of judging occult influences of stars and planets on human affairs.&quot;</p>
<p>In Latin and later Greek, <span class="foreign">astronomia</span> tended to be more scientific than <span class="foreign">astrologia</span>.  In English, the differentiation between <span class="foreign">astrology</span> and <span class="foreign">astronomy</span> began late 1400s and by late 17c. this word was limited to the sense of &quot;reading influences of the stars and their effects on human destiny.&quot;</p>
