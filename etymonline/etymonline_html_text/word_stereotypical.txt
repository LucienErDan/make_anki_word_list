It could be used without conscious racism or contempt until circa World War II. When the word fell from polite usage, collateral casualties included the enormously popular children&#x27;s book &quot;The Story of Little Black Sambo&quot; (by Helen Bannerman), which is about an East Indian child, and the Sambo&#x27;s Restaurant chain, a U.S. pancake-specialty joint originally opened in Santa Barbara, Calif., in 1957 (the name supposedly from a merging of the names of the founders, Sam Battistone and Newell &quot;Bo&quot; Bohnett, but the chain&#x27;s decor and advertising leaned heavily on the book), which once counted 1,200 units coast-to-coast. Civil rights agitation against it began in 1970s and the chain collapsed, though the original restaurant still is open. <p>The letter existed in Greek, but was little used and not alphabetized; the stereotypical connection with <span class="foreign">-u-</span> began in Latin. Anglo-Saxon scribes adopted the habit at first, but later used spellings with <span class="foreign">cw-</span> or <span class="foreign">cu-</span>. The <span class="foreign">qu-</span> pattern returned to English with the Norman Conquest and had displaced <span class="foreign">cw-</span> by c. 1300. In some spelling variants of late Middle English, <span class="foreign">quh-</span> also took work from <span class="foreign">wh-</span>, especially in Scottish and northern dialects, for example Gavin Douglas, Provost of St. Giles, in his vernacular &quot;Aeneid&quot; of 1513:</p>
<blockquote class="poetry">Lyk as the rois in June with hir sueit smell</blockquote>
<blockquote class="poetry">The marygulde or dasy doith excell.</blockquote>
<blockquote class="poetry">Quhy suld I than, with dull forhede and vane,</blockquote>
<blockquote class="poetry">With ruide engine and barrand emptive brane,</blockquote>
<blockquote class="poetry">With bad harsk speche and lewit barbour tong,</blockquote>
<blockquote class="poetry">Presume to write quhar thi sueit bell is rong,</blockquote>
<blockquote class="poetry">Or contirfait sa precious wourdis deir?</blockquote>
