&quot;cover or bag for clothes,&quot; from Middle French <span class="foreign">toilette</span> &quot;a cloth; a bag for clothes,&quot; diminutive of <span class="foreign">toile</span> &quot;cloth, net&quot; (see <span class="crossreference">toil</span> (n.2)). <span class="foreign">Toilet</span> acquired an association with upper class dressing by 18c., through the specific sense &quot;a fine cloth cover on the dressing table for the articles spread upon it;&quot; thence &quot;the articles, collectively, used in dressing&quot; (mirror, bottles, brushes, combs, etc.).
