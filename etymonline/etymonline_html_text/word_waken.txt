<p>These usage guides for<span class="crossreference"> awake</span>, <span class="crossreference">awaken</span>, <span class="foreign">wake</span>, <span class="crossreference">waken</span> are distilled from those in Fowler and Century Dictionary:</p>
<p>1. <span class="foreign">Wake</span> is the ordinary working verb; it alone has the sense &quot;be or remain awake&quot; (chiefly in <span class="foreign">waking</span>).</p>
<p>2. <span class="foreign">Awake</span> and <span class="foreign">awaken</span> are chiefly used in figurative or transferred applications (<span class="foreign">A rude awakening</span>).</p>
<p>3. <span class="foreign">Waken</span> and <span class="foreign">awaken</span> tend to be restricted to the transitive sense, <span class="foreign">awake</span> being preferred in the senses related to arousing from actual sleep.</p>
<p>4.
