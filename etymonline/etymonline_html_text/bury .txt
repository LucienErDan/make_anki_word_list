The vowel was uncertain through Middle English; for vowel evolution, see <span class="crossreference">bury</span>.</p>
<blockquote>Kissing, as an expression of affection or love, is unknown among many races, and in the history of mankind seems to be a late substitute for the more primitive rubbing of noses, sniffing, and licking. [Carl Darling Buck, &quot;A Dictionary of Selected Synonyms in the Principal Indo-European Languages,&quot; 1949, p.1113]</blockquote>
<p>There appears to be no common Indo-European root word for &quot;kiss,&quot; though suggestions of a common <span class="foreign">ku-</span> sound may be found in the Germanic root and Greek <span class="foreign">kynein</span> &quot;to kiss,&quot; Hittite <span class="foreign">kuwash-anzi</span> &quot;they kiss,&quot; Sanskrit <span class="foreign">cumbati</span> &quot;he kisses.&quot; Some languages make a distinction between the kiss of affection and that of erotic love (compare Latin <span class="foreign">saviari</span> &quot;erotic kiss,&quot; vs. <span class="foreign">osculum</span>, literally &quot;little mouth&quot;). French <span class="foreign">embrasser</span> &quot;kiss,&quot; but literally &quot;embrace,&quot; came about in 17c. when the older word <span class="foreign">baiser</span> (from Latin <span class="foreign">basiare</span>) acquired an obscene connotation.</p>
<p>To <span class="foreign">kiss the cup</span> &quot;drink liquor&quot; is early 15c. To <span class="foreign">kiss the dust</span> &quot;die&quot; is from 1835. To <span class="foreign">kiss and tell</span> is from 1690s. Figurative (and often ironic) <span class="foreign">kiss (something) goodbye</span> is from 1935. To <span class="foreign">kiss (someone) off</span> &quot;dismiss, get rid of&quot; is from 1935, originally of the opposite sex. Insulting invitation <span class="foreign">kiss my arse</span> (or <span class="foreign">ass</span>) as an expression of contemptuous rejection is from at least 1705, but probably much older (see &quot;The Miller&#x27;s Tale&quot;).</p></section></object><a data-more="true" class="word_4pc__footer--2yfj3" href="/word/kiss#etymonline_v_1880"><span>Relate entries &amp; more</span> <i class="fa
