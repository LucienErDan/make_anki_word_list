<p>The <span class="foreign">-gh-</span> was an Anglo-French scribal attempt to render the Germanic hard <span class="foreign">-h-</span> sound, which has since disappeared from this word. Meaning &quot;a consideration which puts something in a certain view&quot; (as in <span class="foreign"><strong>in light of</strong></span>) is from 1680s. Short for <span class="foreign">traffic light</span> from 1938. Quaker use is by 1650s; <span class="foreign"><strong>New Light</strong></span><strong>/</strong><span class="foreign"><strong>Old Light</strong></span> in church doctrine also is from 1650s. <blockquote><span class="foreign">Ðu eart dohtor min, minra eagna leoht</span> [Juliana].</blockquote>
