<p>The basic sense is &quot;method by which something is divided,&quot; which arose in Old French and led to the range of modern meanings via the notion of &quot;something invented or fitted to a particular use or purpose,&quot; hence &quot;an invention; a constructed tool; inventiveness; a contriving, a plan or scheme.&quot;</p>

<p>Originally any winged insect (moths, gnats, beetles, locusts, hence <span class="crossreference">butterfly</span>, etc.) and long used by farmers and gardeners for any insect parasite. <span class="foreign">Flies</span> figuratively for &quot;large numbers&quot; of anything is from 1590s. <p>The sense of &quot;a flight, flying&quot; is from mid-15c. Baseball <span class="foreign"><strong>fly ball</strong></span> attested by 1866. To do something <span class="foreign"><strong>on the fly</strong></span> is 1856, apparently from baseball.</p>
