<p>The apparent sense evolution in Middle English was through &quot;to procrastinate, delay, be tardy in coming&quot; (late 12c.), to &quot;linger, remain, stay, sojourn,&quot; to &quot;make a home, abide as a permanent resident&quot; (mid-14c.). <p>The fork in meaning probably comes via the notion of  &quot;not responding through ignorance or incomprehension.&quot; The Old English, Old Saxon (<span class="foreign">dumb</span>), Gothic (<span class="foreign">dumbs</span>), and Old Norse (<span class="foreign">dumbr</span>) forms of the word meant only &quot;mute, speechless;&quot; in Old High German (<span class="foreign">thumb</span>) it meant both this and &quot;stupid,&quot; and in Modern German this latter became the only sense (the sense of &quot;mute, speechless&quot; being expressed by <span class="foreign">stumm</span>). Meaning &quot;foolish, ignorant&quot; was occasional in Middle English, but the modern use in this sense (since 1823) seems to be from influence of German <span class="foreign">dumm</span>, especially in Pennsylvania German.</p>
