<p>The die for stamping metal was wedge-shaped, and by late 14c. the English word came to mean &quot;thing stamped, piece of metal converted into money by being impressed with official marks or characters&quot; (a sense that already had developed in Old French). Meaning &quot;coined money collectively, specie&quot; is from late 14c.</p>
<p>Compare <span class="crossreference">quoin</span>, which split off from this word 16c., taking the architectural sense. Modern French <span class="foreign">coin</span> is &quot;corner, angle, nook.&quot;</p>
