Greek <span class="foreign">khaos</span> &quot;empty space&quot; (see <span class="crossreference">chaos</span>). The sound of Dutch &quot;g&quot; is roughly equivalent to that of Greek &quot;kh.&quot; First used by Flemish chemist J.B. van Helmont (1577-1644), probably influenced by Paracelsus, who used <span class="foreign">khaos</span> in an occult sense of &quot;proper elements of spirits&quot; or &quot;ultra-rarified water,&quot; which was van Helmont&#x27;s definition of <span class="foreign">gas</span>.</p>
<blockquote><span class="foreign">Hunc spiritum, incognitum hactenus, novo nomine gas voco</span> (&quot;This vapor, hitherto unknown, I call by a new name, &#x27;gas.&#x27;&quot;) [Helmont, <span class="foreign">Ortus Medicinae</span>]</blockquote>
