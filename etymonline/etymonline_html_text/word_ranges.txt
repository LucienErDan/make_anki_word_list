<blockquote>In Great Britain heights under 2,000 feet are generally called hills; &#x27;mountain&#x27; being confined to the greater elevations of the Lake District, of North Wales, and of the Scottish Highlands; but, in India, ranges of 5,000 and even 10,000 feet are commonly called &#x27;hills,&#x27; in contrast with the Himalaya Mountains, many peaks of which rise beyond 20,000 feet. <blockquote>The term <span class="foreign">mountain</span> is very loosely used. It commonly means any unusual elevation. <blockquote>Despite the differences in defining mountain systems, Penck (1896), Supan (1911) and Obst (1914) agreed that the distinction between hills, mountains, and mountain systems according to areal extent or height is not a suitable classification.
