<p>From 1819 in the figurative sense &quot;impenetrable barrier.&quot; In reference to the Soviet occupation of Eastern Europe, famously coined by Winston Churchill March 5, 1946, in speech at Westminster College, Fulton, Missouri, but it had been used earlier in this context (for example by U.S. bureaucrat Allen W. <p>The literal sense of the image (if it is one) is obscure today, but &quot;the usual range of fanciful theories has been advanced&quot; [Ayto].
