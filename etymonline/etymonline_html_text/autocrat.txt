<blockquote>In the exact sense, a tyrant is an individual who arrogates to himself the royal authority without having a right to it. <p>Originally in Greek the word was not applied to old hereditary sovereignties (<span class="foreign">basileiai</span>) and despotic kings, but it was used of usurpers, even when popular, moderate, and just (such as Cypselus of Corinth), however it soon became a word of reproach in the usual modern sense.
