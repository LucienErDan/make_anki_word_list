Meaning &quot;mental image or picture&quot; is from 1610s (the Greek word for it was <span class="foreign">ennoia</span>, originally &quot;act of thinking&quot;), as is the sense &quot;concept of something to be done; concept of what ought to be, differing from what is observed.&quot; Sense of &quot;result of thinking&quot; first recorded 1640s. <span class="foreign">Idée fixe</span> (1836) is from French, literally &quot;fixed idea.&quot; Through Latin the word passed into Dutch, German, Danish as <span class="foreign">idee</span>, which also is found in English dialects. The philosophical sense has been somewhat further elaborated since 17c. by Descartes, Locke, Berkeley, Hume, Kant. Colloquial <span class="foreign">big idea</span> (as in <span class="foreign">what&#x27;s the ...</span>) is from 1908.
