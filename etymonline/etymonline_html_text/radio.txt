and meaning of radio" href="/word/radio#etymonline_v_3272"><em>radio</em> (n.)</a><section class="word__defination--2q7ZH undefined"><p>&quot;wireless transmission of voice signals with radio waves,&quot; 1907, abstracted from earlier combinations such as <span class="foreign">radio-receiver</span> (1903), <span class="foreign">radiophone</span> (1881), <span class="foreign">radio-telegraphy</span> (1898), from <span class="crossreference">radio-</span> as a combining form of Latin <span class="foreign">radius</span> &quot;beam.&quot; Use for &quot;radio receiver&quot; is first attested 1913; sense of &quot;sound broadcasting as a medium&quot; is from 1913.</p>
<blockquote>That winter, however--the winter of 1921-22--[radio] came with a rush. Soon everybody was talking, not about wireless telephony, but about radio. A San Francisco paper described the discovery that millions were making: &quot;There is radio music in the air, every night, everywhere. Anybody can hear it at home on a receiving set, which any boy can put up in an hour.&quot; In February President Harding had an outfit installed in his study, and the Dixmoor Golf Club announced that it would install a &quot;telephone&quot; to enable golfers to hear church services. [Frederick Lewis Allen, &quot;Only Yesterday: An Informal History of the 1920&#x27;s,&quot; 1931]</blockquote>

<blockquote>It is not a dream, but a probability that the radio will demolish blocs, cut the strings of red tape, actuate the voice &quot;back home,&quot; dismantle politics and entrench the nation&#x27;s executive in a position of power unlike that within the grasp of any executive in the world&#x27;s history. [&quot;The Reading Eagle,&quot; Reading, Pa., U.S.A., March 16, 1924]</blockquote>
<p>As late as July 1921 the &quot;New York Times&quot; was calling it <span class="foreign">wireless telephony</span>, and <span class="crossreference">wireless</span> remained widespread until World War II, when military preference for <span class="foreign">radio</span> established it as the word.</p>
<p>As an adjective by 1912, &quot;by radio transmission;&quot; meaning &quot;controlled by radio&quot; from 1974. <span class="foreign">Radio _______</span> &quot;radio station or service from _______&quot; is recorded from 1920.
