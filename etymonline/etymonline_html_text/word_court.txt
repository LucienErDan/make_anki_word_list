<p>Both senses of the Latin word emerged in English. From the purely physical sense come &quot;palace, residence of a sovereign&quot; (c. 1200), &quot;enclosed space connected with a building or buildings&quot; (early 14c.), and the sporting sense &quot;smooth, level plot of ground on which a ball game is played&quot; (1510s, originally of tennis). Also &quot;short arm of a public street, enclosed on three sides by buildings&quot; (1680s), formerly noted for poverty or as business districts.</p>
