<p>As &quot;pertaining to the government and affairs of the 13 revolutionary British American colonies,&quot; from 1774; the <span class="foreign"><strong>Continental Congress</strong></span> was so called from 1775.</p>
<p><span class="foreign"><strong>Continental divide</strong></span> &quot;line across a continent such that the drainage on one side feeds into one ocean or sea and that on the other feeds into a different body of water,&quot; was in use by 1865; <span class="foreign"><strong>continental slope</strong></span> &quot;slope between the outer edge of the continental shelf and the ocean floor&quot; is from 1849. <span class="foreign"><strong>Continental shelf</strong></span> &quot;area of shallow sea around a continent, geologically part of the continent&quot; is first attested 1888.</p>
