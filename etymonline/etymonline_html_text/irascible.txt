<p>The historical word for what now are called in ethnological works <span class="foreign">Tatars</span>. A Turkic people, their native region was east of the Caspian Sea. Ghengis&#x27; horde was a mix of Tatars, Mongols, Turks, etc. Used figuratively for &quot;savage, rough, irascible person&quot; (1660s). To <span class="foreign">catch a Tartar</span> &quot;get hold of what cannot be controlled&quot; is recorded from 1660s; original sense not preserved, but probably from some military story similar to the old battlefield joke:</p>
<blockquote class="poetry">Irish soldier (shouting from within the brush): I&#x27;ve captured one of the enemy.</blockquote>
<blockquote class="poetry">Captain: Excellent! Bring him here.</blockquote>
