<p>In Pliny the word is the name of a kind of painting executed by fire or heat. <blockquote>It denoted a kind of painting practised by the ancients, in which the crayon was dipped in wax of various colours. <span class="foreign">Encausto pingere</span> is to practise this art, paint in encaustic or enamel. <span class="foreign">Encaustum</span> afterwards came to signify an ink for the purpose of writing; and the &quot;sacred <span class="foreign">encaustum</span>&quot; of Justinian&#x27;s Code was an ink which the Roman Emperors used for imperial subscriptions. (So that in this use of the word, the notion of burning which there is in the etymology, is still retained.)
