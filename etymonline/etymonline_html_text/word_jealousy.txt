<p>The figurative sense of &quot;cultivation through education, systematic improvement and refinement of the mind&quot; is attested by c. 1500; Century Dictionary writes that it was, &quot;Not common before the nineteenth century, except with strong consciousness of the metaphor involved, though used in Latin by Cicero.&quot; Meaning &quot;learning and taste, the intellectual side of civilization&quot; is by 1805; the closely related sense of &quot;collective customs and achievements of a people, a particular form of collective intellectual development&quot; is by 1867.</p>
<blockquote>For without culture or holiness, which are always the gift of a very few, a man may renounce wealth or any other external thing, but he cannot renounce hatred, envy, jealousy, revenge. Culture is the sanctity of the intellect. [William Butler Yeats]</blockquote>
