title="Origin and meaning of dagger" href="/word/dagger#etymonline_v_734"><em>dagger</em> (n.)</a><section class="word__defination--2q7ZH undefined"><p>&quot;edged or pointed weapon for thrusting, (mid-14c. in Anglo-Latin), apparently related to Old French <span class="foreign">dague</span> &quot;dagger,&quot; from Old Provençal or Italian <span class="foreign">daga</span>, which are of uncertain origin; perhaps from Celtic, perhaps from Vulgar Latin <span class="foreign">*daca</span> &quot;Dacian knife,&quot; from the Roman province (see <span class="crossreference">Dacian</span>). <p>Attested earlier (1279) as a surname (<span class="foreign">Dagard</span>, presumably &quot;one who carried a dagger&quot;). Also compare <span class="crossreference">dogwood</span>. Middle Dutch <span class="foreign">dagge</span>, Danish <span class="foreign">daggert</span>, German <span class="foreign">Degen</span> also are from French.
