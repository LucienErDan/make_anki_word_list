<p>Given specific uses in late Middle English (<span class="foreign">small gentleman</span>, <span class="foreign">gentleman-of-arms</span>, <span class="foreign">gentleman-usher</span>, etc.), hence in England the word often meant any man above the social rank of a yeoman, including the nobility, but it was sometimes restricted to those who bear a coat of arms but not a title; in U.S., &quot;man of property, not engaged in business or a profession&quot; (1789).
