<p>The meanings &quot;beside oneself with excitement or enthusiasm, under the influence of uncontrollable emotion&quot; and &quot;enraged, furious, beside oneself with anger&quot; are attested from early 14c., but the latter was deplored by Rev. Of animals, &quot;affected with rabies, furious from disease&quot; from late 13c.</p>
