<p>Many Indo-European languages show signs of once having distinguished the horizontal back of an animal (or a mountain range) from the upright back of a human. <blockquote>If I durst speak to herself, you may be certain I would never dream of trusting it to you; because I know you like the back of my hand, and all your blustering talk is that much wind to me.</blockquote>
<p>The story, a sequel to &quot;Kidnapped,&quot; has a Scottish setting and context, and <span class="foreign">the back of my hand to you</span> was noted in the late 19th century as a Scottish expression meaning &quot;I will have nothing to do with you&quot; [see Longmuir&#x27;s edition of Jamieson&#x27;s Scottish dictionary].
