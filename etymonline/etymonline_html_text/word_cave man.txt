<p>Specific sense of &quot;adult male of the human race&quot; (distinguished from a woman or boy) is by late Old English (c. 1000); Old English used <span class="foreign">wer</span> and <span class="foreign">wif</span> to distinguish the sexes, but <span class="foreign">wer</span> began to disappear late 13c. and was replaced by <span class="foreign">man</span>. <p><span class="foreign">Man</span> also was in Old English as an indefinite pronoun, &quot;one, people, they.&quot; It was used generically for &quot;the human race, mankind&quot; by c. 1200.
