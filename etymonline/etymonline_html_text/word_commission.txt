title="Origin and meaning of commission" href="/word/commission#etymonline_v_17232"><em>commission</em> (n.)<i class="fa fa-image" style="margin-left:5px;color:#888"></i></a><section class="word__defination--2q7ZH undefined"><p>mid-14c., &quot;authority entrusted to someone, delegated authority or power,&quot; from Old French <span class="foreign">commission</span> and directly from Latin <span class="foreign">commissionem</span> (nominative <span class="foreign">commissio</span>) &quot;act of committing,&quot; in Medieval Latin &quot;delegation of business,&quot; noun of action from past participle stem of <span class="foreign">committere</span> &quot;to unite, connect, combine; to bring together,&quot; from<span class="foreign"> com</span> &quot;with, together&quot; (see <span class="crossreference">com-</span>) + <span class="foreign">mittere</span> &quot;to release, let go; send, throw&quot; (see <span class="crossreference">mission</span>).</p>
<p>Meaning &quot;document delegating authority&quot; is from early 15c.; meaning &quot;body of persons charged with authority for the performance of certain special duties&quot; is from late 15c. Sense of &quot;anything entrusted to anyone to perform&quot; is from 1560s; sense of &quot;act of committing or doing&quot; is from 1590s.</p>
<p>Naval sense &quot;period of active service of a warship&quot; is by 1882 (<span class="foreign">in commission</span> &quot;under the command of an officer&quot; is from 1733). Hence <span class="foreign">out of commission</span> &quot;laid up in a navy yard or in reserve&quot; (1878), subsequently extended to other machinery, and, figuratively, to persons or human qualities by 1917.</p>
<p>In commercial use, &quot;authority delegated by another for the purchase and sale of goods,&quot; 1620s. Meaning &quot;allowance made or percentage given to an agent for transacting business&quot; is from 1725.</p></section></object><a data-more="true" class="word_4pc__footer--2yfj3" href="/word/commission#etymonline_v_17232"><span>Relate entries &amp; more</span> <i class="fa fa-chevron-right" style="opacity:0"></i></a></div><div data-ad-container="true" class="adContainer--6CVz1 horizontal"><span class="adLabel--35hfi">Advertisement</span><ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-7093273770345366" style="display:block;width:100%" data-ad-format="fluid" data-ad-slot="1604734010" data-ad-layout-key="-fb+5w+4e-db+86"></ins></div><div class="word--C9UPa word_4pc--2SZw8"><object><a class="word__name--TTbAA word_thumbnail__name--1khEg" title="Origin and meaning of commission" href="/word/commission#etymonline_v_46125"><em>commission</em> (v.)</a><section class="word__defination--2q7ZH undefined"><p>1660s, &quot;empower or authorize by commission,&quot; from <span class="crossreference">commission</span> (n.).
