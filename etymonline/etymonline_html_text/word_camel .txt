<p>Another Old English word for the beast was <span class="foreign">olfend</span>, apparently based on confusion of camels with elephants in a place and time when both were unknown but for travelers&#x27; vague descriptions. Of the two distinct species, the Arabian has one hump (the lighter, thoroughbred variety is the <span class="crossreference">dromedary</span>); the Bactrian has two.
