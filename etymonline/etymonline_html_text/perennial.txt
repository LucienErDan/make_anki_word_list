<p>Used variously to abbreviate other proper names beginning with <span class="foreign">Metropolitan</span>, such as &quot;Metropolitan Museum of Art&quot; (N.Y.), by 1919; &quot;Metropolitan Railway&quot; (stock), by 1890; &quot;Metropolitan Opera Company (N.Y.), by 1922. <p>&quot;[R]eckoned on the continent of Europe and in America as the last month of spring, but in Great Britain as the first of summer&quot; [Century Dictionary, 1897]. Replaced Old English <span class="foreign">þrimilce</span>, month in which cows can be milked three times a day. May marriages have been considered unlucky at least since Ovid&#x27;s day. <span class="foreign">May-apple</span>, perennial herb native to North America, so called for its time of blooming and its
