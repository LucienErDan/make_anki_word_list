<p>It evolved to mean any round plate or face over which a pointer moves to indicate something about the machinery to which it is attached. Sense of &quot;face of a clock (or later a watch), upon which hours and minutes are marked and over which the hands move&quot; is from mid-15c.</p>
<p>Telephone sense &quot;circular plate marked with numbers and letters which can be rotated to establish connection&quot; is from 1879, which led to <span class="foreign">dial tone</span> (1921), &quot;the signal to begin dialing.&quot; <span class="foreign">Dial-plate</span> is attested from 1680s.</p></section></object><a data-more="true" class="word_4pc__footer--2yfj3" href="/word/dial#etymonline_v_8513"><span>Relate entries &amp; more</span> <i class="fa fa-chevron-right" style="opacity:0"></i></a></div><div class="word--C9UPa word_4pc--2SZw8"><object><a class="word__name--TTbAA word_thumbnail__name--1khEg" title="Origin and meaning of moment" href="/word/moment#etymonline_v_17422">moment (n.)</a><section class="word__defination--2q7ZH undefined"><p>late 14c., &quot;very brief portion of time, instant,&quot; in <span class="foreign">moment of time</span>, from Old French <span class="foreign">moment</span> (12c.) &quot;moment, minute; importance, weight, value&quot; and directly from Latin <span class="foreign">momentum</span> &quot;movement, motion; moving power; alteration, change;&quot; also &quot;short time, instant&quot; (also source of Spanish, Italian <span class="foreign">momento</span>), contraction of <span class="foreign">*movimentum</span>, from <span class="foreign">movere</span> &quot;to move&quot; (from PIE root <span class="crossreference">*meue-</span> &quot;to push away&quot;).</p>

<p>Some (but not OED) explain the sense evolution of the Latin word by notion of a particle so small it would just &quot;move&quot; the pointer of a scale, which led to the transferred sense of &quot;minute time division.&quot;</p>

<p>In careful use, a <span class="foreign">moment</span> has duration, an <span class="foreign">instant</span> does not. Meaning &quot;opportunity&quot; (as in <span class="foreign">seize the moment</span>) is from 1781.</p>
