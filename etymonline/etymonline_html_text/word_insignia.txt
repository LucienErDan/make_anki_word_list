A mystery word, with no known cognates beyond English. Perhaps akin to French <span class="foreign">brasser</span> &quot;to brew,&quot; because it is an alloy. <p>Words for &quot;brass&quot; in other languages (such as German <span class="foreign">Messing</span>, Old English <span class="foreign">mæsling</span>, French <span class="foreign">laiton</span>, Italian <span class="foreign">ottone</span>) also tend to be difficult to explain. As brass was unknown in early antiquity (it was well-known to Strabo, 1c., but not mentioned by Homer), the use of the English word in Bible translations, etc., likely means &quot;bronze.&quot; The Romans were the first to deliberately make it.</p>
