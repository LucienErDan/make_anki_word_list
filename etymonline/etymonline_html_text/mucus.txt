<p>The modern spelling is from mid-15c. The meaning &quot;piece of cord or tow soaked in sulfur, used for lighting fires, lamps, candles, etc.&quot; is from 1530. It was first used 1831 for the modern type of sulfur-tipped wooden friction match, which were perfected about that time, and competed with <span class="crossreference">lucifer</span> for much of 19c. as the name for this invention. An earlier version consisted of a thin strip of wood tipped with combustible matter that required contact with phosphorous carried separately in a box or vial.</p>
