title="Origin and meaning of burlesque" href="/word/burlesque#etymonline_v_18111"><em>burlesque</em> (n.)</a><section class="word__defination--2q7ZH undefined"><p>1660s, &quot;piece composed in burlesque style, derisive imitation, grotesque parody,&quot; earlier as an adjective, &quot;odd, grotesque&quot; (1650s), from French <span class="foreign">burlesque</span> (16c.), from Italian <span class="foreign">burlesco</span> &quot;ludicrous,&quot; from <span class="foreign">burla</span> &quot;joke, fun, mockery,&quot; possibly ultimately from Late Latin <span class="foreign">burra</span> &quot;trifle, nonsense,&quot; literally &quot;flock of wool&quot; (a word of unknown origin). The more precise adjectival meaning &quot;tending to excite laughter by ludicrous contrast between the subject and the manner of treating it&quot; is attested in English by 1700.</p>
<blockquote>The two great branches of ridicule in writing are comedy and burlesque. The first ridicules persons by drawing them in their proper characters; the other, by drawing them quite unlike themselves. Burlesque is therefore of two kinds; the first represents mean persons in accoutrements of heroes, the other describes great persons acting and speaking like the basest among the people. [Addison, &quot;Spectator,&quot; Dec. 15, 1711]</blockquote>
<p>By 1880s it typically meant &quot;travesties on the classics and satires on accepted ideas&quot; and vulgar comic opera. Modern sense of &quot;variety show featuring striptease&quot; is American English, evolved after 1870 and predominant from 1920s, probably from the earlier sense &quot;sketches at the end of minstrel shows&quot; (1857).</p>

<p><span class="foreign"><strong>Banana-skin</strong></span> is from 1851, <span class="foreign"><strong>banana-peel</strong></span> from 1874, both originally with reference to them being left carelessly on the ground and liable to cause a pratfall when trodden upon. <blockquote>The companies that have paid damages for fraudulent claims are the Manhattan Elevated Company, New York; West End Street Railway, Boston; Chicago City Railway Company, Chicago; Illinois Central Railroad Company, Chicago.
