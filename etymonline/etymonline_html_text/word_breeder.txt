<p>Greek had words for &quot;good weather&quot; (<span class="foreign">aithria</span>, <span class="foreign">eudia</span>) and words for &quot;storm&quot; and &quot;winter,&quot; but no generic word for &quot;weather&quot; until <span class="foreign">kairos</span> (literally &quot;time&quot;) began to be used as such in Byzantine times.
