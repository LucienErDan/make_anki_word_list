<p>Always used in English for the main meal of the day, but the time of that has gradually shifted later.</p>
<blockquote>In medieval and modern Europe the common practice, down to the middle of the eighteenth century, was to take this meal about midday, or in more primitive times even as early as 9 or 10 A.M. <p>The change from midday to evening began with the fashionable classes. Compare <span class="crossreference">dinette</span>.</p>
