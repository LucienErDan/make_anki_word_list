<p>Weekley calls it &quot;A very puzzling word.&quot; Sense of &quot;a rush of water&quot; in a stream (1520s), is probably from <span class="crossreference">flush</span> (v.1). From this likely come the extended senses &quot;rush of emotion or passion&quot; (1610s); &quot;a sudden shooting up&quot; (1773); &quot;act of cleansing (a drain) by flushing&quot; (1883); &quot;glow of light or color&quot; (especially sudden redness in the face), 1620s. Independently from the verb, probably, is the noun sense of &quot;a flight of birds suddenly started up&quot; (1590s).</p>
