title="Origin and meaning of season" href="/word/season#etymonline_v_23062"><em>season</em> (n.)</a><section class="word__defination--2q7ZH undefined"><p>c. 1300, &quot;a period of the year,&quot; <p>Sense shifted in Vulgar Latin from &quot;act of sowing&quot; to &quot;time of sowing,&quot; especially &quot;spring, regarded as the chief sowing season.&quot; In Old Provençal and Old French (and thus in English), this was extended to &quot;season&quot; in general. In other Indo-European languages, generic &quot;season&quot; (of the year) words typically are from words for &quot;time,&quot; sometimes with a word for &quot;year&quot; (as in Latin <span class="foreign">tempus (anni)</span>, German <span class="foreign">Jahreszeit</span>). Of game (as in <span class="foreign">out of season</span>) from late 14c. Spanish <span class="foreign">estacion</span>, Italian <span class="foreign">stagione</span> are unrelated, being from Latin <span class="foreign">statio</span> &quot;station.&quot;</p>
