<p> Generic words for &quot;chicken&quot; in Indo-European tend to be extended uses of &quot;hen&quot; words, as hens are more numerous than cocks among domestic fowl, but occasionally they are from words for the young, as in English and in Latin (<span class="foreign">pullus</span>). (compare <span class="foreign">hen-herte</span> &quot;a chicken-hearted person,&quot; mid-15c.). As the name of a game of danger to test courage, it is first recorded 1953.</p>
