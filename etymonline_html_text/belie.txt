<p>Meaning &quot;conviction of the truth of a proposition or alleged fact without knowledge&quot; is by 1530s; it is also &quot;sometimes used to include the absolute conviction or certainty which accompanies knowledge&quot; [Century Dictionary]. Related: <span class="foreign">Beliefs</span>.</p>
<p><span class="foreign">Belief</span> meant &quot;trust in God,&quot; while <span class="crossreference">faith</span> meant &quot;loyalty to a person based on promise or duty&quot; (a sense preserved in <span class="foreign">keep one&#x27;s faith</span>, <span class="foreign">in good (or bad) faith</span>, and in common usage of <span class="foreign">faithful</span>, <span class="foreign">faithless</span>, which contain no notion of divinity).
