<p>Original sense of &quot;greatest&quot; survives in phrase <span class="foreign"><strong>for the most part</strong></span> (mid-14c.; late Old English had <span class="foreign">þa mæste dæl</span>). Also used as an adverb in Old English and in late Old English as a noun, &quot;the greatest or greater number.&quot; The sense of &quot;greatest value or advantage&quot; in the phrase <span class="foreign"><strong>make the most of</strong></span> (something) is by 1520s. <p>Double superlative <span class="foreign"><strong>mostest</strong></span> &quot;greatest amount or degree&quot; is by 1849 in U.S. Southern and African-American vernacular.
