title="Origin and meaning of bracket" href="/word/bracket#etymonline_v_15707"><em>bracket</em> (n.)</a><section class="word__defination--2q7ZH undefined"><p>1570s, <span class="foreign">bragget</span>, &quot;architectural support, supporting piece projecting from a vertical surface,&quot; probably from Middle French <span class="foreign">braguette</span>, originally &quot;codpiece armor&quot; (16c.), from a fancied resemblance of architectural supports to that article of attire (Spanish cognate <span class="foreign">bragueta</span> meant both &quot;codpiece&quot; <p>The architectural meaning also might be directly from the &quot;breeches&quot; sense, on the notion of two limbs or of appliances used in pairs.
