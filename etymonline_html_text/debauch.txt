title="Origin and meaning of debauch" href="/word/debauch#etymonline_v_823"><em>debauch</em> (v.)</a><section class="word__defination--2q7ZH undefined"><p>1590s, &quot;to entice, seduce, lead astray&quot; (from allegiance, family, etc.), from Middle French <span class="foreign">débaucher</span> &quot;entice from work or duty,&quot; from Old French <span class="foreign">desbaucher</span> &quot;to lead astray,&quot; a word of uncertain origin.</p>
<p>Supposedly it is literally &quot;to trim (wood) to make a beam&quot; (from <span class="foreign">bauch</span> &quot;beam,&quot; from Frankish <span class="foreign">balk</span> or some other Germanic source akin to English <span class="crossreference">balk</span> (n.)). The notion of &quot;shaving&quot; something away, perhaps, but the root is also said to be a word meaning &quot;workshop,&quot; which gets toward the notion of &quot;to lure someone off the job;&quot; either way the sense evolution is unclear.</p>
<p>The more specific meaning &quot;seduce from virtue or morality, corrupt the morals or principles of&quot; is from c. 1600, especially &quot;to corrupt with lewdness, seduce sexually,&quot; usually in reference to women. Intransitive sense &quot;indulge in excess in sensual enjoyment&quot; is from 1640s. As a noun, &quot;a bout of excessive sensual pleasure,&quot; c. 1600.</p></section></object><a data-more="true" class="word_4pc__footer--2yfj3" href="/word/debauch#etymonline_v_823"><span>Relate entries &amp; more</span> <i class="fa fa-chevron-right" style="opacity:0"></i></a></div><div data-ad-container="true" class="adContainer--6CVz1 horizontal"><span class="adLabel--35hfi">Advertisement</span><ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-7093273770345366" style="display:block;width:100%" data-ad-format="fluid" data-ad-slot="1604734010" data-ad-layout-key="-fb+5w+4e-db+86"></ins></div><div class="word--C9UPa word_4pc--2SZw8"><object><a class="word__name--TTbAA word_thumbnail__name--1khEg" title="Origin and meaning of debauched" href="/word/debauched#etymonline_v_53804">debauched (adj.)</a><section class="word__defination--2q7ZH undefined"><p>&quot;seduced or corrupted from duty or virtue, vitiated in morals or purity of character,&quot; 1590s, past-participle adjective from <span class="crossreference">debauch</span> (v.).
