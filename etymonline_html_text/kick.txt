<p>Transitive sense &quot;give a blow with the foot&quot; is from 1580s. Meaning &quot;to strike in recoiling&quot; (as a gun, etc.) is from 1832. Figurative sense of &quot;complain, protest, manifest strong objection, rebel against&quot; (late 14c.) probably is at least in part from the Bible verse. Slang sense of &quot;die&quot; is attested from 1725 (<span class="foreign">kick the wind</span> was slang for &quot;be hanged,&quot; 1590s; see also <span class="crossreference">bucket</span>). Meaning &quot;to end one&#x27;s drug habit&quot; is from 1936.</p>
