Old English words for &quot;coast, shore&quot; were <span class="crossreference">strand</span> (n.), <span class="foreign">waroþ</span>, <span class="foreign">ofer</span>. Few Indo-European languages have such a single comprehensive word for &quot;land bordering water&quot; (Homer uses one word for sandy beaches, another for rocky headlands).
