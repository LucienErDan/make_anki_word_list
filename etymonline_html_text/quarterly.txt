William Dwight Whitney (&quot;The Life and Growth of Language,&quot; 1875) credits its widespread use to Franz Bopp. </p>
<p>The alternative <span class="foreign">Indo-Germanic</span> (1835) was coined in German in 1823 (<span class="foreign">indogermanisch</span>), based on the two peoples then thought to be at the extremes of the geographic area covered by the languages, but this was before Celtic was realized also to be an Indo-European language. After this was proved, many German scholars switched to <span class="foreign">Indo-European</span> as more accurate, but <span class="foreign">Indo-Germanic</span> continued in use (popularized by the titles of major works) and the predominance of German scholarship in this field made it the popular term in England, too, through the 19c. See also <span class="crossreference">Aryan</span> and <span class="crossreference">Japhetic</span>.</p>
<p><span class="foreign">Indo-Aryan</span> (1850) seems to have been used only of the Aryans of India. <span class="foreign">Indo-European</span> also was used in reference to trade between Europe and India or European colonial enterprises in India (1813).</p></section></object><a data-more="true" class="word_4pc__footer--2yfj3" href="/word/Indo-European#etymonline_v_6397"><span>Relate entries &amp; more</span> <i class="fa fa-chevron-right" style="opacity:0"></i></a></div><div class="word--C9UPa word_4pc--2SZw8"><object><a class="word__name--TTbAA word_thumbnail__name--1khEg" title="Origin and meaning of conservative" href="/word/conservative#etymonline_v_18236">conservative (adj.)</a><section class="word__defination--2q7ZH undefined"><p>late 14c., <span class="foreign">conservatyf</span>, &quot;tending to preserve or protect, preservative, having the power to keep whole or safe,&quot; from Middle French <span class="foreign">conservatif</span>, from Medieval Latin <span class="foreign">conservativus</span>, from Latin <span class="foreign">conservatus</span>, past participle of <span class="foreign">conservare</span> &quot;to keep, preserve, keep intact, guard,&quot; from assimilated form of <span class="foreign">com-</span>, here probably an intensive prefix (see <span class="crossreference">com-</span>), + <span class="foreign">servare</span> &quot;keep watch, maintain&quot; (from PIE root <span class="crossreference">*ser-</span> (1) &quot;to protect&quot;).</p>
<p>From 1840 in the general sense &quot;disposed to retain and maintain what is established, opposed to innovation and change,&quot; or, in a negative sense &quot;opposed to progress.&quot;</p>
<p>As a modern political tradition, &quot;antagonistic to change in the institutions of a country,&quot; often especially &quot;opposed to changes toward pure democracy,&quot; <span class="foreign">conservatism</span> traces to Edmund Burke&#x27;s opposition to the French Revolution (1790), but the word <span class="foreign">conservative</span> is not found in his writing. It was coined by his French disciples (such as Chateaubriand, who titled his journal defending clerical and political restoration <span class="foreign">&quot;Le Conservateur&quot;</span>).</p>
<p><span class="foreign">Conservative</span> as the name of a British political faction first appeared in an 1830 issue of the &quot;Quarterly Review,&quot; in an unsigned article sometimes attributed to John Wilson Croker. It replaced <span class="crossreference">Tory</span> (q.v.) by 1843, reflecting both a change from the pejorative name (in use for 150 years) and repudiation of some reactionary policies.</p>
<blockquote>Strictly speaking, conservatism is not a political system, but rather a way of looking at the civil order. The conservative of Peru ... will differ greatly from those of Australia, for though they may share a preference for things established, the institutions and customs which they desire to preserve are not identical. [Russell Kirk (1918-1994)]</blockquote>
<p>Phrases such as <span class="foreign">conservative estimate</span> (1874), in which it means &quot;characterized by caution, deliberately low,&quot; make no sense etymologically. Related: <span class="foreign">Conservatively; conservativeness.</span></p></section></object><a data-more="true" class="word_4pc__footer--2yfj3"
