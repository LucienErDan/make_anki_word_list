<p>The old sagas tell of the <span class="foreign">troll-bull</span>, a supernatural being in the form of a bull, as well as <span class="foreign">boar-trolls</span>. The first record of the word in modern English is from a court document from the Shetlands, regarding a certain Catherine, who, among other things, was accused of &quot;airt and pairt of witchcraft and sorcerie, in hanting and seeing the Trollis ryse out of the kyrk yeard of Hildiswick.&quot;</p>
<p>Originally conceived as a race of malevolent giants, they have suffered the same fate as the Celtic <span class="foreign">Danann</span> and by 19c. were regarded by peasants in in Denmark and Sweden as dwarfs and imps supposed to live in caves or under the ground.</p>

Barnhart says Old English <span class="foreign">buc</span> &quot;male deer,&quot; listed in some sources, is a &quot;ghost word or scribal error.&quot; The Germanic word (in the sense &quot;he-goat&quot;) was borrowed in French as <span class="foreign">bouc</span>.</p>
<p>Meaning &quot;a man&quot; is from c. 1300 (Old Norse <span class="foreign">bokki</span> also was used in this sense). Especially &quot;fashionable man&quot; (1725); also used of a male Native American (c. 1800) or Negro (1835). This also is perhaps the sense in army slang <span class="foreign">buck private</span> &quot;private of the lowest class&quot; (1870s).</p>
<p>The phrase <span class="foreign">pass the buck</span> is recorded in the literal sense 1865, American English poker slang; the buck in question being originally perhaps a buckhorn-handled knife:</p>
<blockquote>The &#x27;buck&#x27; is any inanimate object, usually [a] knife or pencil, which is thrown into a jack pot and temporarily taken by the winner of the pot. Whenever the deal reaches the holder of the &#x27;buck&#x27;, a new jack pot must be made. [J.W. Keller, &quot;Draw Poker,&quot; 1887]</blockquote>
<p>The figurative sense of &quot;shift responsibility&quot; is first recorded 1912; the phrase <span class="foreign">the buck stops here</span> (1952) is associated with U.S. President Harry Truman.</p></section></object><a data-more="true" class="word_4pc__footer--2yfj3" href="/word/buck#etymonline_v_17199"><span>Relate entries &amp; more</span> <i class="fa fa-chevron-right"
