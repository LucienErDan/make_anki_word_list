title="Origin and meaning of jungle" href="/word/jungle#etymonline_v_6557"><em>jungle</em> (n.)</a><section class="word__defination--2q7ZH undefined">1776, &quot;dense growth of trees and other tangled vegetation,&quot; such as that of the swampy regions at the base of the Himalayas in India, from Hindi <span class="foreign">jangal</span> &quot;desert, forest, wasteland, uncultivated ground,&quot; from Sanskrit <span class="foreign">jangala-s</span> &quot;arid, sparsely grown with trees,&quot; a word of unknown origin.
<br/><br/>
Extended by 1849 to other places overgrown by vegetation in a wild, tangled mass. Figurative sense &quot;wild, tangled mass&quot; of anything is from 1850. Meaning &quot;place notoriously lawless and violent&quot; is first recorded 1906, from Upton Sinclair&#x27;s novel. Meaning &quot;hobo camp&quot; is from 1908. <span class="foreign">Asphalt jungle</span> (1949) is from William R. Burnett&#x27;s novel title, made into a film 1950 by John Huston; <span class="foreign">blackboard jungle</span> (1954) is from Evan Hunter&#x27;s novel title and 1955 movie.
