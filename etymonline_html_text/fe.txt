<p>The other word is Anglo-French <span class="foreign">fee</span>, from Old French <span class="foreign">fieu</span>, a variant of <span class="foreign">fief</span> &quot;possession, holding, domain; feudal duties, payment&quot; (see <span class="crossreference">fief</span>), which apparently is a Germanic compound in which the first element is cognate with Old English <span class="foreign">feoh</span>.</p>
<p>Via Anglo-French come the legal senses &quot;estate in land or tenements held on condition of feudal homage; land, property, possession&quot; (c. 1300).
