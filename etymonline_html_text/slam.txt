<p>Specific modern sense of &quot;woman who enjoys sex in a degree considered shamefully excessive&quot; is by 1966. Meaning &quot;woman of loose character, bold hussy&quot; is attested from mid-15c., but the primary association through 18c. was untidiness. Johnson has it (second definition) as &quot;A word of slight contempt to a woman&quot; but sexual activity does not seem to figure into his examples. Playful use of the word, without implication of messiness or loose morals, is attested by 1660s:</p>
<blockquote>My wife called up the people to washing by four o&#x27;clock in the morning; and our little girl Susan is a most admirable slut, and pleases us mightily, doing more service than both the others, and deserves wages better. [Pepys, diary, Feb. 21, 1664]</blockquote>
