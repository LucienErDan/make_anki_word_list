<br/><br/>
The women&#x27;s order, though sometimes persecuted, generally preserved its good reputation, but it quickly drew imposters who did not; nonetheless it eventually was condemned as heretical. A male order, called <span class="foreign">Beghards</span> founded communities by the 1220s in imitation of them, but they soon degenerated (compare Old French <span class="foreign">beguin</span> &quot;(male) Beguin,&quot; also &quot;hypocrite&quot;) and wandered begging in the guise of religion; they likely were the source of the words <span class="crossreference">beg</span> and <span class="crossreference">beggar</span>, though there is disagreement over whether <span class="foreign">Beghard</span> produced Middle Dutch <span class="foreign">beggaert</span> &quot;mendicant&quot; or was produced by it. The male order was condemned by the Church early 14c. and vanished by mid-16c.
