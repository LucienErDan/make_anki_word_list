<p>Of inanimate things, &quot;cessation, end,&quot; late 14c. From late 12c. as &quot;death personified, a skeleton as the figure of mortality.&quot; As &quot;a plague, a great mortality,&quot; late 14c. To <span class="foreign"><strong>be the death of</strong></span> &quot;be the cause or occasion of death&quot; is in Shakespeare (1596). Expression <span class="foreign"><strong>a</strong></span><strong> </strong><span class="foreign"><strong>fate worse than death</strong></span> is from 1810 though the idea is ancient.</p>
