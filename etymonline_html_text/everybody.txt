<blockquote>The Main Line, Philadelphia&#x27;s most famous suburban district, was deliberately conceived in the 1870&#x27;s and 1880&#x27;s by the [Pennsylvania] Railroad, which built high-toned housing developments, ran hotels, more or less forced its executives to plunk their estates out there, and created a whole series of somewhat spurious Welsh towns along the railroad tracks. ... Now everybody assumes these all date from 1682, like the Robertses; but as Chestnut Hill people like to say, &quot;nobody but Welsh peasants lived on the Main Line till the Railroad built it up.&quot; [Nathaniel Burt, &quot;The Perennial Philadelphians,&quot; 1963]</blockquote>
<p>The original station stops were, in order out from the city, Overbrook, Merion, Narberth, Wynnewood, Ardmore, Haverford, Bryn Mawr, Paoli. <blockquote>It is well known to everybody who knows anything of its history, that this court [Court of Claims] was made a sort of retreat for lame duck politicians that got wounded and had to retreat before the face of popular condemnation.
