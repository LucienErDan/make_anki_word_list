The West Indian island of <span class="foreign">Tobago</span> was said to have been named by Columbus in 1498 from Haitian <span class="foreign">tambaku</span> &quot;pipe,&quot; in reference to the native custom of smoking dried tobacco leaves [Room].</p>
<p>Cultivation in France began 1556 with an importation of seed by Andre Thevet; introduced in Spain 1558 by Francisco Fernandes. <span class="foreign">Tobacco Road</span> as a mythical place representative of rural Southern U.S. poverty is from the title of Erskine Caldwell&#x27;s 1932 novel. Early German and Portuguese accounts of Brazil also record another name for tobacco, <span class="foreign">bittin</span> or <span class="foreign">betum</span>, evidently a native word in South America, which made its way into 17c.
