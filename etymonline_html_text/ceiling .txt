Alphabetic writing came to Rome via the southern Etruscan &quot;Caeretan&quot; script, in which <span class="foreign">gamma</span> was written as a crescent. <p>In some Old English words, before some vowels and in certain positions, <span class="foreign">-c-</span> had a &quot;ts&quot; sound that was respelled <span class="foreign">ch-</span> in Middle English by French scribes (<span class="crossreference">chest</span>, <span class="crossreference">cheese</span>, <span class="crossreference">church</span>; see <span class="crossreference">ch</span>).
