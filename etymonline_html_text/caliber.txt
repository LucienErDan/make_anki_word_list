title="Origin and meaning of caliber" href="/word/caliber#etymonline_v_599"><em>caliber</em> (n.)</a><section class="word__defination--2q7ZH undefined">&quot;inside diameter of a gun barrel,&quot; 1580s, from Middle French <span class="foreign">calibre</span> (by mid-16c., perhaps late 15c.), often said to be ultimately from Arabic <span class="foreign">qalib</span> &quot;a mold for <br/><br/>

It is far more likely that the word was formed in French&quot; from Medieval Latin <span class="foreign">qua libra</span> &quot;of what weight&quot; (a theory first published 19c. by Mahn), from fem. ablative of <span class="foreign">quis</span> (from PIE root <span class="crossreference">*kwo-</span>, stem of relative and interrogative pronouns) + ablative of <span class="foreign">libra</span> &quot;balance&quot; (see <span class="crossreference">Libra</span>).

The earliest sense in English is a figurative one, &quot;degree of merit or importance&quot; (1560s), from French.
