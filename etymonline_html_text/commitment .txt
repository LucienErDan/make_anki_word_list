<p>The evolution of the modern range of meanings in English is not entirely clear. Sense of &quot;to perpetrate (a crime), do, perform (especially something reprehensible)&quot; was ancient in Latin; in English it is attested from mid-15c. Meaning &quot;consign (someone) to custody (of prison, a mental institution, etc.) by official warrant&quot; is from early 15c.</p>

The meaning &quot;a flat strip&quot; (late 14c.) is from French. In Middle English, this was sometimes distinguished by the spelling <span class="foreign">bande</span>, <span class="foreign">bonde</span>, but with loss of terminal <span class="foreign">-e</span> the words have fully merged via the notion of &quot;flat strip of flexible material used to wind around something.&quot;
<br/><br/>
