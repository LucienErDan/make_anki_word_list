A Middle English name for the wild carrot was <span class="foreign">dauke</span> (late 14c.), from Latin.</p>
<p>The plant originally was white-rooted and was a medicinal plant to the ancients, who used it as an aphrodisiac and to prevent poisoning. Not entirely distinguished from parsnips in ancient times. A purple-rooted variety existed perhaps as early as 7c. in Afghanistan and was introduced in Europe by Arabs c. 1100. It was cultivated into the modern orange root 16c.-17c. in the Netherlands. Thus the word&#x27;s use as a color name is not recorded before 1670s in English, originally of yellowish-red hair.</p>
