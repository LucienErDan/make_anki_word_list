Modern chemistry sense is from 1790. Meaning &quot;experienced sailor&quot; is first attested 1840, in reference to the salinity of the sea. Salt was long regarded as having power to repel spiritual and magical evil. Many metaphoric uses reflect that this was once a rare and important resource, such as <span class="foreign">worth one&#x27;s salt</span> (1830), <span class="foreign">salt of the earth</span> (Old English, after Matthew v.13). Belief that spilling salt brings bad luck is attested from 16c. To be <span class="foreign">above</span> (or <span class="foreign">below</span>) <span class="foreign">the salt</span> (1590s) refers to customs of seating at a long table according to rank or honor, and placing a large salt-cellar in the middle of the dining table.
