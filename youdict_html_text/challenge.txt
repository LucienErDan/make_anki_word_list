                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/challenge">challenge</a> 挑战</span><p>来自PIE*kel, 欺骗，挑刺，词源同calumny, cavil.词义褒义化，由贬义的挑刺到褒义的挑战。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/challenge">challenge</a></dt><dd><b>challenge: </b><span>[13] The original notion contained in <i>challenge </i>in English was of ‘accusation’. The word comes, via Old French <i>chalenge </i>or <i>calenge</i>, from Latin <i>calumnia </i>‘false charge, deception’ (source of English <i>calumny </i>[15]). By the early 14th century, the modern, more neutral sense of ‘inviting to a contest’ had emerged, however, and before the end of the 17th century the word’s accusatory connotations had virtually died out.<br><br>Latin <i>calumnia </i>probably came from the verb <i>calvī </i>‘deceive’. This may, via an unrecorded intermediary *<i>calvilla</i>, be related to Latin <i>cavilla </i>‘raillery’, whose derived verb <i>cavillārī </i>was the source of English <i>cavil </i>[16].</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/calumny" target="_blank">calumny</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/challenge">challenge (n.)</a></dt><dd>early 14c., "something one can be accused of, a fault, blemish;" mid-14c., "false accusation, malicious charge; accusation of wrong-doing," also "act of laying claim" (to something), from Anglo-French <span class="foreign">chalenge</span>, Old French <span class="foreign">chalonge</span> "calumny, slander; demand, opposition," in legal use, "accusation, claim, dispute," from Anglo-French <span class="foreign">chalengier</span>, Old French <span class="foreign">chalongier</span> "to accuse, to dispute" (see <a href="/etym/s/challenge" target="_blank">challenge</a> (v.)). Accusatory connotations died out 17c. Meanings "an objection" in law, etc.; "a calling to fight" are from mid-15c. Meaning "difficult task" is from 1954.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/challenge">challenge (v.)</a></dt><dd>c. 1200, "to rebuke," from Old French <span class="foreign">chalongier</span> "complain, protest; haggle, quibble," from Vulgar Latin <span class="foreign">calumniare</span> "to accuse falsely," from Latin <span class="foreign">calumniari</span> "to accuse falsely, misrepresent, slander," from <span class="foreign">calumnia</span> "trickery" (see <a href="/etym/s/calumny" target="_blank">calumny</a>).&#13; <br /><br />&#13; From late 13c. as "to object to, take exception to;" c. 1300 as "to accuse," especially "to accuse falsely," also "to call to account;" late 14c. as "to call to fight." Also used in Middle English with sense "claim, take to oneself." Related: <span class="foreign">Challenged</span>; <span class="foreign">challenging</span>.</dd></dl></div></div>

            <div class="row" id="yd-liju">

Online gaming allows players from around the world to <b>challenge</b> each other. </dt> <dd>网络游戏使得全球的玩家可以相互较量。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>3. This <b>challenge</b> will occupy Europe for a generation or more. </dt> <dd>这将是欧洲在未来二三十年或者更长的时间里所要面临的挑战。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>4. The new Germany must rise to the <b>challenge</b> of its enhanced responsibilities. </dt> <dd>一个崭新的德国必须迎接挑战，承担更多的责任。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>5. He said he's game for a similar <b>challenge</b> next year. </dt> <dd>他说他明年还愿意接受类似的挑战。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl>                 <dl><p>[ <a href="/liju/w/challenge">challenge 造句</a> ]</p></dl>
