                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/roll">roll</a> 卷，卷轴，翻滚，打滚</span><p>来自古法语 rolle,纸卷，文件，来自拉丁语 rotulus,纸卷，来自 rota,轮子，词源同 rotary,round. 引申诸相关词义。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/roll">roll</a></dt><dd><b>roll: </b><span>English has two words <i>roll</i>, both of which go back ultimately to Latin <i>rotulus </i>‘small wheel’, a diminutive form of <i>rota </i>‘wheel’ (source of English <i>rotate</i>, <i>rotund</i>, <i>round</i>, etc). This passed via Old French <i>rolle </i>into English as <i>roll </i>‘rolledup parchment’ [13]. The modern French version of the word has given English <i>role </i>[17], whose underlying notion is of a ‘rolled-up’ piece of paper with the actor’s lines written on it. From <i>rotulus </i>was derived the Vulgar Latin verb *<i>rotulāre</i>, which has given English its verb <i>roll </i>[14]. <i>Control </i>comes from the same source.</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/control" target="_blank">control</a>, <a href="/etym/s/rota" target="_blank">rota</a>, <a href="/etym/s/rotate" target="_blank">rotate</a>, <a href="/etym/s/round" target="_blank">round</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/roll">roll (n.)</a></dt><dd>early 13c., "rolled-up piece of parchment or paper" (especially one inscribed with an official record), from Old French <span class="foreign">rolle</span> "document, parchment scroll, decree" (12c.), from Medieval Latin <span class="foreign">rotulus</span> "a roll of paper" (source also of Spanish <span class="foreign">rollo</span>, Italian <span class="foreign">rullo</span>), from Latin <span class="foreign">rotula</span> "small wheel," diminutive of <span class="foreign">rota</span> "wheel" (see <a href="/etym/s/rotary" target="_blank">rotary</a>).&#13; <br /><br />&#13; Meaning "a register, list, catalogue" is from late 14c., common from c. 1800. Meaning "dough which is rolled before baking" is first recorded mid-15c. Sense of "act of rolling" is from 1743. Meaning "quantity of material rolled up" is from late 14c.; meaning "quantity of paper money" is from 1846; sense of "quantity of (rolled) film" is from 1890. Meaning "act of sexual intercourse" is attested from 1942 (<span class="foreign">roll in the hay</span>), from <a href="/etym/s/roll" target="_blank">roll</a> (v.). Dutch <span class="foreign">rol</span>, German <span class="foreign">Rolle</span>, Danish <span class="foreign">rulle</span>, etc. are from French.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/roll">roll (v.)</a></dt><dd>c. 1300 "turn over and over, move by rotating" (intransitive); late 14c. as "to move (something) by turning it over and over;" from Old French <span class="foreign">roeller</span> "roll, wheel round" (Modern French <span class="foreign">rouler</span>), from Medieval Latin <span class="foreign">rotulare</span>, from Latin <span class="foreign">rotula</span>, diminutive of <span class="foreign">rota</span> "wheel" (see <a href="/etym/s/rotary" target="_blank">rotary</a>). Related: <span class="foreign">Rolled</span>; <a href="/etym/s/rolling" target="_blank">rolling</a>.&#13; <br /><br />&#13; Of sounds (such as thunder) somehow suggestive of a rolling ball, 1590s; of a drum from 1680s. Of eyes, from late 14c. Of a movie camera, "to start filming," from 1938. Sense of "rob a stuporous drunk" is from 1873, from the action required to get to his pockets. To <span class="foreign">roll up</span> "gather, congregate" is from 1861, originally Australian. To <span class="foreign">be on a roll</span> is from 1976. To <span class="foreign">roll with the punches</span> is a metaphor from boxing (1940). <span class="foreign">Heads will roll</span> is a Hitlerism:<blockquote>If our movement is victorious there will be a revolutionary tribunal which will punish the crimes of November 1918. Then decapitated heads will roll in the sand. [1930]</blockquote></dd></dl></div></div>
