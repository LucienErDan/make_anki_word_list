                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/this">this</a> 这，这个</span><p>来自古英语 this,这个，来自 North Sea Germanic*tha-si,这个，来自 PIE*to,指示代词，词源同 that,they.-s,在 North Sea Germanic 表限定性后缀。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/this">this</a></dt><dd><b>this: </b><span>[OE] <i>This </i>is descended from the neuter form of the Old English demonstrative adjective and pronoun whose masculine form was <i>thes</i>. It was formed in the prehistoric Germanic period from the demonstrative base *<i>tha</i>- (source also of English <i>that</i>, <i>there</i>, etc) and a suffix *-<i>se </i>which had the function of indicating a specific thing.</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/the" target="_blank">the</a>, <a href="/etym/s/those" target="_blank">those</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/this">this (pron.)</a></dt><dd>Old English <span class="foreign">þis</span>, neuter demonstrative pronoun and adjective (masc. <span class="foreign">þes</span>, fem. <span class="foreign">þeos</span>), probably from a North Sea Germanic pronoun <span class="foreign">*tha-si-</span>, formed by combining the base <span class="foreign">*þa-</span> (see <a href="/etym/s/that" target="_blank">that</a>) with <span class="foreign">-s</span>, which is probably identical with Old English <span class="foreign">se</span> "the" (representing here "a specific thing"), or with Old English <span class="foreign">seo</span>, imperative of <a href="/etym/s/see" target="_blank">see</a> (v.) "to behold." Compare Old Saxon <span class="foreign">these</span>, Old Frisian <span class="foreign">this</span>, Old Norse <span class="foreign">þessi</span>, Middle Dutch <span class="foreign">dese</span>, Dutch <span class="foreign">deze</span>, Old High German <span class="foreign">deser</span>, German <span class="foreign">dieser</span>.&#13; <br /><br />&#13; Once fully inflected, with 10 distinct forms (see table below); the oblique cases and other genders gradually fell away by 15c. The Old English plural was <span class="foreign">þæs</span> (nominative and accusative), which in Northern Middle English became <span class="foreign">thas</span>, and in Midlands and Southern England became <span class="foreign">thos</span>. The Southern form began to be used late 13c. as the plural of <span class="foreign">that</span> (replacing Middle English <span class="foreign">tho</span>, from Old English <span class="foreign">þa</span>) and acquired an <span class="foreign">-e</span> (apparently from the influence of Middle English adjective plurals in <span class="foreign">-e</span>; compare <span class="foreign">alle</span> from <span class="foreign">all</span>, <span class="foreign">summe</span> from <span class="foreign">sum</span> "some"), emerging early 14c. as modern <span class="foreign">those</span>.&#13; <br /><br />&#13; About 1175 <span class="foreign">thes</span> (probably a variant of Old English <span class="foreign">þæs</span>) began to be used as the plural of <span class="foreign">this</span>, and by 1200 it had taken the form <span class="foreign">these</span>, the final <span class="foreign">-e</span> acquired via the same mechanism that gave one to <span class="foreign">those</span>.&#13; <br /><br /><table width="90%" bgcolor="#CCFFFF" align="center" cellspacing="1" cellpadding="4" border="1"><tr><td></td><td>Masc.</td><td>Fem.</td><td>Neut.</td><td>Plural</td></tr><tr><td>Nom.</td><td>þes</td><td>þeos</td><td>þis</td><td>þas</td></tr><tr><td>Acc.</td><td>þisne</td><td>þas</td><td>þis</td><td>þas</td></tr><tr><td>Gen.</td><td>þisses</td><td>þisse</td><td>þisses</td><td>þissa</td></tr><tr><td>Dat.</td><td>þissum</td><td>þisse</td><td>þissum</td><td>þissum</td></tr><tr><td>Inst.</td><td>þys</td><td>þisse</td><td>þys</td><td>þissum</td></tr></table></dd></dl></div></div>
