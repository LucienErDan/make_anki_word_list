                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/pet">pet</a> 宠物，宠儿</span><p>可能来自petty的回构词，由小的引申词义精巧的，可爱的，后用于指宠物，宠儿。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/pet">pet (n.1)</a></dt><dd>"tamed animal," originally in Scottish and northern England dialect (and exclusively so until mid-18c.), of unknown origin. Sense of "indulged child" (c. 1500) is recorded slightly earlier than that of "animal kept as a favorite" (1530s), but the latter may be the primary meaning. Probably associated with or influenced by <a href="/etym/s/petty" target="_blank">petty</a>. As a term of endearment by 1849. <span class="foreign">Teacher's pet</span> is attested from 1890. <span class="foreign">Pet-shop</span> from 1928.&#13; <blockquote>&#13; Know nature's children all divide her care;&#13; <br />The fur that warms a monarch warm'd a bear.&#13; <br />While man exclaims, 'See all things for my use!'&#13; <br />'See man for mine!' replies a pamper'd goose: &#13; <br /><br />&#13; [Alexander Pope, "Essay on Man"]&#13; </blockquote></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/pet">pet (n.2)</a></dt><dd>"peevishness, offense at feeling slighted," 1580s, in phrase <span class="foreign">take the pet</span> "take offense." Perhaps from <a href="/etym/s/pet" target="_blank">pet</a> (n.1) on a similar notion to that in American English <span class="foreign">that gets my goat</span>, but the underlying notion is obscure, and the form of the original expression makes this doubtful. This word seems to have been originally a southern English term, while <span class="foreign">pet</span> (n.1) was northern and Scottish.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/pet">pet (v.)</a></dt><dd>1620s, "treat as a pet," from <a href="/etym/s/pet" target="_blank">pet</a> (n.1). Sense of "to stroke" is first found 1818. Slang sense of "kiss and caress" is from 1920 (implied in <a href="/etym/s/petting" target="_blank">petting</a>). Related: <span class="foreign">Petted</span>.&#13; &#13;</dd></dl></div></div>

For a child the death of a <b>pet</b> can be traumatic. </dt> <dd>对孩子来说，一只宠物的死亡可能会造成心理创伤。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>4. I sell my surplus birds to a local <b>pet</b> shop. </dt> <dd>我把多余的鸟卖给当地的宠物商店。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>5.         if (word == '') {
            return false;
        } else if (word.search(/^[\w \-]+$/) == 0) {
            top.location.href = '/w/' + encodeURIComponent(word.replace(/ /g, '_'));
            return false;

                var char_code = match.charCodeAt(0) - 0xfee0;
                return String.fromCharCode(char_code);

        }
