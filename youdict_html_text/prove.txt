            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/prove">prove</a> 证明，证实</span><p>来自拉丁语probare,检验，证明，测试，来自pro-,向前，-be,成为，是，词源同be.</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/prove">prove</a></dt><dd><b>prove: </b><span>[12] The ultimate source of <i>prove </i>was Latin <i>probus </i>‘good’. This went back (like the related Sanskrit <i>prabhu</i>- ‘eminent, mighty’) to a prehistoric Indo-European compound *<i>probhwo</i>- ‘being in front’, hence ‘excelling’ (*<i>pro</i>meant ‘in front’, and *<i>bhwo</i>- was the ancestor of English <i>be</i>). From it was derived the verb <i>probāre </i>‘test, approve, prove’, which has given English <i>approve</i>, <i>probable</i>, <i>probe</i>, <i>proof</i>, <i>reprobate</i>, <i>reprove</i>, and of course <i>prove</i>, acquired via Old French <i>prover</i>. Another Latin derivative of <i>probus </i>was <i>probitās </i>‘honesty’, from which English gets <i>probity </i>[16].</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/approve" target="_blank">approve</a>, <a href="/etym/s/probable" target="_blank">probable</a>, <a href="/etym/s/probe" target="_blank">probe</a>, <a href="/etym/s/proof" target="_blank">proof</a>, <a href="/etym/s/reprobate" target="_blank">reprobate</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/prove">prove (v.)</a></dt><dd>late 12c., <span class="foreign">pruven</span>, <span class="foreign">proven</span> "to try, test; evaluate; demonstrate," from Old French <span class="foreign">prover</span>, <span class="foreign">pruver</span> "show; convince; put to the test" (11c., Modern French <span class="foreign">prouver</span>), from Latin <span class="foreign">probare</span> "to make good; esteem, represent as good; make credible, show, demonstrate; test, inspect; judge by trial" (source also of Spanish <span class="foreign">probar</span>, Italian <span class="foreign">probare</span>), from <span class="foreign">probus</span> "worthy, good, upright, virtuous," from PIE <span class="foreign">*pro-bhwo-</span> "being in front," from <span class="foreign">*pro-</span>, extended form of root <span class="foreign">*per-</span> (1) "forward, through" (see <a href="/etym/s/per" target="_blank">per</a>), + root <span class="foreign">*bhu-</span> "to be" (cognates: Latin <span class="foreign">fui</span> "I have been," <span class="foreign">futurus</span> "about to be;" Old English <span class="foreign">beon</span> "to be;" see <a href="/etym/s/be" target="_blank">be</a>). Related: <span class="foreign">Proved</span>; <a href="/etym/s/proven" target="_blank">proven</a>; <span class="foreign">proving</span>.</dd></dl></div></div>
