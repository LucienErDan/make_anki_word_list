                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/feel">feel</a> 感觉</span><p>来自PIE*pal,触摸，感受，词源同palpitate.</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/feel">feel</a></dt><dd><b>feel: </b><span>[OE] Like its West Germanic cousins, German <i>fühlen </i>and Dutch <i>voelen</i>, <i>feel </i>is part of a wider Indo-European word-family covering notions like ‘touching’ and ‘handling’, including Greek <i>palámē </i>and Latin <i>palma </i>‘palm of the hand’ and Latin <i>palpāre</i>, originally ‘stroke, touch lightly’, later ‘feel’ (source of English <i>palpable </i>and <i>palpitation</i>). Its ultimate ancestor was the Indo-European base *<i>pōl</i>-, *<i>pal</i>-.</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/palm" target="_blank">palm</a>, <a href="/etym/s/palpable" target="_blank">palpable</a>, <a href="/etym/s/palpitation" target="_blank">palpitation</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/feel">feel (v.)</a></dt><dd>Old English <span class="foreign">felan</span> "to touch or have a sensory experience of; perceive, sense (something)," in late Old English "have a mental perception," from Proto-Germanic <span class="foreign">*foljan</span> (cognates: Old Saxon <span class="foreign">gifolian</span>, Old Frisian <span class="foreign">fela</span>, Dutch <span class="foreign">voelen</span>, Old High German <span class="foreign">vuolen</span>, German <span class="foreign">fühlen</span> "to feel," Old Norse <span class="foreign">falma</span> "to grope"), from PIE root <span class="foreign">*pal-</span> "to touch, feel, shake, strike softly" (cognates: Greek <span class="foreign">psallein</span> "to pluck (the harp)," Latin <span class="foreign">palpare</span> "to touch softly, stroke," <span class="foreign">palpitare</span> "to move quickly"), perhaps ultimately imitative.&#13; <br /><br />&#13; The meaning in Old English was "to perceive through senses which are not referred to any special organ." Sense of "be conscious of a tactile sensation, sense pain, pleasure, illness, etc.; have an emotional experience or reaction," developed by c. 1200, also "have an opinion or conviction;" that of "to react with sympathy or compassion" is from mid-14c. Meaning "to try by touch" is from early 14c. From late 14c. as "know (something) beforehand, to have foreknowledge of." To <span class="foreign">feel like</span> "want to" attested from 1829.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/feel">feel (n.)</a></dt><dd>early 13c., "sensation, understanding," from <a href="/etym/s/feel" target="_blank">feel</a> (v.). Meaning "action of feeling" is from mid-15c. That of "sensation produced (by an object, surface, etc.)" is from 1739. Slang sense of "a sexual grope" is from 1932; from verbal phrase <span class="foreign">to feel (someone) up</span> (1930).</dd></dl></div></div>

            <div class="row" id="yd-liju">
                 <dl><dt>1. I know it's nothing serious and I <b>feel</b> quite unemotional about it. </dt> <dd>我知道那根本没什么大不了的，所以有些无动于衷。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>2. I <b>feel</b> it's done me good to get it off my chest. </dt> <dd>我感觉吐吐苦水对我有好处。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>3. I've gotten my feet thoroughly soaked and <b>feel</b> frozen through and through. </dt> <dd>我双脚湿透，感觉都冻到骨头里了。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>4. He doesn't <b>feel</b> he is cut out to be a leader. </dt> <dd>他认为自己没有做领导的才能。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>5. I always <b>feel</b> at home at Ye Olde Starre Inn. </dt> <dd>在昨日星辰客栈，我总有种宾至如归的感觉。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl>                 <dl><p>[ <a href="/liju/w/feel">feel 造句</a> ]</p></dl>
