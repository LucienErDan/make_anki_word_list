                        <div class="page-header"><h3>记忆方法</h3></div>
            <div class="row" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;">1. sketch, sketching: 谐音“速稿成、使概之、是概称、蚀刻(石刻/私刻)奇、是刻起”。<br />
2. sk-（谐音“速可、是快、使快”）+ etch => sketch.<br />
3. sky + etch => sketch: 刻画、描绘、描写天空的素描。<br />
4. 软件：SketchUp 草图大师。</div>

                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/sketch">sketch</a> 素描，梗概，轮廓，草图</span><p>来自拉丁语 schedium,临时作品，来自希腊语 skhedios,临时做的，来自 PIE*segh,持有，握住， 想出，词源同 schema,scheme.引申诸相关词义。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/sketch">sketch</a></dt><dd><b>sketch: </b><span>[17] <i>Sketch </i>comes ultimately from Greek <i>skhédios </i>‘impromptu’. This reached English by a rather roundabout route: via Latin <i>schedius</i>, which led to a Vulgar Latin verb <i>schediāre </i>‘do hastily’, source of Italian <i>schizzare </i>‘make a sketch’, which in turn produced the noun <i>schizzo </i>‘sketch’, borrowed into English via German <i>skizze </i>or Dutch <i>schets</i>.</span><br></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/sketch">sketch (n.)</a></dt><dd>"rough drawing intended to serve as the basis for a finished picture," 1660s, from Dutch <span class="foreign">schets</span> or Low German <span class="foreign">skizze</span>, both apparently 17c. artists' borrowings from Italian <span class="foreign">schizzo</span> "sketch, drawing," which is commonly said to be from Latin <span class="foreign">*schedius</span> (OED compares <span class="foreign">schedia</span> "raft," <span class="foreign">schedium</span> "an extemporaneous poem"), from or related to Greek <span class="foreign">skhedios</span> "temporary, extemporaneous, done or made off-hand," related to <span class="foreign">skhema</span> "form, shape, appearance" (see <a href="/etym/s/scheme" target="_blank">scheme</a> (n.)). But according to Barnhart Italian <span class="foreign">schizzo</span> is a special use of <span class="foreign">schizzo</span> "a splash, squirt," from <span class="foreign">schizzare</span> "to splash or squirt," of uncertain origin.&#13; <br /><br />&#13; Extended sense of "brief account" is from 1660s; meaning "short play or performance, usually comic" is from 1789. <span class="foreign">Sketch-book</span> recorded from 1820. German <span class="foreign">Skizze</span>, French <span class="foreign">esquisse</span>, Spanish <span class="foreign">esquicio</span> are likewise from Italian <span class="foreign">schizzo</span>. &#13;</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/sketch">sketch (v.)</a></dt><dd>1690s, "present the essential facts of," from <a href="/etym/s/sketch" target="_blank">sketch</a> (n.). Meaning "draw, portray in outline and partial shading" is from 1725. Related: <span class="foreign">Sketched</span>; <span class="foreign">sketcher</span>; <span class="foreign">sketching</span>.</dd></dl></div></div>
