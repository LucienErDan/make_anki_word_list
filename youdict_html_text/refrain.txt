                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/refrain">refrain</a> 克制，节制</span><p>来自拉丁语 refrenare,勒住马嚼子，勒住，控制，来自 re-,向后，往回，frenare,控制，用绳子 勒住，来自 frenum,缰绳，马勒，来自 PIE*dher,握住，固定，词源同 firm,during.引申词义克 制，节制。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/refrain">refrain</a></dt><dd><b>refrain: </b><span><i>Refrain </i>‘chorus of a song’ [14] and <i>refrain </i>‘desist’ [14] are different words. The former comes via Old French <i>refrain </i>from Provençal <i>refranh</i>. This was a derivative of the verb <i>refranhar</i>, which went back via Vulgar Latin *<i>refrangere </i>to Latin <i>refringere </i>‘break off’ (source of English <i>refract </i>[17]). The etymological notion underlying the word is that the chorus of a song ‘breaks off’ and then resumes. <i>Refrain </i>‘desist’ is descended from Latin <i>refrēnāre </i>‘hold back’, a compound verb formed from the prefix <i>re</i>- ‘back’ and <i>frēnum </i>‘bridle’. It reached English via Old French <i>refrener</i>.</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/fraction" target="_blank">fraction</a>, <a href="/etym/s/refract" target="_blank">refract</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/refrain">refrain (v.)</a></dt><dd>mid-14c., from Old French <span class="foreign">refraigner</span> "restrain, repress, keep in check" (12c., Modern French <span class="foreign">Réfréner</span>), from Latin <span class="foreign">refrenare</span> "to bridle, hold in with a bit, check, curb, keep down, control," from <span class="foreign">re-</span> "back" (see <a href="/etym/s/re-" target="_blank">re-</a>) + <span class="foreign">frenare</span> "restrain, furnish with a bridle," from <span class="foreign">frenum</span> "a bridle." Related: <span class="foreign">Refrained</span>; <span class="foreign">refraining</span>.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/refrain">refrain (n.)</a></dt><dd>late 14c., from Old French <span class="foreign">refrain</span> "chorus" (13c.), alteration of <span class="foreign">refrait</span>, noun use of past participle of <span class="foreign">refraindre</span> "repeat," also "break off," from Vulgar Latin <span class="foreign">*refrangere</span> "break off," alteration of Latin <span class="foreign">refringere</span> "break up, break open" (see <a href="/etym/s/refraction" target="_blank">refraction</a>) by influence of <span class="foreign">frangere</span> "to break." Influenced in French by cognate Provençal <span class="foreign">refranhar</span> "singing of birds, refrain." The notion is of something that causes a song to "break off" then resume. OED says not common before 19c.</dd></dl></div></div>
