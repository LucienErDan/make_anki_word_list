                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/guide">guide</a> 指南</span><p>来自PIE*weid, 看，词源同vision, wise. 日耳曼w音进入罗曼语系通常发gu音。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/guide">guide</a></dt><dd><b>guide: </b><span>[14] The ancestor of <i>guide </i>was Germanic *<i>wit</i>- ‘know’, source of English <i>wise</i>, <i>wit</i>, and <i>witness</i>. From it was derived a verb *<i>wītan</i>, and the Franks, a West Germanic people who conquered Gaul in the 3rd and 4th centuries AD, brought it with them. It eventually became Old French <i>guider</i>, and was borrowed by English. The semantic progression from ‘knowing’ to ‘showing’ is also displayed in the related German <i>weisen </i>‘show, direct, indicate’.</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/wise" target="_blank">wise</a>, <a href="/etym/s/wit" target="_blank">wit</a>, <a href="/etym/s/witness" target="_blank">witness</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/guide">guide (v.)</a></dt><dd>late 14c., "to lead, direct, conduct," from Old French <span class="foreign">guider</span> "to guide, lead, conduct" (14c.), earlier <span class="foreign">guier</span>, from Frankish <span class="foreign">*witan</span> "show the way" or a similar Germanic source, from Proto-Germanic <span class="foreign">*witanan</span> "to look after, guard, ascribe to, reproach" (cognates: German <span class="foreign">weisen</span> "to show, point out," Old English <span class="foreign">witan</span> "to reproach," <span class="foreign">wite</span> "fine, penalty"), from PIE <span class="foreign">*weid-</span> "to see" (see <a href="/etym/s/vision" target="_blank">vision</a>). The form of the French word influenced by Old Provençal <span class="foreign">guidar</span> (n.) "guide, leader," or Italian <span class="foreign">guidare</span>, both from the same source. Related: <span class="foreign">Guided</span>; <span class="foreign">guiding</span>. <span class="foreign">Guided missile</span>, one capable of altering course in flight, is from 1945.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/guide">guide (n.)</a></dt><dd>mid-14c., "one who shows the way," from Old French <span class="foreign">guide</span>, 14c., verbal noun from <span class="foreign">guider</span> (see <a href="/etym/s/guide" target="_blank">guide</a> (v.)). In book titles from 1610s; meaning "book of information on local sites" is from 1759. In 18c. France, a "for Dummies" or "Idiot's Guide to" book would have been a <span class="foreign">guid' âne</span>, literally "guide-ass." <span class="foreign">Guide-dog</span> for the blind is from 1932.</dd></dl></div></div>
