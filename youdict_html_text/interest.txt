                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/interest">interest</a> 兴趣，利益，利息</span><p>来自拉丁语interesse,关心，关切，来自inter-,在内，在中间，相互，-esse,存在，是，词源同is,essence,entity.即内在的利益，兴趣等。后用于经济学指利息，以与高利贷(usury)相区别。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/interest">interest</a></dt><dd><b>interest: </b><span>[15] The Latin verb <i>interesse </i>meant literally ‘be between’ (it was a compound of <i>inter </i>‘between’ and <i>esse </i>‘be’). It was used metaphorically for ‘be of concern, be important, matter’, and appears to have been borrowed into Anglo-Norman as a noun, meaning ‘what one has a legal concern in or share of’. English took this over in the 14th century as <i>interesse</i>, but it gradually changed over the next hundred years or so into <i>interest</i>, mainly due to the influence of Old French <i>interest </i>‘damage’, which came from the third person present singular form of the Latin verb.<br><br>The main modern sense ‘curiosity’ developed towards the end of the 18th century.</span><br></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/interest">interest (n.)</a></dt><dd>mid-15c., "legal claim or right; concern; benefit, advantage;" earlier <span class="foreign">interesse</span> (late 14c.), from Anglo-French <span class="foreign">interesse</span> "what one has a legal concern in," from Medieval Latin <span class="foreign">interesse</span> "compensation for loss," noun use of Latin <span class="foreign">interresse</span> "to concern, make a difference, be of importance," literally "to be between," from <span class="foreign">inter-</span> "between" (see <a href="/etym/s/inter-" target="_blank">inter-</a>) + <span class="foreign">esse</span> "to be" (see <a href="/etym/s/essence" target="_blank">essence</a>).&#13; <br /><br />&#13; Compare German <span class="foreign">Interesse</span>, from the same Medieval Latin source. Form in English influenced 15c. by French <span class="foreign">interest</span> "damage," from Latin <span class="foreign">interest</span> "it is of importance, it makes a difference," third person singular present of <span class="foreign">interresse</span>. Financial sense of "money paid for the use of money lent" (1520s) earlier was distinguished from <span class="foreign">usury</span> (illegal under Church law) by being in reference to "compensation due from a defaulting debtor." Meaning "curiosity" is first attested 1771. <span class="foreign">Interest group</span> is attested from 1907; <span class="foreign">interest rate</span> by 1868.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/interest">interest (v.)</a></dt><dd>"to cause to be interested," c. 1600, earlier <span class="foreign">interesse</span> (1560s), from the noun (see <a href="/etym/s/interest" target="_blank">interest</a> (n.)). Perhaps also from or influenced by <span class="foreign">interess'd</span>, past participle of <span class="foreign">interesse</span>.</dd></dl></div></div>
