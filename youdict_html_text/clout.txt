                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/clout">clout</a> 猛击，影响力</span><p>词源同clod,clot,cloud.原指大块状的东西。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/clout">clout</a></dt><dd><b>clout: </b><span>[OE] In Old English, a <i>clout </i>was a patch of cloth put over a hole to mend it. Hence in due course it came to be used simply for a ‘piece of cloth’, and by further extension for a ‘garment’ (as in ‘Ne’er cast a clout till May be out’). However, the reason for its colloquial application to ‘hit, blow’, which dates from the 14th century, is not known, and indeed this may be an entirely different word. As for the word’s ultimate antecedents, it probably comes, along with <i>cleat</i>, <i>clot</i>, <i>cluster</i>, and <i>clutter</i>, from a prehistoric Germanic base *<i>klut</i>-, *<i>kleut</i>-, *<i>klaut</i>-.</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/cleat" target="_blank">cleat</a>, <a href="/etym/s/clot" target="_blank">clot</a>, <a href="/etym/s/cluster" target="_blank">cluster</a>, <a href="/etym/s/clutter" target="_blank">clutter</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/clout">clout (n.)</a></dt><dd>Old English <span class="foreign">clut</span> "lump of something," also "patch of cloth put over a hole to mend it," from Proto-Germanic <span class="foreign">*klutaz</span> (cognates: Old Norse <span class="foreign">klute</span> "kerchief," Danish <span class="foreign">klud</span> "rag, tatter," Frisian <span class="foreign">klut</span> "lump," Dutch <span class="foreign">kluit</span> "clod, lump"); perhaps related to <a href="/etym/s/clot" target="_blank">clot</a> (v.).&#13; <br /><br />&#13; In later use "a handkerchief," also "a woman's sanitary napkin." Sense of "a blow" is from c. 1400 early 14c., from the verb. Sense of "personal influence" is 1958, on the notion of "punch, force."</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/clout">clout (v.)</a></dt><dd>"to beat, strike," early 14c., from <a href="/etym/s/clout" target="_blank">clout</a> (n.), perhaps on the notion of hitting someone with a lump of something, or from the "patch of cloth" sense of that word (compare <span class="foreign">clout</span> (v.) "to patch, mend," mid-14c.). Related: <span class="foreign">Clouted</span>; <span class="foreign">clouting</span>.</dd></dl></div></div>
