                        <div class="page-header"><h3>记忆方法</h3></div>
            <div class="row" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;">1. 不能够持续就爆炸, 爆炸就是那种瞬间炸开的东西，而不是那种持续性的、慢慢的发生的那种情形。<br />
2. 不持续就爆炸. 无法持续只能爆炸.<br />
3. blast => clast: c‐开，炸开了岩石成碎屑.<br />
4. 由b到c, 这是字母顺序关联性，因此clast可以看成是blast爆炸后的结果.</div>

            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/blast">blast</a> 爆炸</span><p>词源同blow.</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/blast">blast (n.)</a></dt><dd>Old English <span class="foreign">blæst</span> "blowing, breeze, puff of wind," from Proto-Germanic <span class="foreign">*bles-</span> (cognates: Old Norse <span class="foreign">blastr</span>, Old High German <span class="foreign">blast</span> "a blowing, blast," German <span class="foreign">blasen</span>, Gothic <span class="foreign">blesan</span> "to blow"), from PIE <span class="foreign">*bhle-</span> "to blow," probably a variant of root <span class="foreign">*bhel-</span> (2) "to blow, inflate, swell" (see <a href="/etym/s/bole" target="_blank">bole</a>).&#13; <br /><br />&#13; Meaning "explosion" is from 1630s; that of "noisy party, good time" is from 1953, American English slang. Sense of "strong current of air for iron-smelting" (1690s) led to <span class="foreign">blast furnace</span> and transferred sense in <span class="foreign">full blast</span> "the extreme" (1839). <span class="foreign">Blast</span> was the usual word for "a smoke of tobacco" c. 1600.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/blast">blast (v.)</a></dt><dd>Old English <span class="foreign">blæstan</span> "to blow, belch forth," from the root of <a href="/etym/s/blast" target="_blank">blast</a> (n.). Since 16c., often "to breathe on balefully." Meaning "to blow up by explosion" is from 1758. Related: <span class="foreign">Blasted</span>; <span class="foreign">blasting</span>. <span class="foreign">Blast off</span> (n.) is attested from 1950.</dd></dl></div></div>
