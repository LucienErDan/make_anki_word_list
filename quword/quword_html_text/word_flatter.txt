            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/flatter">flatter</a> 奉承，讨好</span><p>flat, 平的。-er, 表反复。引申词义俯伏在地，拜倒，奉承等。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/flatter">flatter</a></dt><dd><b>flatter: </b><span>[13] Etymologically, <i>flatter </i>means ‘smooth down or caress with the flat of the hand’. It comes from Old French <i>flatter</i>, in which the original literal notion of ‘caressing’ had already passed into the figurative ‘buttering up’. The Old French verb in turn was based on Frankish *<i>flat</i>, the ‘flat or palm of someone’s hand’, a word which shared a common source with English <i>flat</i>.</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/flat" target="_blank">flat</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/flatter">flatter (v.)</a></dt><dd>c. 1200, <span class="foreign">flateren</span>, <span class="foreign">flaterien</span>, "seek to please or gratify (someone) by undue praise, praise insincerely, beguile with pleasing words," from Old French <span class="foreign">flater</span> "to deceive; caress, fondle; prostrate, throw, fling (to the ground)" (13c.), probably from a Germanic source, perhaps from Proto-Germanic <span class="foreign">*flata-</span> "flat" (see <a href="/etym/s/flat" target="_blank">flat</a> (adj.)).&#13; <br /><br />&#13; "Of somewhat doubtful etymology" [OED]. Liberman calls it "one of many imitative verbs beginning with <span class="foreign">fl-</span> and denoting unsteady or light, repeated movement" (for example <span class="foreign">flicker</span>, <span class="foreign">flutter</span>). If it is related to <span class="foreign">flat</span> the notion could be either "caress with the flat of the hand, stroke, pet," or "throw oneself flat on the ground" (in fawning adoration). The <span class="foreign">-er</span> ending is unusual for an English verb from French; perhaps it is by influence of <span class="foreign">shimmer</span>, <span class="foreign">flicker</span>, etc., or from <span class="foreign">flattery</span>.&#13; <br /><br />&#13; Meaning "give a pleasing but false impression to" is from late 14c. Sense of "show (something) to best advantage" is from 1580s, originally of portraits. Related: <span class="foreign">Flattered</span>; <a href="/etym/s/flattering" target="_blank">flattering</a>.</dd></dl></div></div>

            <div class="row" id="yd-liju">

You can make your stomach look <b>flatter</b> instantly by improving your posture. </dt> <dd>通过采取更好的坐姿，你的腹部马上就会看起来平坦一些。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>3. I <b>flatter</b> myself I've done it all rather well. </dt> <dd>我认为自己已经做得相当好了。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>4. Orange and khaki <b>flatter</b> those with golden skin tones. </dt> <dd>橘黄色和卡其色能衬托那些金黄色皮肤的人。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>5. Strong colours would <b>flatter</b> her pale skin and dark hair. </dt> <dd>亮颜色更能衬托她的白皮肤和黑头发。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl>                 <dl><p>[ <a href="/liju/w/flatter">flatter 造句</a> ]</p></dl>
