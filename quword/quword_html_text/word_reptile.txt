                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/reptile">reptile</a> 爬行动物</span><p>来自拉丁语 repere,爬行，-t,过去分词格，来自 PIE*rep,爬行。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/reptile">reptile (n.)</a></dt><dd>late 14c., "creeping or crawling animal," from Old French <span class="foreign">reptile</span> (early 14c.) and directly from Late Latin <span class="foreign">reptile</span>, noun use of neuter of <span class="foreign">reptilis</span> (adj.) "creping, crawling," from <span class="foreign">rept-</span>, past participle stem of <span class="foreign">repere</span> "to crawl, creep," from PIE root <span class="foreign">*rep-</span> "to creep, crawl" (cognates: Lithuanian <span class="foreign">replioju</span> "to creep"). Used of persons of low character from 1749.&#13; <br /><br />&#13; Precise scientific use began to develop mid-18c., but the word was used as well at first of animals now known as amphibians, including toads, frogs, salamanders; separation of <span class="foreign">Reptilia</span> (1835 as a distinct class) and <span class="foreign">Amphibia</span> took place early 19c.; popular use lagged, and <span class="foreign">reptile</span> still was used late 18c. with sense "An animal that creeps upon many feet" [Johnson, who calls the scorpion a reptile], sometimes excluding serpents.&#13; <blockquote>&#13; And the terrestrial animals may be divided into quadrupeds or beasts, reptiles, which have many feet, and serpents, which have no feet at all. [Locke, "Elements of Natural Philosophy," 1689]&#13; <br /><br /><hr /><br />&#13; An inadvertent step may crush the snail&#13; <br />That crawls at ev'ning in the public path ;&#13; <br />But he that has humanity, forewarn'd,&#13; <br />Will tread aside, and let the reptile live.&#13; <br />[Cowper, "The Task," 1785]&#13; </blockquote>&#13; The Old English word for "reptile" was <span class="foreign">slincend</span>, related to <a href="/etym/s/slink" target="_blank">slink</a>.</dd></dl></div></div>

Rise, and don't degrade youself into an abject <b>reptile</b>. </dt> <dd>起来, 可别把你自己贬成一个下贱的爬虫.</dd> <p>来自辞典例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>3. The land is teeming with <b>reptile</b> life. </dt> <dd>这片土地多爬行动物.</dd> <p>来自辞典例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>4. The frog is not a true <b>reptile</b>. </dt> <dd>青蛙并非真正的爬行动物.</dd> <p>来自辞典例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>5. So you should not be surprised to see someone keep a <b>reptile</b> as a pet. </dt> <dd>所以,你不必惊奇有人养了一只爬行动物作为宠物.</dd> <p>来自英语晨读30分（高二）</p> </dl>                 <dl><p>[ <a href="/liju/w/reptile">reptile 造句</a> ]</p></dl>

    document.getElementById('yd-search').onsubmit = function() {
        var word = this.word.value.replace(/\u3000/g, ' ').replace(/(^\s+|\s+$)/g, '');
        if (word == '') {
            return false;
        } else if (word.search(/^[\w \-]+$/) == 0) {
            top.location.href = '/w/' + encodeURIComponent(word.replace(/ /g, '_'));
            return false;
        } else {
            this.word.value = word.replace(/[\uff01-\uffe1]/g, function(match) {
                var char_code = match.charCodeAt(0) - 0xfee0;
                return String.fromCharCode(char_code);
            });
        }
    }
