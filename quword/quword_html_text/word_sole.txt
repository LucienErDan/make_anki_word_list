                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/sole">sole</a> 单独的，唯一的，全权处理的</span><p>来自拉丁语 solus,单独的，唯一的，词源不详。可能来自 se,自己，来自 PIE*swo,自身，词源 同 self,solitary.引申比喻义全权处理的。</p><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/sole">sole</a> 脚掌，鳎（一种可食用比目鱼）</span><p>来自拉丁语 solum,脚掌，底部，基础，词源同 soil,大地，陆地。后也用于指一种可食用的比 目鱼，因其形如脚掌而得名。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/sole">sole</a></dt><dd><b>sole: </b><span>English has three separate words <i>sole</i>, two of them closely related. <i>Sole </i>‘underneath of the foot’ [14] comes via Old French <i>sole </i>from Vulgar Latin *<i>sola</i>, a descendant of Latin <i>solea </i>‘sandal, sill’ (a possible relative of English <i>sill</i>). And this in turn was derived from Latin <i>solum </i>‘ground, sole of the foot’ (a possible contributor to English <i>soil</i>). <i>Sole </i>‘flatfish’ [14] was independently borrowed from Old French <i>sole </i>in the sense ‘flatfish’, a metaphorical extension based on the similarity in shape between the fish and the sole of the foot. <i>Sole </i>‘only’ [14] comes via Old French <i>soul </i>(ancestor of modern French <i>seul </i>‘only, sole’) from Latin <i>sōlus </i>‘alone, single’.<br><br>The origins of this are uncertain, but it may be related to the pronoun <i>sē </i>‘oneself’, in which case it could mean etymologically ‘by oneself’. Its other contributions to English include <i>desolate </i>[14], <i>soliloquy </i>[17], <i>solitary </i>[14], <i>solo </i>[17] (via Italian), and <i>sullen</i>.</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/sill" target="_blank">sill</a>; <a href="/etym/s/desolate" target="_blank">desolate</a>, <a href="/etym/s/solitary" target="_blank">solitary</a>, <a href="/etym/s/solo" target="_blank">solo</a>, <a href="/etym/s/sullen" target="_blank">sullen</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/sole">sole (n.1)</a></dt><dd>"bottom of the foot" ("technically, the planta, corresponding to the palm of the hand," Century Dictionary), early 14c., from Old French <span class="foreign">sole</span>, from Vulgar Latin <span class="foreign">*sola</span>, from Latin <span class="foreign">solea</span> "sandal, bottom of a shoe; a flatfish," from <span class="foreign">solum</span> "bottom, ground, foundation, lowest point of a thing" (hence "sole of the foot"), a word of uncertain origin. In English, the meaning "bottom of a shoe or boot" is from late 14c.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/sole">sole (adj.)</a></dt><dd>"single, alone, having no husband or wife; one and only, singular, unique," late 14c., from Old French <span class="foreign">soul</span> "only, alone, just," from Latin <span class="foreign">solus</span> "alone, only, single, sole; forsaken; extraordinary," of unknown origin, perhaps related to <span class="foreign">se</span> "oneself," from PIE reflexive root <span class="foreign">*swo-</span> (see <a href="/etym/s/so" target="_blank">so</a>).</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/sole">sole (n.2)</a></dt><dd>common European flatfish, mid-13c., from Old French <span class="foreign">sole</span>, from Latin <span class="foreign">solea</span> "a kind of flatfish," originally "sandal" (see <a href="/etym/s/sole" target="_blank">sole</a> (n.1)); so called from resemblance of the fish to a flat shoe.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/sole">sole (v.)</a></dt><dd>"furnish (a shoe) with a sole," 1560s, from <a href="/etym/s/sole" target="_blank">sole</a> (n.1). Related: <span class="foreign">Soled</span>; <span class="foreign">soling</span>.</dd></dl></div></div>
