                        <div class="page-header"><h3>记忆方法</h3></div>
            <div class="row" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;">1、plag- + -ue.<br />
2、=> malignant disease strike to people, or that disease makes people pain and agony.<br />
3、想象一下让人们哀鸿遍野的瘟疫以及天灾人祸吧！<br />
4. pl- + argue => plargue => plague.<br />
5. 漂亮(pl)美眉每天和你争吵(argue)，在你面前唠叨、争吵个不停、没完没了，就连花儿(r)都是受不了而凋谢掉落了，更何况你呢？因此这对你来说简直就是灾难，就像瘟疫一样想摆脱都摆脱不了，这简直就是对你的折磨，让你苦恼不已。<br />
6. 所以娶个漂亮美眉并不一定是好事，她很可能脾气大、你得惯着她，伺候她，稍不顺心就和你争吵、对你纠缠不休，让你痛苦不堪，对你来说简直就是灾难。<br />
7. 因此，敬告各位男同胞们，千万不要跟漂亮美眉争吵，否则是灾难性的后果！<br />
8. plague 读作:铺雷哥。越南丛林里铺地雷的哥哥染上了瘟疫。</div>

                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/plague">plague</a> 瘟疫，祸患</span><p>来自拉丁语plaga,瘟疫，灾害，来自plangere,击，打，哀伤，捶胸顿足，来自PIE*plak,击，打，捶胸，可能来自声词，捶胸哀叹的声音，词源同plangent,apoplexy.比喻用法。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/plague">plague</a></dt><dd><b>plague: </b><span>[14] Etymologically, <i>plague </i>means a ‘blow’ or ‘stroke’. It goes back to the same prehistoric base, *<i>plag</i>- ‘hit’, as produced Latin <i>plangere </i>‘beat’ (source of English <i>complain</i>, <i>plaintiff </i>[14], <i>plaintive </i>[14], and <i>plangent </i>[19] – which originally denoted the sound of waves ‘beating’ against the shore) and English <i>plankton</i>.<br><br>From this was derived Greek <i>plāgá </i>‘blow’, which was borrowed into Latin as <i>plāga </i>‘blow’, hence ‘wound’. In the Vulgate it was used for an ‘infectious disease’, and was borrowed in this sense (as well as the now defunct ‘blow’) via Old French into English. (*<i>Plak</i>-, a parallel form to *<i>plag</i>-, lies behind English <i>apoplexy </i>and <i>plectrum </i>[17].)</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/apoplexy" target="_blank">apoplexy</a>, <a href="/etym/s/complain" target="_blank">complain</a>, <a href="/etym/s/plaintive" target="_blank">plaintive</a>, <a href="/etym/s/plangent" target="_blank">plangent</a>, <a href="/etym/s/plankton" target="_blank">plankton</a>, <a href="/etym/s/plectrum" target="_blank">plectrum</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/plague">plague (n.)</a></dt><dd>late 14c., <span class="foreign">plage</span>, "affliction, calamity, evil, scourge;" early 15c., "malignant disease," from Old French <span class="foreign">plage</span> (14c.), from Late Latin <span class="foreign">plaga</span>, used in Vulgate for "pestilence," from Latin <span class="foreign">plaga</span> "stroke, wound," probably from root of <span class="foreign">plangere</span> "to strike, lament (by beating the breast)," from or cognate with Greek (Doric) <span class="foreign">plaga</span> "blow," from PIE <span class="foreign">*plak-</span> (2) "to strike, to hit" (cognates: Greek <span class="foreign">plazein</span> "to drive away," <span class="foreign">plessein</span> "to beat, strike;" Old English <span class="foreign">flocan</span> "to strike, beat;" Gothic <span class="foreign">flokan</span> "to bewail;" German <span class="foreign">fluchen</span>, Old Frisian <span class="foreign">floka</span> "to curse").&#13; <br /><br />&#13; The Latin word also is the source of Old Irish <span class="foreign">plag</span> (genitive <span class="foreign">plaige</span>) "plague, pestilence," German <span class="foreign">Plage</span>, Dutch <span class="foreign">plaage</span>. Meaning "epidemic that causes many deaths" is from 1540s; specifically in reference to bubonic plague from c. 1600. Modern spelling follows French, which had <span class="foreign">plague</span> from 15c. Weakened sense of "anything annoying" is from c. 1600.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/plague">plague (v.)</a></dt><dd>late 15c., from Middle Dutch <span class="foreign">plaghen</span>, from <span class="foreign">plaghe</span> (n.) "plague" (see <a href="/etym/s/plague" target="_blank">plague</a> (n.)). Sense of "bother, annoy" it is first recorded 1590s. Related: <span class="foreign">Plagued</span>; <span class="foreign">plaguing</span>.</dd></dl></div></div>
