            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/dirk">dirk (n.)</a></dt><dd>c. 1600, perhaps from <span class="foreign">Dirk</span>, the proper name, which was used in Scandinavian for "a picklock." But the earliest spellings were <span class="foreign">dork</span>, <span class="foreign">durk</span> (Johnson, 1755, seems to be responsible for the modern spelling), and the earliest association is with Highlanders, however there seems to be no such word in Gaelic, where the proper name is <span class="foreign">biodag</span>. Another candidate is German <span class="foreign">dolch</span> "dagger." The masc. given name is a variant of <span class="foreign">Derrick</span>, ultimately from the Germanic compound in <a href="/etym/s/Dietrich" target="_blank">Dietrich</a>.</dd></dl></div></div>
