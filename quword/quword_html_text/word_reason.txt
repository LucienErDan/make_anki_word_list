                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/reason">reason</a> 原因，理由，推理，推论</span><p>来自古法语 raison,原因，理由，思考，来自拉丁语 rationem,思考，考虑，动机，来自 PIE*rei, 思考，辩理，词源同 rate,read.</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/reason">reason</a></dt><dd><b>reason: </b><span>[13] <i>Reason</i>, together with <i>rational</i>, represent in English the ‘thinking’ aspects of the Latin verb <i>rērī </i>(it also meant ‘calculate’, and in that guise has given English <i>rate</i>, <i>ration</i>, etc). From it was derived the noun <i>ratiō </i>‘thinking, calculation’ (source of English <i>ratio </i>and the rest). This spawned a Vulgar Latin variant *<i>ratiōne</i>, which passed into Old French as <i>reisun </i>– whence English <i>reason</i>.</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/rate" target="_blank">rate</a>, <a href="/etym/s/ratio" target="_blank">ratio</a>, <a href="/etym/s/ration" target="_blank">ration</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/reason">reason (n.)</a></dt><dd>c. 1200, "intellectual faculty that adopts actions to ends," also "statement in an argument, statement of explanation or justification," from Anglo-French <span class="foreign">resoun</span>, Old French <span class="foreign">raison</span> "course; matter; subject; language, speech; thought, opinion," from Latin <span class="foreign">rationem</span> (nominative <span class="foreign">ratio</span>) "reckoning, understanding, motive, cause," from <span class="foreign">ratus</span>, past participle of <span class="foreign">reri</span> "to reckon, think," from PIE root <span class="foreign">*re(i)-</span> "to reason, count" (source of Old English <span class="foreign">rædan</span> "to advise;" see <a href="/etym/s/read" target="_blank">read</a> (v.)).&#13; <br /><br />&#13; Meaning "sanity; degree of intelligence that distinguishes men from brutes" is recorded from late 13c. Sense of "grounds for action, motive, cause of an event" is from c. 1300. Middle English sense of "meaning, signification" (early 14c.) is in the phrase <span class="foreign">rhyme or reason</span>. Phrase <span class="foreign">it stands to reason</span> is from 1630s. <span class="foreign">Age of Reason</span> "the Enlightenment" is first recorded 1794, as the title of Tom Paine's book.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/reason">reason (v.)</a></dt><dd>early 14c., <span class="foreign">resunmen</span>, "to question (someone)," also "to challenge," from Old French <span class="foreign">raisoner</span> "speak, discuss; argue; address; speak to," from Late Latin <span class="foreign">rationare</span> "to discourse," from <span class="foreign">ratio</span> (see <a href="/etym/s/reason" target="_blank">reason</a> (n.)). Intransitive sense of "to think in a logical manner" is from 1590s; transitive sense of "employ reasoning (with someone)" is from 1847. Related: <span class="foreign">Reasoned</span>; <a href="/etym/s/reasoning" target="_blank">reasoning</a>.</dd></dl></div></div>
                                    <div class="page-header"><h3><a href="/liju/w/reason">权威例句</a></h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-liju">
                 <dl><dt>1. I really don't see any <b>reason</b> for changing it. </dt> <dd>我真的不明白为什么要改变它。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>2. There's a <b>reason</b> why women don't read this stuff; it's not funny. </dt> <dd>女人不读这种东西是有原因的，它并不好笑。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>3. To an extent, that is the <b>reason</b> for the meeting. </dt> <dd>在某种程度上，那就是召开这次会议的原因。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>4. The <b>reason</b> women are drinking is just because they like it. </dt> <dd>女人喝酒只是因为她们喜欢喝。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>5. The boss retains enormous influence by <b>reason</b> of his position. </dt> <dd>老板由于自身的地位而一直有极大的影响力。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl>                 <dl><p>[ <a href="/liju/w/reason">reason 造句</a> ]</p></dl>
