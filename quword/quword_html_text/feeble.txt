                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/feeble">feeble</a> 虚弱的</span><p>来自拉丁语flebilis的拼写异化词，呻吟，哭叫，词源同bleat, blare.</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/feeble">feeble</a></dt><dd><b>feeble: </b><span>[12] Semantically, <i>feeble </i>was originally a close relative of <i>deplorable </i>and <i>lamentable</i>, but over the centuries it has diverged markedly from them. Its ultimate source was Latin <i>flēbilis</i>, a derivative of the verb <i>flēre </i>‘weep’. In classical times this meant literally ‘worthy of being cried over, lamentable’, but later it came to signify ‘weak’. It passed in this sense into Old French as <i>fleible</i>, which subsequently became <i>feible </i>or <i>feble </i>(source of English <i>feeble</i>), and later still <i>foible </i>(whence English <i>foible </i>[17]) and <i>faible </i>(the modern French form).</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/foible" target="_blank">foible</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/feeble">feeble (adj.)</a></dt><dd>late 12c., "lacking strength or vigor" (physical, moral, or intellectual), from Old French <span class="foreign">feble</span> "weak, feeble" (12c., Modern French <span class="foreign">faible</span>), dissimilated from Latin <span class="foreign">flebilis</span> "lamentable," literally "that is to be wept over," from <span class="foreign">flere</span> "weep, cry, shed tears, lament," from PIE <span class="foreign">*bhle-</span> "to howl" (see <a href="/etym/s/bleat" target="_blank">bleat</a> (v.)). The first <span class="foreign">-l-</span> was lost in Old French. The noun meaning "feeble person" is recorded from mid-14c.</dd></dl></div></div>
