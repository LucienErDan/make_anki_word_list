                        <div class="page-header"><h3>记忆方法</h3></div>
            <div class="row" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;">1、dis- "apart" + cip- => discip- "grasp intellectually, analyze thoroughly" => 进而引申为: learn.<br />
2、discip- + -le(diminutive) => people that learn.<br />
3、disciple => discipl- + -ine.<br />
4、含义：order necessary for instruction, treatment that corrects or punishes, branch of instruction or education, military training, orderly conduct as a result of training.</div>

                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/discipline">discipline</a></dt><dd><b>discipline: </b><span>[13] The Latin word for ‘learner’ was <i>discipulus</i>, a derivative of the verb <i>discere </i>‘learn’ (which was related to <i>docēre </i>‘teach’, source of English <i>doctor</i>, <i>doctrine</i>, and <i>document</i>). English acquired the word in Anglo- Saxon times, as <i>discipul</i>, and it was subsequently reformulated as <i>disciple </i>on the model of Old French <i>deciple</i>. Derived from <i>discipulus </i>was the noun <i>disciplīna </i>‘instruction, knowledge’. Its meaning developed gradually into ‘maintenance of order (necessary for giving instruction)’, the sense in which the word first entered English (via Old French <i>discipline</i>).</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/disciple" target="_blank">disciple</a>, <a href="/etym/s/doctor" target="_blank">doctor</a>, <a href="/etym/s/doctrine" target="_blank">doctrine</a>, <a href="/etym/s/document" target="_blank">document</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/discipline">discipline (n.)</a></dt><dd>early 13c., "penitential chastisement; punishment," from Old French <span class="foreign">descepline</span> (11c.) "discipline, physical punishment; teaching; suffering; martyrdom," and directly from Latin <span class="foreign">disciplina</span> "instruction given, teaching, learning, knowledge," also "object of instruction, knowledge, science, military discipline," from <span class="foreign">discipulus</span> (see <a href="/etym/s/disciple" target="_blank">disciple</a> (n.)).&#13; <br /><br />&#13; Sense of "treatment that corrects or punishes" is from notion of "order necessary for instruction." The Latin word is glossed in Old English by <span class="foreign">þeodscipe</span>.&#13; Meaning "branch of instruction or education" is first recorded late 14c. Meaning "military training" is from late 15c.; that of "orderly conduct as a result of training" is from c. 1500.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/discipline">discipline (v.)</a></dt><dd>c. 1300; see <a href="/etym/s/discipline" target="_blank">discipline</a> (n.). Related: <span class="foreign">Disciplined</span>; <span class="foreign">disciplines</span>; <span class="foreign">disciplining</span>.</dd></dl></div></div>
