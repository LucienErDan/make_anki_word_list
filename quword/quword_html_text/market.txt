                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/market">market</a> 集市，市场，销售</span><p>来自拉丁语mercari,交易，买卖，词源同mercantile,commercial.可能来自意大利特鲁斯坎语。引申词义市场，销售，营销等。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/market">market</a></dt><dd><b>market: </b><span>[12] The Latin word for ‘goods to be sold’ was <i>merx </i>(source of English <i>commerce</i>, <i>merchant</i>, and <i>mercury</i>). From it was derived the verb <i>mercārī </i>‘buy’, and its past participle produced the noun <i>mercātus </i>‘trade, market’. In Vulgar Latin this became *<i>marcātus</i>, which was adopted into early Middle English as <i>market</i>. The now seldom used synonym <i>mart </i>[15] comes from early modern Dutch <i>mart</i>, a variant of <i>markt </i>‘market’.</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/commerce" target="_blank">commerce</a>, <a href="/etym/s/mart" target="_blank">mart</a>, <a href="/etym/s/merchant" target="_blank">merchant</a>, <a href="/etym/s/mercury" target="_blank">mercury</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/market">market (n.)</a></dt><dd>early 12c., "a meeting at a fixed time for buying and selling livestock and provisions," from Old North French <span class="foreign">market</span> "marketplace, trade, commerce" (Old French <span class="foreign">marchiet</span>, Modern French <span class="foreign">marché</span>), from Latin <span class="foreign">mercatus</span> "trading, buying and selling, trade, market" (source of Italian <span class="foreign">mercato</span>, Spanish <span class="foreign">mercado</span>, Dutch <span class="foreign">markt</span>, German <span class="foreign">Markt</span>), from past participle of <span class="foreign">mercari</span> "to trade, deal in, buy," from <span class="foreign">merx</span> (genitive <span class="foreign">mercis</span>) "wares, merchandise," from Italic root <span class="foreign">*merk-</span>, possibly from Etruscan, referring to various aspects of economics. Meaning "public building or space where markets are held" first attested mid-13c. Sense of "sales, as controlled by supply and demand" is from 1680s. <span class="foreign">Market value</span> (1690s) first attested in writings of John Locke. <span class="foreign">Market economy</span> is from 1948; <span class="foreign">market research</span> is from 1921.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/market">market (v.)</a></dt><dd>1630s, from <a href="/etym/s/market" target="_blank">market</a> (n.). Related: <span class="foreign">Marketed</span>; <span class="foreign">marketing</span>.</dd></dl></div></div>
