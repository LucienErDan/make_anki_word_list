                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/repair">repair</a> 修复</span><p>来自拉丁语 reparare,修复，复原，来自 re-,再，重新，parare,准备，词源同 prepare.</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/repair">repair</a></dt><dd><b>repair: </b><span><i>Repair </i>‘mend’ [14] and <i>repair </i>‘go’ [14] are two distinct words. The former comes via Old French <i>reparer </i>from Latin <i>reparāre </i>‘put back in order’, a compound verb formed from the prefix <i>re</i>- ‘back’ and <i>parāre </i>‘put in order’ (source of English <i>prepare</i>). <i>Repair </i>‘go’ is ultimately the same word as <i>repatriate </i>[17]. Both go back to late Latin <i>repatriāre </i>‘go home’, a compound verb based on Latin <i>patria </i>‘homeland’ (a relative of English <i>father</i>, <i>patron</i>, etc). <i>Repatriate </i>was acquired direct from Latin, whereas <i>repair </i>was routed via Old French <i>repairer</i>.</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/prepare" target="_blank">prepare</a>; <a href="/etym/s/father" target="_blank">father</a>, <a href="/etym/s/paternal" target="_blank">paternal</a>, <a href="/etym/s/patriot" target="_blank">patriot</a>, <a href="/etym/s/patron" target="_blank">patron</a>, <a href="/etym/s/repatriate" target="_blank">repatriate</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/repair">repair (v.1)</a></dt><dd>"to mend, to put back in order," mid-14c., from Old French <span class="foreign">reparer</span> "repair, mend" (12c.), from Latin <span class="foreign">reparare</span> "restore, put back in order," from <span class="foreign">re-</span> "again" (see <a href="/etym/s/re-" target="_blank">re-</a>) + <span class="foreign">parare</span> "make ready, prepare" (see <a href="/etym/s/pare" target="_blank">pare</a>). Related: <span class="foreign">Repaired</span>; <span class="foreign">repairing</span>.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/repair">repair (v.2)</a></dt><dd>"go" (to a place), c. 1300, from Old French <span class="foreign">repairer</span> "to frequent, return (to one's country)," earlier <span class="foreign">repadrer</span>, from Late Latin <span class="foreign">repatriare</span> "return to one's own country" (see <a href="/etym/s/repatriate" target="_blank">repatriate</a>). Related: <span class="foreign">Repaired</span>; <span class="foreign">repairing</span>.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/repair">repair (n.)</a></dt><dd>1590s, "act of restoring, restoration after decay," from <a href="/etym/s/repair" target="_blank">repair</a> (v.1). Meaning "state or condition in respect to reparation" is from c. 1600.</dd></dl></div></div>
