                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/ancient">ancient</a></dt><dd><b>ancient: </b><span>[14] Like <i>antique</i>, <i>ancient </i>was originally, in Latin, an adjectivized version of the adverb and preposition ‘before’: to <i>ante </i>‘before’ was added the adjective suffix -<i>ānus</i>, to produce the adjective *<i>anteānus </i>‘going before’. In Old French this became <i>ancien</i>, and it passed into English via Anglo-Norman <i>auncien</i>. The final -<i>t </i>began to appear in the 15th century, by the same phonetic process as produced it in <i>pageant </i>and <i>tyrant</i>. The now archaic use of <i>ancient </i>as ‘standard, flag’ and as ‘standard-bearer’ (as most famously in Shakespeare’s ‘ancient Pistol’) arose from an alteration of <i>ensign</i>.</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/antique" target="_blank">antique</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/ancient">ancient (adj.)</a></dt><dd>mid-14c., <span class="foreign">auncyen</span>, from Old French <span class="foreign">ancien</span> "old, long-standing, ancient," from Vulgar Latin <span class="foreign">*anteanus</span>, literally "from before," adjectivization of Latin <span class="foreign">ante</span> "before, in front of, against" (from PIE <span class="foreign">*anti</span> "against," locative singular of <span class="foreign">*ant-</span> "front, forehead;" see <a href="/etym/s/ante" target="_blank">ante</a>). The parasitic <span class="foreign">-t</span> dates from 15c. by influence of words in <span class="foreign">-ent</span>.&#13; <br /><br />&#13; Specifically, in history, "belonging to the period before the fall of the Western Roman Empire" (and contrasted with <span class="foreign">medieval</span> and <span class="foreign">modern</span>). In English law, "from before the Norman Conquest." <span class="foreign">Ancient of Days</span> is from Dan. vii:9. Related: <span class="foreign">Anciently</span>.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/ancient">ancient (n.)</a></dt><dd>"standard-bearer," 1550s, a corruption of <a href="/etym/s/ensign" target="_blank">ensign</a>. Archaic, but preserved in Shakespeare's character <span class="foreign">Aunchient Pistoll</span> in "Henry V."</dd></dl></div></div>
