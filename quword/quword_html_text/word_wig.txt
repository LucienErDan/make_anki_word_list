                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/wig">wig</a> 假发</span><p>缩写自periwig.</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/wig">wig</a></dt><dd><b>wig: </b><span>[17] <i>Wig </i>is short for <i>periwig </i>[16], which in turn is an alteration of <i>perwike</i>, a now defunct variant of <i>peruke </i>[16]; and <i>peruke </i>came via French <i>perruque </i>from Italian <i>perrucca</i>, a word of unknown origin. <i>Wigging </i>‘scolding’, first recorded at the beginning of the 19th century, may have been inspired by the notion of being told off by a <i>bigwig </i>[18], etymologically a ‘highranking bewigged dignitary’.</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/periwig" target="_blank">periwig</a>, <a href="/etym/s/peruke" target="_blank">peruke</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/wig">wig (n.)</a></dt><dd>1670s, shortened form of <a href="/etym/s/periwig" target="_blank">periwig</a>. Meaning "person who wears a wig (professionally)" is from 1828.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/wig">wig (v.)</a></dt><dd>1826, "supply with a wig," from <a href="/etym/s/wig" target="_blank">wig</a> (n.). The meaning "to behave hysterically" (usually with <span class="foreign">out</span>) is attested from 1955, perhaps from notion in <span class="foreign">flip one's wig</span>. Compare <span class="foreign">dash my wig!</span>, a former mild imprecation (1797), also <span class="foreign">wigs on the green</span> (1856), Irish colloquial for "a fight or rumble" (because wigs are likely to get detached from owners in such an event). The verb also had a colloquial sense of "scold severely," attested by 1829, perhaps related to these. Related: <span class="foreign">Wigged</span>; <span class="foreign">wigging</span>.</dd></dl></div></div>

            return false;
