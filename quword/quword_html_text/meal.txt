                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/meal">meal</a> 餐，一顿饭</span><p>来自古英语mael,固定时间，吃饭点，来自Proto-Germanic*maela,来自PIE*me,测量，词源同meter,measure.词义由测量引申为固定时间，最后引申词义用餐时间，一顿饭。词义演变比较同源词time,tide,deal.</p><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/meal">meal</a> 谷物粗粉</span><p>来自古英语melu,面粉，谷粉，来自PIE*mel,软的，磨，词源同mill,mallet.</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/meal">meal</a></dt><dd><b>meal: </b><span>[OE] <i>Meal </i>‘repast’ and <i>meal </i>‘flour’ are two distinct words. The former originally meant ‘measure’: it goes back via prehistoric Germanic *<i>mǣlaz </i>(source of German <i>mal </i>‘time, occasion’ and <i>mahl </i>‘meal’, Dutch <i>maal </i>‘time, meal’, and Swedish <i>mål </i>‘meal’) to the Indo-European base *<i>me</i>- ‘measure’, which is also the ancestor of English <i>measure</i>.<br><br>The semantic progression from ‘measure’ (which died out for <i>meal </i>in the Middle English period, but survives in the compound <i>piecemeal </i>[13], etymologically ‘measured piece by piece’) to ‘repast’ was via ‘measured or fixed time’ (hence the meaning ‘time, occasion’ in many of the related Germanic forms) and ‘time fixed for eating’. <i>Meal </i>‘flour’ (as in <i>oatmeal</i>) goes back ultimately to Indo-European *<i>mel</i>-, *<i>mol</i>-, *<i>ml</i>- ‘grind’, source of a wide range of other English words from <i>mild </i>and <i>mill </i>to <i>molar </i>and <i>mould</i>.<br><br>From it was descended West and North Germanic *<i>melwam</i>, which has differentiated to German <i>mehl</i>, Dutch <i>meel</i>, Swedish <i>mjōl</i>, Danish <i>mel</i>, and English <i>meal</i>. It has been speculated that <i>mellow </i>[15] may have originated in the use of Old English <i>melu </i>‘meal’ as an adjective, in the sense ‘soft and rich like flour’.</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/measure" target="_blank">measure</a>, <a href="/etym/s/piecemeal" target="_blank">piecemeal</a>; <a href="/etym/s/mellow" target="_blank">mellow</a>, <a href="/etym/s/mild" target="_blank">mild</a>, <a href="/etym/s/mill" target="_blank">mill</a>, <a href="/etym/s/molar" target="_blank">molar</a>, <a href="/etym/s/mould" target="_blank">mould</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/meal">meal (n.1)</a></dt><dd>"food; time for eating," c. 1200 (perhaps late Old English), <span class="foreign">mel</span> "appointed time for eating," also "a meal, feast," from Old English <span class="foreign">mæl</span> "fixed time, occasion, a meal," from Proto-Germanic <span class="foreign">*mæla-</span> (cognates: Old Frisian <span class="foreign">mel</span> "time;" Middle Dutch <span class="foreign">mael</span>, Dutch <span class="foreign">maal</span> "time, meal;" Old Norse <span class="foreign">mal</span> "measure, time, meal;" German <span class="foreign">Mal</span> "time," <span class="foreign">Mahl</span> "meal;" Gothic <span class="foreign">mel</span> "time, hour"), from PIE <span class="foreign">*me-lo-</span>, from root <span class="foreign">*me-</span> "to measure" (see <a href="/etym/s/meter" target="_blank">meter</a> (n.2)). Original sense of "time" is preserved in <a href="/etym/s/piecemeal" target="_blank">piecemeal</a>. <span class="foreign">Meals-on-wheels</span> attested from 1961. <span class="foreign">Meal ticket</span> first attested 1870 in literal sense of "ticket of admission to a dining hall;" figurative sense of "source of income or livelihood" is from 1899.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/meal">meal (n.2)</a></dt><dd>"edible ground grain," Old English <span class="foreign">melu</span> "meal, flour," from Proto-Germanic <span class="foreign">*melwan</span> "grind" (cognates: Old Frisian <span class="foreign">mele</span> "meal," Old Saxon <span class="foreign">melo</span>, Middle Dutch <span class="foreign">mele</span>, Dutch <span class="foreign">meel</span>, Old High German <span class="foreign">melo</span>, German <span class="foreign">Mehl</span>, Old Norse <span class="foreign">mjöl</span> "meal;" Old Saxon, Old High German, Gothic <span class="foreign">malan</span> "to grind," German <span class="foreign">mahlen</span>), from PIE root <span class="foreign">*mel-</span> (1) "soft" (see <a href="/etym/s/mallet" target="_blank">mallet</a>).</dd></dl></div></div>

            <div class="row" id="yd-liju">
