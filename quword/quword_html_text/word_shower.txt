                        <div class="page-header"><h3>记忆方法</h3></div>
            <div class="row" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;">shower,n,阵雨，音“潲哇”。解：这个英语单词真妙，直接出了阵雨的下法――潲哇。绝对地说，直下的雨是没有的，无论是阵雨还是非阵雨。</div>

                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/shower">shower</a> 阵雨，阵雪，淋浴，沐浴</span><p>来自古英语 scur,阵风，阵雪，暴风雪，来自 Proto-Germanic*skuro,风暴，暴风雪，来自 PIE*skewer,北方，冷风，词源同拉丁语 caurus,东方风。拼写比较 tower,turret.引申词义淋浴， 沐浴。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/shower">shower</a></dt><dd><b>shower: </b><span>[OE] <i>Shower </i>comes from a prehistoric West Germanic *<i>skūra</i>, which also produced German <i>schauer </i>and Dutch <i>schoer</i>. Its ultimate ancestry is uncertain.</span><br></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/shower">shower (n.1)</a></dt><dd>Old English <span class="foreign">scur</span> "a short fall of rain, storm, tempest; fall of missiles or blows; struggle, commotion; breeze," from Proto-Germanic <span class="foreign">*skuraz</span> (cognates: Old Norse <span class="foreign">skur</span>, Old Saxon and Old Frisian <span class="foreign">scur</span> "fit of illness;" Old High German <span class="foreign">scur</span>, German <span class="foreign">Schauer</span> "shower, downpour;" Gothic <span class="foreign">skura</span>, in <span class="foreign">skura windis</span> "windstorm"), from PIE root <span class="foreign">*kew-(e)ro-</span> "north, north wind" (cognates: Latin <span class="foreign">caurus</span> "northwest wind;" Old Church Slavonic <span class="foreign">severu</span> "north, north wind;" Lithuanian <span class="foreign">šiaurus</span> "raging, stormy," <span class="foreign">šiaurys</span> "north wind," <span class="foreign">šiaure</span> "north").&#13; <br /><br />&#13; Of blood, tears, etc., from c. 1400. Of meteors from 1835. Sense of "bath in which water is poured from above" first recorded 1851 (short for <span class="foreign">shower-bath</span>, itself attested from 1803). Meaning "large number of gifts bestowed on a bride" (1904, American English colloquial) later was extended to the party at which it happens (1926). <span class="foreign">Shower curtain</span> attested from 1914.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/shower">shower (v.)</a></dt><dd>1570s, "come down in showers;" 1580s, "to discharge a shower," from <a href="/etym/s/shower" target="_blank">shower</a> (n.1). Intransitive sense from 1930. Related: <span class="foreign">Showered</span>; <span class="foreign">showering</span>.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/shower">shower (n.2)</a></dt><dd>"one who shows," Old English <span class="foreign">sceawere</span> "spectator, watchtower, mirror," agent noun; see <a href="/etym/s/show" target="_blank">show</a> (v.).</dd></dl></div></div>
