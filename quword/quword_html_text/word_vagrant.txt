                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/vagrant">vagrant</a> 流浪者</span><p>通常认为来自古法语walcrer,漫游者，流浪者，词源同walk,拼写受拉丁语vagus影响。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/vagrant">vagrant (n.)</a></dt><dd>mid-15c., "person who lacks regular employment, one without fixed abode, a tramp," probably from Anglo-French <span class="foreign">vageraunt</span>, also <span class="foreign">wacrant</span>, <span class="foreign">walcrant</span>, which is said in many sources to be a noun use of the past participle of Old French <span class="foreign">walcrer</span> "to wander," from Frankish (Germanic) <span class="foreign">*walken</span>, from the same source as Old Norse <span class="foreign">valka</span> "wander" and English <a href="/etym/s/walk" target="_blank">walk</a> (v.).&#13; <br /><br />&#13; Under this theory the word was influenced by Old French <span class="foreign">vagant</span>, <span class="foreign">vagaunt</span> "wandering," from Latin <span class="foreign">vagantem</span> (nominative <span class="foreign">vagans</span>), past participle of <span class="foreign">vagari</span> "to wander, stroll about" (see <a href="/etym/s/vagary" target="_blank">vagary</a>). But on another theory the Anglo-French word ultimately is from Old French <span class="foreign">vagant</span>, with an intrusive <span class="foreign">-r-</span>. Middle English also had <span class="foreign">vagaunt</span> "wandering, without fixed abode" (late 14c.), from Old French <span class="foreign">vagant</span>.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/vagrant">vagrant (adj.)</a></dt><dd>early 15c., from Anglo-French <span class="foreign">vagarant</span>, <span class="foreign">waucrant</span>, and sharing with it the history to be found under <a href="/etym/s/vagrant" target="_blank">vagrant</a> (n.). Dogberry's corruption <span class="foreign">vagrom</span> ("Much Ado about Nothing") persisted through 19c. in learned jocularity.</dd></dl></div></div>
                                    <div class="page-header"><h3><a href="/liju/w/vagrant">权威例句</a></h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-liju">
                 <dl><dt>1. A <b>vagrant</b> is everywhere at home. </dt> <dd>流浪者四海为家.</dd> <p>来自《简明英汉词典》</p> </dl><dl><dt>2. In the old society, owing to cruel exploitation and succesive years of tangled warfare among warloads, the labouring people led a <b>vagrant</b> life. </dt> <dd>在旧社会, 残酷的剥削和连年不断的军阀混战, 使劳动人民过着颠沛流离的生活.</dd> <p>来自《现代汉英综合大词典》</p> </dl><dl><dt>3. He lived on the street as a <b>vagrant</b>. </dt> <dd>他以在大街上乞讨为生。</dd> <p>来自辞典例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>4. We met a band of <b>vagrant</b> beggars there. </dt> <dd>我们在那里遇到了一伙流浪的乞丐.</dd> <p>来自辞典例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>5. The degree of contrast will also be diminished by the presence of some <b>vagrant</b> ordinary light. </dt> <dd>反差度还将因某些杂散自然光的存在而降低.</dd> <p>来自辞典例句</p> </dl>                 <dl><p>[ <a href="/liju/w/vagrant">vagrant 造句</a> ]</p></dl>
