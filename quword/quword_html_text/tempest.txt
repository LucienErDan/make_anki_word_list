                    <div class="row"><h3 id="yd-word">tempest</h3></div>
                                        <div class="row" id="yd-word-pron">英 ['tempɪst]
美 ['tɛmpɪst]</div>
                                        <div class="row" id="yd-word-meaning"><ul>
<li>n. 暴风雨；骚动；动乱</li>
<li>vt. 使狂怒；扰乱，使激动</li>
<li>vi. 小题大作；起大风暴</li>
<li>n. (Tempest)人名；(英)坦皮斯特</li>
</ul></div>

                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/tempest">tempest</a> 暴风雨，暴风雪</span><p>来自拉丁语 tempestas,风暴，暴风雨，暴风雪，来自 tempus,时间，季节，词源同 temporal. 词义由一段时间引申为特殊的时间，恶劣天气，最后指暴风雨雪天气，即代表性的天气特征。 词义演变比较 meteor,meteorology.</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/tempest">tempest</a></dt><dd><b>tempest: </b><span>[13] Latin <i>tempestās </i>started off meaning nothing more alarming than ‘period of time’ (it was a derivative of <i>tempus </i>‘time’, source of English <i>temporary</i>). Gradually, however, it progressed via ‘weather’ to ‘bad weather, storm’. <i>Tempus </i>moved in to take its place in the neutral sense ‘weather’, and provides the word for ‘weather’ in modern French (<i>temps</i>), Italian (<i>tempo</i>), Spanish (<i>tiempo</i>), and Romanian (<i>timp</i>). Other languages whose word for ‘weather’ comes from a term originally denoting ‘time’ include Russian (<i>pogoda</i>), Polish (<i>czas</i>), Czech (<i>počasí</i>), Latvian (<i>laiks</i>), and Breton (<i>amzer</i>).</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/temporary" target="_blank">temporary</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/tempest">tempest (n.)</a></dt><dd>"violent storm," late 13c., from Old French <span class="foreign">tempeste</span> "storm; commotion, battle; epidemic, plague" (11c.), from Vulgar Latin <span class="foreign">*tempesta</span>, from Latin <span class="foreign">tempestas</span> "a storm; weather, season, time, point in time, season, period," also "commotion, disturbance," related to <span class="foreign">tempus</span> "time, season" (see <a href="/etym/s/temporal" target="_blank">temporal</a>).&#13; <br /><br />&#13; Sense evolution is from "period of time" to "period of weather," to "bad weather" to "storm." Words for "weather" originally were words for "time" in languages from Russia to Brittany. Figurative sense of "violent commotion" in English is recorded from early 14c. <span class="foreign">Tempest in a teapot</span> attested from 1818; the image in other forms is older, such as <span class="foreign">storm in a creambowl</span> (1670s).</dd></dl></div></div>
                                    <div class="page-header"><h3><a href="/liju/w/tempest">权威例句</a></h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-liju">
                 <dl><dt>1. I hadn't foreseen the <b>tempest</b> my request would cause. </dt> <dd>我没有料到我的请求会掀起这么大一场风波。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>2. The takeover provoked a <b>tempest</b> of criticism. </dt> <dd>这次收购引发了潮水般的批评。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>3. The sailors took in sail when the <b>tempest</b> was approaching. </dt> <dd>暴风雨来临之际,水手们将帐篷放下.</dd> <p>来自《简明英汉词典》</p> </dl><dl><dt>4. He won a <b>tempest</b> of applause when he ended his speech. </dt> <dd>演讲结束时,他博得暴风雨般的掌声.</dd> <p>来自《简明英汉词典》</p> </dl><dl><dt>5. No <b>tempest</b> is capable of shattering his firm determination. </dt> <dd>任何惊涛骇浪都不能动摇他坚如磐石的决心.</dd> <p>来自《现代汉英综合大词典》</p> </dl>                 <dl><p>[ <a href="/liju/w/tempest">tempest 造句</a> ]</p></dl>

    document.getElementById('yd-search').onsubmit = function() {

        if (word == '') {
            return false;
        } else if (word.search(/^[\w \-]+$/) == 0) {
            top.location.href = '/w/' + encodeURIComponent(word.replace(/ /g, '_'));
            return false;

                var char_code = match.charCodeAt(0) - 0xfee0;
                return String.fromCharCode(char_code);
            });
        }
