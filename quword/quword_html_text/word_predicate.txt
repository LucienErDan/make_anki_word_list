                        <div class="page-header"><h3>记忆方法</h3></div>
            <div class="row" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;">1. predicate <===> proclaim. 构词逻辑差不多。<br />
2. 什么是谓语？谓语就是对主语的断言，或者谓语就是主语所宣布、声明的话语。<br />
3. pre- + dic- + -ate.<br />
4. => "assert, proclaim, declare publicly".<br />
5. => "that which is said of the subject".</div>

                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/predicate">predicate</a> 表明，断言，基于</span><p>来自拉丁语praedicare,预测，断言，声称，来自prae,在前，早于，dicare,说，声称，词源同diction.并引申诸相关词义。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/predicate">predicate</a></dt><dd><b>predicate: </b><span>see <a href="/etym/s/indicate" target="_blank">indicate</a></span><br></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/predicate">predicate (n.)</a></dt><dd>mid-15c., a term in logic, from Middle French <span class="foreign">predicat</span> and directly from Medieval Latin <span class="foreign">predicatum</span>, from Latin <span class="foreign">praedicatum</span> "that which is said of the subject," noun use of neuter past participle of <span class="foreign">praedicare</span> "assert, proclaim, declare publicly," from <span class="foreign">prae-</span> "forth, before" (see <a href="/etym/s/pre-" target="_blank">pre-</a>) + <span class="foreign">dicare</span> "proclaim," from stem of <span class="foreign">dicere</span> "to speak, to say" (see <a href="/etym/s/diction" target="_blank">diction</a>). Grammatical sense is from 1630s. Related: <span class="foreign">Predicative</span>; <span class="foreign">predicator</span>; <span class="foreign">predicatory</span>.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/predicate">predicate (adj.)</a></dt><dd>1887, from Latin <span class="foreign">praedicatus</span>, past participle of <span class="foreign">praedicare</span> "proclaim, announce" (see <a href="/etym/s/predicate" target="_blank">predicate</a> (n.)).</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/predicate">predicate (v.)</a></dt><dd>1550s, back formation from <span class="foreign">predication</span>, or else from Latin <span class="foreign">praedicatus</span>, past participle of <span class="foreign">praedicare</span> "proclaim, announce" (see <a href="/etym/s/predicate" target="_blank">predicate</a> (n.)). Related: <span class="foreign">Predicated</span>; <span class="foreign">predicating</span>. Phrase <span class="foreign">predicated on</span> "founded on, based on," is American English, first recorded 1766.</dd></dl></div></div>

Most religions <b>predicate</b> life after death. </dt> <dd>大多数的宗教宣称死后仍有生命.</dd> <p>来自《现代英汉综合大词典》</p> </dl><dl><dt>3. In general, a grammatical clause contains a subject and a <b>predicate</b>. </dt> <dd>一般说来, 一个合乎语法的从句包含有主语与谓语.</dd> <p>来自辞典例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>5.
