                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/junk">junk</a> 垃圾</span><p>来自中古英语junke,船上的旧缆绳或绳索，可能来自古法语junc,来自拉丁语juncus,芦苇，灯芯草，词源同junket.后引申词义船上的垃圾，废弃物。</p><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/junk">junk</a> 中国式帆船</span><p>来自葡萄牙语junco,来自马来语jong,船，大船，最终来自汉语chuan,船。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/junk">junk (n.1)</a></dt><dd>"worthless stuff," mid-14c., <span class="foreign">junke</span> "old cable or rope" (nautical), of uncertain origin, perhaps from Old French <span class="foreign">junc</span> "rush, reed," also used figuratively as a type of something of little value, from Latin <span class="foreign">iuncus</span> "rush, reed" (but OED finds "no evidence of connexion"). Nautical use extended to "old refuse from boats and ships" (1842), then to "old or discarded articles of any kind" (1884). <span class="foreign">Junk food</span> is from 1971; <span class="foreign">junk art</span> is from 1966; <span class="foreign">junk mail</span> first attested 1954.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/junk">junk (n.2)</a></dt><dd>"Chinese sailing ship," 1610s, from Portuguese <span class="foreign">junco</span>, from Malay <span class="foreign">jong</span> "ship, large boat" (13c.), probably from Javanese <span class="foreign">djong</span>.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/junk">junk (v.)</a></dt><dd>1803, "to cut off in lumps," from <a href="/etym/s/junk" target="_blank">junk</a> (n.1). The meaning "to throw away as trash, to scrap" is from 1908. Related: <span class="foreign">Junked</span>; <span class="foreign">junking</span>.&#13; <blockquote>&#13; New settlers (who should always be here as early in the spring as possible) begin to cut down the wood where they intend to erect their first house. As the trees are cut the branches are to be lopped off, and the trunks cut into lengths of 12 or 14 feet. This operation they call junking them; if they are not junked before fire is applied, they are much worse to junk afterwards. [letter dated Charlotte Town, Nov. 29, 1820, in "A Series of Letters Descriptive of Prince Edward Island," 1822]&#13; </blockquote></dd></dl></div></div>

There was no mail except the usual <b>junk</b> addressed to the occupier. </dt> <dd>除了寄给业主的常见垃圾信件之外，再没有其他邮件了。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>4. Rose finds her furniture in <b>junk</b> shops. </dt> <dd>罗丝从旧货店买来了家具。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>5. I've cleared out all that old <b>junk</b> in the attic. </dt> <dd>我把阁楼里所有的废旧杂物都清除干净了。</dd> <p>来自《权威词典》</p> </dl>                 <dl><p>[ <a href="/liju/w/junk">junk 造句</a> ]</p></dl>

    document.getElementById('yd-search').onsubmit = function() {

        if (word == '') {
            return false;
        } else if (word.search(/^[\w \-]+$/) == 0) {
            top.location.href = '/w/' + encodeURIComponent(word.replace(/ /g, '_'));
            return false;

                return String.fromCharCode(char_code);

        }
