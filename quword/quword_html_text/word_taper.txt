                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/taper">taper</a> 灯芯，细蜡烛，锥形物，逐渐变细</span><p>来自古英语 taper,灯芯，蜡烛，异化自拉丁语 papyrus,纸莎草，词源同 paper.因过去用纸莎草 芯做灯芯而引申该词义，后进一步引申词义锥形物，逐渐变细等。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/taper">taper</a></dt><dd><b>taper: </b><span>[OE] <i>Taper </i>is ultimately the same word as <i>paper</i>. Both go bach to Latin <i>papyrus </i>‘papyrus’. This was used among other things for a ‘candlewick made from papyrus’, and hence for a ‘candle’. It seems to have been borrowed in this sense into Old English as *<i>papur</i>, and by a process known as dissimilation (in which one of a pair of similar speech sounds is changed, so as to break up the pair) it became <i>tapur</i>. The verb <i>taper </i>‘become narrower’, which emerged in the 16th century, is an allusion to the shape of the candle.</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/paper" target="_blank">paper</a>, <a href="/etym/s/papyrus" target="_blank">papyrus</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/taper">taper (n.)</a></dt><dd>Old English <span class="foreign">tapur</span>, <span class="foreign">taper</span> "candle, lamp-wick," not found outside English, possibly a dissimilated borrowing from Latin <span class="foreign">papyrus</span> (see <a href="/etym/s/papyrus" target="_blank">papyrus</a>), which was used in Medieval Latin and some Romance languages for "wick of a candle" (such as Italian <span class="foreign">papijo</span> "wick"), because these often were made from the pith of papyrus. Compare also German <span class="foreign">kerze</span> "candle," from Old High German <span class="foreign">charza</span>, from Latin <span class="foreign">charta</span>, from Greek <span class="foreign">khartes</span> "papyrus, roll made from papyrus, wick made from pith of papyrus."</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/taper">taper (v.)</a></dt><dd>1580s, "shoot up like a flame or spire," via an obsolete adjective <span class="foreign">taper</span>, from <a href="/etym/s/taper" target="_blank">taper</a> (n.), on the notion of the converging form of the flame of a candle. Sense of "become slender, gradually grow less in size, force, etc." first recorded c. 1600. Transitive sense from 1670s. Related: <span class="foreign">Tapered</span>; <span class="foreign">tapering</span>.</dd></dl></div></div>
