                        <div class="page-header"><h3>记忆方法</h3></div>
            <div class="row" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;">1. 音译“幽默”。它是外来词，由英文音译而来的。而最初将此词移入中国的，则是林语堂。这是一个音、意两译的词，其表达恰到好处。<br />
2. 字母h很多时候是不发音的，这里在音译的时候，就把首字母h当不发音处理。</div>

                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/humor">humor</a> 体液，心情，幽默，诙谐</span><p>来自拉丁语umor,体液，来自umere,湿的，词源同humid.在古代指人体的四种体液，即blood,phlegm,choler,black bile,血液，黏液，胆汁液，黑胆汁液，这四种体液的组合和多寡决定人的性格，心境，情绪，同时，这四种体液的组合虽然基本固定，但也会发生变化，从而人的心情也随之发生变化，引申词义兴致，心血来潮，纵容等词义，并同时引申词义逗笑的能力，即幽默感。20世纪初学者严复将该词翻译为幽默并广为流传，需说明的是，当时h是不发音的。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/humor">humor (n.)</a></dt><dd>mid-14c., "fluid or juice of an animal or plant," from Old North French <span class="foreign">humour</span> (Old French <span class="foreign">humor</span>; Modern French <span class="foreign">humeur</span>), from Latin <span class="foreign">umor</span> "body fluid" (also <span class="foreign">humor</span>, by false association with <span class="foreign">humus</span> "earth"); related to <span class="foreign">umere</span> "be wet, moist," and to <span class="foreign">uvescere</span> "become wet," from PIE <span class="foreign">*wegw-</span> "wet."&#13; <br /><br />&#13; In ancient and medieval physiology, "any of the four body fluids" (blood, phlegm, choler, and melancholy or black bile) whose relative proportions were thought to determine state of mind. This led to a sense of "mood, temporary state of mind" (first recorded 1520s); the sense of "amusing quality, funniness" is first recorded 1680s, probably via sense of "whim, caprice" (1560s), which also produced the verb sense of "indulge," first attested 1580s.
