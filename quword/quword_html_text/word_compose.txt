            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/compose">compose</a></dt><dd><b>compose: </b><span>[15] Etymologically, <i>compose </i>means simply ‘put together’; it comes, via Old French <i>composer</i>, from <i>compos</i>-, the perfect stem of Latin <i>compōnere</i>, a compound verb formed from the prefix <i>com</i>- ‘with’ and <i>pōnere </i>‘place, put’, source of English <i>position</i>. Amongst its many descendants and derivatives are <i>compound</i>, <i>component </i>[17] (from the Latin present participle <i>compōnent</i>-), <i>composite </i>[16] (from the Latin past participle <i>compositus</i>), and <i>compost </i>[14] (which originally meant ‘stewed fruit’, like the later-borrowed <i>compote </i>[17]).</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/component" target="_blank">component</a>, <a href="/etym/s/composite" target="_blank">composite</a>, <a href="/etym/s/compost" target="_blank">compost</a>, <a href="/etym/s/compote" target="_blank">compote</a>, <a href="/etym/s/compound" target="_blank">compound</a>, <a href="/etym/s/position" target="_blank">position</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/compose">compose (v.)</a></dt><dd>c. 1400, <span class="foreign">compousen</span>, from Old French <span class="foreign">composer</span> "put together, arrange, write" a work (12c.), from <span class="foreign">com-</span> "with" (see <a href="/etym/s/com-" target="_blank">com-</a>) + <span class="foreign">poser</span> "to place," from Late Latin <span class="foreign">pausare</span> "to cease, lay down" (see <a href="/etym/s/pause" target="_blank">pause</a> (n.)). Meaning influenced in Old French by <span class="foreign">componere</span> (see <a href="/etym/s/composite" target="_blank">composite</a>; also see <a href="/etym/s/pose" target="_blank">pose</a> (v.)). Musical sense is from 1590s. Related: <a href="/etym/s/Composed" target="_blank">Composed</a>; <span class="foreign">composing</span>.</dd></dl></div></div>
