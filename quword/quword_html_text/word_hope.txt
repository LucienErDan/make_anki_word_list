                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/hope">hope</a> 希望</span><p>来自古英语hopa,希望，期望，来自Proto-Germanic*hupo,希望，来自PIE*kwep,蒸发，蒸腾，沸腾，词源同vapor,Cupid,cupidity.由沸腾引申词义希冀，渴求，希望。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/hope">hope</a></dt><dd><b>hope: </b><span>[OE] The origins of the word <i>hope </i>are obscure. It appears to have started life among the Low German dialects of northern Germany (whence English <i>hope </i>and Dutch <i>hoop</i>), and later spread to Scandinavia (giving Swedish <i>hopp </i>and Danish <i>haab</i>) and High German (modern German has the verb <i>hoffen </i>and the derived noun <i>hoffnung </i>‘hope’). Where did the original Low German forms come from, though? A suggestion that has found some favour is that the word is related to <i>hop</i>, and that it started from the notion of ‘jumping to safety’. The theory goes that the ‘place of refuge’ thus reached gives one ‘hope’, but it has an air of desperation.</span><br></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/hope">hope (v.)</a></dt><dd>Old English <span class="foreign">hopian</span> "wish, expect, look forward (to something)," of unknown origin, a general North Sea Germanic word (cognates: Old Frisian <span class="foreign">hopia</span>, Middle Low German, Middle Dutch, Dutch <span class="foreign">hopen</span>; Middle High German <span class="foreign">hoffen</span> "to hope," borrowed from Low German). Some suggest a connection with <a href="/etym/s/hop" target="_blank">hop</a> (v.) on the notion of "leaping in expectation" [Klein]. Related: <span class="foreign">Hoped</span>; <span class="foreign">hoping</span>.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/hope">hope (n.)</a></dt><dd>Old English <span class="foreign">hopa</span>, from <a href="/etym/s/hope" target="_blank">hope</a> (v.). Compare Old Frisian and Middle Dutch <span class="foreign">hope</span>, Dutch <span class="foreign">hoop</span>, all from their respective verbs.</dd></dl></div></div>

        if (word == '') {
            return false;
        } else if (word.search(/^[\w \-]+$/) == 0) {
            top.location.href = '/w/' + encodeURIComponent(word.replace(/ /g, '_'));
            return false;

                var char_code = match.charCodeAt(0) - 0xfee0;
                return String.fromCharCode(char_code);
            });
        }
