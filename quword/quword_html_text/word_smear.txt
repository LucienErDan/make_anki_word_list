The Swedish and Danish words for ‘butter’, <i>smör </i>and <i>smør</i>, come from the same source (the former is the first element in the compound <i>smörgåsbord </i>‘opensandwich table’, literally ‘butter goose table’, from which English gets <i>smorgasbord </i>[19]). Related: <span class="foreign">Smeared</span>; <span class="foreign">smearing</span>. <span class="foreign">Smear-word</span>, one used regardless of its literal meaning but invested with invective, is from 1938.</dd></dl></div></div>
