                        <div class="page-header"><h3>记忆方法</h3></div>
            <div class="row" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;">1. hare (grey animal): 取名自它的颜色，野兔皮毛基本上都是呈灰色、灰白色的。<br />
2. herring (grey fish): 取名自它的颜色，鲱鱼的颜色都是灰色、灰白色的。<br />
3. hare, herring => hoar, hoary.<br />
4. perhaps related to or influenced in form by Old English har "gray, hoar," from the color, or to Old High German heri "host, multitude" from its large schools.<br />
5. 鲱鱼战役（Battle of Herrings），又称鲱鱼之战是1429年2月12日发生在法国奥尔良北部的军事行动，此时正奥尔良之围期间。</div>

                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/herring">herring</a> 鲱鱼</span><p>来自古英语har,灰白色，词源同hoar.因这种鱼灰白色的颜色而得名。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/herring">herring</a></dt><dd><b>herring: </b><span>[OE] Just as the hare is probably the ‘grey animal’, so the herring could well be the ‘grey fish’. Old English <i>hǣring </i>goes back to a prehistoric West Germanic *<i>khēringgaz</i>, in which the first syllable could represent the ancestor of English <i>hoar </i>‘silvery-grey’ – the colour of the herring. French <i>hareng </i>comes from a variant of the West Germanic word.</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/hare" target="_blank">hare</a>, <a href="/etym/s/hoar" target="_blank">hoar</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/herring">herring (n.)</a></dt><dd>Old English <span class="foreign">hering</span> (Anglian), <span class="foreign">hæring</span> (West Saxon), from West Germanic <span class="foreign">*heringgaz</span> (cognates: Old Frisian <span class="foreign">hereng</span>, Middle Dutch <span class="foreign">herinc</span>, German <span class="foreign">Hering</span>), of unknown origin, perhaps related to or influenced in form by Old English <span class="foreign">har</span> "gray, hoar," from the color, or to Old High German <span class="foreign">heri</span> "host, multitude" from its large schools.&#13; <br /><br />&#13; French <span class="foreign">hareng</span>, Italian <span class="foreign">aringa</span> are from Germanic. The <span class="foreign">Battle of the Herrings</span> (French <span class="foreign">bataille des harengs</span>) is the popular name for the battle at Rouvrai, Feb. 12, 1492, fought in defense of a convoy of provisions, mostly herrings and other "lenten stuffe."</dd></dl></div></div>
