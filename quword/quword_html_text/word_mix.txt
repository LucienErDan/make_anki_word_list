                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/mix">mix</a></dt><dd><b>mix: </b><span>[15] English originally acquired this word in the form <i>mixt </i>or <i>mixed</i>, a past participial adjective, and did not coin the new verb <i>mix </i>from it until the 16th century. <i>Mixt </i>came via Old French from Latin <i>mixtus</i>, the past participle of the verb <i>miscēre </i>‘mix’. Derivatives of <i>miscēre </i>to have reached English include <i>miscellaneous </i>[17] and <i>promiscuous </i>[17], and its Vulgar Latin descendant *<i>misculāre </i>‘mix up’ has given English <i>meddle </i>[14], <i>medley </i>[14], and <i>mêlée </i>[17]. <i>Miscegenation </i>[19] was coined in the USA around 1863 from <i>miscēre </i>and Latin <i>genus </i>‘race’.</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/meddle" target="_blank">meddle</a>, <a href="/etym/s/medley" target="_blank">medley</a>, <a href="/etym/s/miscellaneous" target="_blank">miscellaneous</a>, <a href="/etym/s/mustang" target="_blank">mustang</a>, <a href="/etym/s/promiscuous" target="_blank">promiscuous</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/mix">mix (v.)</a></dt><dd>1530s, back-formation from Middle English <span class="foreign">myxte</span> (early 15c.) "composed of more than one element, of mixed nature," from Anglo-French <span class="foreign">mixte</span>, from Latin <span class="foreign">mixtus</span>, past participle of <span class="foreign">miscere</span> "to mix, mingle, blend; fraternize with; throw into confusion," from PIE <span class="foreign">*meik-</span> "to mix" (cognates: Sanskrit <span class="foreign">misrah</span> "mixed," Greek <span class="foreign">misgein</span>, <span class="foreign">mignynai</span> "to mix, mix up, mingle; to join, bring together; join (battle); make acquainted with," Old Church Slavonic <span class="foreign">mešo</span>, <span class="foreign">mesiti</span> "to mix," Russian <span class="foreign">meshat</span>, Lithuanian <span class="foreign">maišau</span> "to mix, mingle," Welsh <span class="foreign">mysgu</span>). Also borrowed in Old English as <span class="foreign">miscian</span>. Related: <a href="/etym/s/Mixed" target="_blank">Mixed</a>; <span class="foreign">mixing</span>.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/mix">mix (n.)</a></dt><dd>1580s, "act of mixing," from <a href="/etym/s/mix" target="_blank">mix</a> (v.).</dd></dl></div></div>
