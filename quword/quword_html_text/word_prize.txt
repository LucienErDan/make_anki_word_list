            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/prize">prize</a> 奖品，奖赏</span><p>来自古法语pris,价格，价值，奖品，词源同price,praise.</p><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/prize">prize</a> 撬开</span><p>来自古法语prise,抓住，来自（缩写自）拉丁语prehendere,抓住，词源同apprise,comprehend. 引申词义撬开，强力扒开。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/prize">prize</a></dt><dd><b>prize: </b><span>English has four words <i>prize</i>. The one meaning ‘reward’ [16] is essentially the same word as <i>price</i>. This was originally <i>pris</i>, mirroring its immediate Old French ancestor <i>pris</i>. It became <i>prise</i>, to indicate the length of its vowel <i>i</i>, and in the 16th century this differentiated into <i>price </i>for ‘amount to pay’ and <i>prize </i>for ‘reward’. (Modern French <i>prix </i>has given English <i>grand prix </i>[19], literally ‘great prize’, first used for a ‘car race’ in 1908.) <i>Prize </i>‘esteem’ [14] was based on <i>pris</i>-, the stem of Old French <i>preisier </i>‘praise’ (source of English <i>praise</i>). <i>Prize </i>‘something captured in war’ [14] comes via Old French <i>prise </i>‘capture, seizure, booty’ from Vulgar Latin *<i>prēsa </i>or *<i>prēnsa </i>‘something seized’.<br><br>This was a noun use of the past participle of *<i>prēndere </i>‘seize’, a contraction of classical Latin <i>praehendere </i>(from which English gets <i>prehensile</i>, <i>prison</i>, etc). Another sense of Old French <i>prise </i>was ‘grasp’. English borrowed this in the 14th century as <i>prize </i>‘lever’, which in due course was turned into modern English’s fourth <i>prize</i>, the verb <i>prize</i>, or <i>prise</i>, ‘lever’ [17]. <i>Pry </i>‘lever’ [19] is an alteration of <i>prize</i>, based on the misapprehension that it is a third-person singular present form (*<i>pries</i>).</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/grand_prix" target="_blank">grand prix</a>, <a href="/etym/s/price" target="_blank">price</a>; <a href="/etym/s/praise" target="_blank">praise</a>; <a href="/etym/s/comprehensive" target="_blank">comprehensive</a>, <a href="/etym/s/prison" target="_blank">prison</a>, <a href="/etym/s/reprehensible" target="_blank">reprehensible</a>; <a href="/etym/s/pry" target="_blank">pry</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/prize">prize (n.1)</a></dt><dd>"reward," <span class="foreign">prise</span> (c. 1300 in this sense), from Old French <span class="foreign">pris</span> "price, value, worth; reward" (see <a href="/etym/s/price" target="_blank">price</a> (n.)). As an adjective, "worthy of a prize," from 1803. The spelling with <span class="foreign">-z-</span> is from late 16c. <span class="foreign">Prize-fighter</span> is from 1703; <span class="foreign">prize-fight</span> from 1730 (<span class="foreign">prize-fighter</span> from 1785).</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/prize">prize (n.2)</a></dt><dd>"something taken by force," mid-13c., <span class="foreign">prise</span> "a taking, holding," from Old French <span class="foreign">prise</span> "a taking, seizing, holding," noun use of fem. past participle of <span class="foreign">prendre</span> "to take, seize," from Latin <span class="foreign">prendere</span>, contraction of <span class="foreign">prehendere</span> "lay hold of, grasp, seize, catch" (see <a href="/etym/s/prehensile" target="_blank">prehensile</a>). Especially of ships captured at sea (1510s). The spelling with <span class="foreign">-z-</span> is from late 16c.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/prize">prize (v.)</a></dt><dd>"to estimate," 1580s, alteration of Middle English <span class="foreign">prisen</span> "to prize, value" (late 14c.), from stem of Old French <span class="foreign">preisier</span> "to praise" (see <a href="/etym/s/praise" target="_blank">praise</a> (v.)). Related: <span class="foreign">Prized</span>; <span class="foreign">prizing</span>.</dd></dl></div></div>
