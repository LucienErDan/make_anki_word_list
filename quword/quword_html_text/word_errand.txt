                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/errand">errand</a> 差使</span><p>来自PIE*ei, 走，离开。其现在分词ion, 过去分词it, 词源同exit, itinerary, 该词来自其拉丁语现在主动不完全格ire.</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/errand">errand</a></dt><dd><b>errand: </b><span>[OE] Despite the passing similarity, <i>errand </i>has no etymological connection with <i>err </i>and <i>error</i>. It comes from a prehistoric Germanic *<i>ǣrundjam</i>, which meant ‘message’ – a sense which in fact survived in English until as recently as the 18th century (Miles Coverdale, for example, in his 1535 translation of 1 Samuel 11:5 wrote ‘So they told him the errand of the men of Jabesh’ – where the Authorized Version has ‘tidings’).<br><br>The main modern meaning, ‘task one goes to perform’, developed in the 13th century (in American English it has latterly gained specific connotations of ‘shopping’). The source of the Germanic word is not known, but it is no doubt related to Swedish <i>ärende </i>and Danish <i>ærinde </i>‘errand, message, business’.</span><br></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/errand">errand (n.)</a></dt><dd>Old English <span class="foreign">ærende</span> "message, mission; answer, news, tidings," a common Germanic word (cognates: Old Saxon <span class="foreign">arundi</span>, Old Norse <span class="foreign">erendi</span>, Danish <span class="foreign">ærende</span>, Swedish <span class="foreign">ärende</span>, Old Frisian <span class="foreign">erende</span>, Old High German <span class="foreign">arunti</span> "message"), which is of uncertain origin. Compare Old English <span class="foreign">ar</span> "messenger, servant, herald." Originally of important missions; meaning "short, simple journey and task" is attested by 1640s. Related: <span class="foreign">Errands</span>. In Old English, <span class="foreign">ærendgast</span> was "angel," <span class="foreign">ærendraca</span> was "ambassador."</dd></dl></div></div>

            <div class="row" id="yd-liju">
                 <dl><dt>1. She went off on some <b>errand</b>. </dt> <dd>她办事去了。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>2. He sent me on a fool's <b>errand</b> . </dt> <dd>他派我去干白费力的事。</dd> <p>来自《权威词典》</p> </dl><dl><dt>3. Unable to get about, she sent Joy on one <b>errand</b> after another. </dt> <dd>她自己动不了窝,便派小福子一趟八趟的去买东西.</dd> <p>来自汉英文学 - 骆驼祥子</p> </dl><dl><dt>4. She had a more urgent <b>errand</b>. </dt> <dd>她有一个更加紧急的差事要办。</dd> <p>来自辞典例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>5. His <b>errand</b> - running capacity also improved. </dt> <dd>他做家务事的能力也有所提高.</dd> <p>来自英汉文学 - 嘉莉妹妹</p> </dl>                 <dl><p>[ <a href="/liju/w/errand">errand 造句</a> ]</p></dl>

        if (word == '') {
            return false;
        } else if (word.search(/^[\w \-]+$/) == 0) {
            top.location.href = '/w/' + encodeURIComponent(word.replace(/ /g, '_'));
            return false;

                var char_code = match.charCodeAt(0) - 0xfee0;
                return String.fromCharCode(char_code);

        }
