                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/noise">noise</a> 噪音</span><p>词源不详，通常认为来自nausea,晕船，恶心。引申词义呕吐声，大叫，后用于指噪音。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/noise">noise</a></dt><dd><b>noise: </b><span>[13] Unlikely as it may seem, the ancestor of English <i>noise </i>meant ‘sickness’. It comes from Latin <i>nausea</i>, source also, of course, of English <i>nausea</i>. This was used colloquially for the sort of ‘hubbub’ or ‘confusion’ which is often coincident with someone being sick (and particularly seasick, which was what <i>nausea </i>originally implied), and Old French took it over, as <i>noise</i>, with roughly these senses. They later developed to ‘noisy dispute’, and modern French <i>noise </i>has retained the ‘dispute’ element of this, while English <i>noise </i>has gone for the ‘intrusive sound’.</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/nausea" target="_blank">nausea</a>, <a href="/etym/s/nautical" target="_blank">nautical</a>, <a href="/etym/s/navy" target="_blank">navy</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/noise">noise (n.)</a></dt><dd>early 13c., "loud outcry, clamor, shouting," from Old French <span class="foreign">noise</span> "din, disturbance, uproar, brawl" (11c., in modern French only in phrase <span class="foreign">chercher noise</span> "to pick a quarrel"), also "rumor, report, news," apparently from Latin <span class="foreign">nausea</span> "disgust, annoyance, discomfort," literally "seasickness" (see <a href="/etym/s/nausea" target="_blank">nausea</a>).&#13; <br /><br />&#13; Another theory traces the Old French word to Latin <span class="foreign">noxia</span> "hurting, injury, damage." OED considers that "the sense of the word is against both suggestions," but <span class="foreign">nausea</span> could have developed a sense in Vulgar Latin of "unpleasant situation, noise, quarrel" (compare Old Provençal <span class="foreign">nauza</span> "noise, quarrel"). Meaning "loud or unpleasant sound" is from c. 1300. Replaced native <span class="foreign">gedyn</span> (see <a href="/etym/s/din" target="_blank">din</a>).</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/noise">noise (v.)</a></dt><dd>late 14c., "to praise; to talk loudly about," from <a href="/etym/s/noise" target="_blank">noise</a> (n.). Related: <span class="foreign">Noised</span>; <span class="foreign">noising</span>.</dd></dl></div></div>

            <div class="row" id="yd-liju">
                 <dl><dt>1. Sightseers may be a little overwhelmed by the crowds and <b>noise</b>. </dt> <dd>拥挤的人群和喧闹的噪音可能会让游客有些茫然不知所措。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>2. Flying at 1,000 ft. he heard a peculiar <b>noise</b> from the rotors. </dt> <dd>在1,000英尺的高度飞行时，他听到旋翼发出一种奇怪的噪音。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>3. With a low-pitched rumbling <b>noise</b>, the propeller began to rotate. </dt> <dd>伴随着隆隆的低沉噪声，螺旋桨开始旋转起来。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>4. There was a heavy thudding <b>noise</b> against the bedroom door. </dt> <dd>卧室门上发出一声沉重的撞击声。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>5.
