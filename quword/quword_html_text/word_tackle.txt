                        <div class="page-header"><h3>记忆方法</h3></div>
            <div class="row" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;">1. take "grasp, seize" => tack- + -le => tackle.<br />
2. tack => tackle.<br />
<br />
分析：ta——“他”的拼音；ck——“刺客”的拼音首字母；le——“了”的拼音。<br />
记忆：他把刺客解决了。</div>

                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/tackle">tackle</a> 装备，用具，应付，处理，抢断，阻截</span><p>来自中古荷兰语 takel,船帆，索具，来自 taken,抓住，拿到，词源同 take.词义和拼写受 tack 影响，-le,工具格后缀。引申诸相关词义。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/tackle">tackle (n.)</a></dt><dd>mid-13c., "apparatus, gear," especially the rigging of a ship, from Middle Dutch or Middle Low German <span class="foreign">takel</span> "the rigging of a ship," perhaps related to Middle Dutch <span class="foreign">taken</span> "grasp, seize" (see <a href="/etym/s/take" target="_blank">take</a> (v.)), or perhaps from root of <a href="/etym/s/tack" target="_blank">tack</a> (n.1), which, if not the origin, has influenced the sense. Meaning "apparatus for fishing" is recorded from late 14c. Meaning "device for grasping and shifting or moving" is from 1530s. Meaning "act of tackling" in the sporting sense is recorded from 1876 (see <a href="/etym/s/tackle" target="_blank">tackle</a> (v.)); as the name of a position in North American football, it is recorded from 1884. Welsh <span class="foreign">tacl</span> is fro English.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/tackle">tackle (v.)</a></dt><dd>mid-14c., "entangle, involve," from <a href="/etym/s/tackle" target="_blank">tackle</a> (n.). Sense of "to furnish (a ship) with tackles" is from c. 1400; meaning "to harness a horse" is recorded from 1714. The meaning "lay hold of, come to grips with, attack" is attested from 1828, described by Webster that year as "a common popular use of the word in New England, though not elegant;" figurative sense of "try to deal with" (a task or problem) is from 1840. The verb in the sporting sense first recorded 1867, "to seize and stop." Related: <span class="foreign">Tackled</span>; <span class="foreign">tackling</span>.</dd></dl></div></div>
