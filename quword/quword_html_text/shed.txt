                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/shed">shed</a> 工棚，厂房</span><p>来自 shade 拼写变体，引申词义简易遮盖地，工棚，厂房。</p><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/shed">shed</a> 去除，摆脱，蜕，落</span><p>来自古英语 sceadan,分开，来自 Proto-Germanic*skaith,分开，劈开，来自 PIE*skei,切，分开， 词源同 segment,section.引申诸相关比喻义。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/shed">shed</a></dt><dd><b>shed: </b><span>English has two distinct words <i>shed</i>. The verb [OE] originally meant ‘divide, separate, split’ (a 14th-century religious poem paraphrased Genesis with ‘the sun to shed the day from the night’), and the modern range of senses, ‘give off, drop’, did not begin to emerge until the Middle English period. It goes back to a prehistoric Germanic *<i>skaithan</i>, which also produced German and Dutch <i>scheiden </i>‘separate’.<br><br>This was derived from a base *<i>skaith</i>- ‘divide, split’, source also of English <i>ski </i>and probably <i>sheath</i>. <i>Shed </i>‘hut’ [15] may be an alteration of <i>shade </i>(but the <i>shed </i>of <i>watershed </i>is of course a noun use of the verb <i>shed</i>).</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/sheath" target="_blank">sheath</a>, <a href="/etym/s/ski" target="_blank">ski</a>; <a href="/etym/s/shade" target="_blank">shade</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/shed">shed (n.)</a></dt><dd>"building for storage," 1855, earlier "light, temporary shelter" (late 15c., <span class="foreign">shadde</span>), possibly a dialectal variant of a specialized use of <a href="/etym/s/shade" target="_blank">shade</a> (n.). Originally of the barest sort of shelter. Or from or influenced in sense development by Middle English <span class="foreign">schudde</span> (<span class="foreign">shud</span>) "a shed, hut."</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/shed">shed (v.)</a></dt><dd>"cast off," Old English <span class="foreign">sceadan</span>, <span class="foreign">scadan</span> "to divide, separate, part company; discriminate, decide; scatter abroad, cast about," strong verb (past tense <span class="foreign">scead</span>, past participle <span class="foreign">sceadan</span>), from Proto-Germanic <span class="foreign">*skaithan</span> (cognates: Old Saxon <span class="foreign">skethan</span>, Old Frisian <span class="foreign">sketha</span>, Middle Dutch <span class="foreign">sceiden</span>, Dutch <span class="foreign">scheiden</span>, Old High German <span class="foreign">sceidan</span>, German <span class="foreign">scheiden</span> "part, separate, distinguish," Gothic <span class="foreign">skaidan</span> "separate"), from <span class="foreign">*skaith</span> "divide, split."&#13; <br /><br />&#13; According to Klein's sources, this probably is related to PIE root <span class="foreign">*skei-</span> "to cut, separate, divide, part, split" (cognates: Sanskrit <span class="foreign">chid-</span>, Greek <span class="foreign">skhizein</span>, Latin <span class="foreign">scindere</span> "to split;" Lithuanian <span class="foreign">skedzu</span> "I make thin, separate, divide;" Old Irish <span class="foreign">scian</span> "knife;" Welsh <span class="foreign">chwydu</span> "to break open"). Related: <span class="foreign">Shedding</span>. A <span class="foreign">shedding-tooth</span> (1799) was a milk-tooth or baby-tooth. &#13; <br /><br />&#13; In reference to animals, "to lose hair, feathers, etc." recorded from c. 1500; of trees losing leaves from 1590s; of clothes, 1858. This verb was used in Old English to gloss Late Latin words in the sense "to discriminate, to decide" that literally mean "to divide, separate" (compare <a href="/etym/s/discern" target="_blank">discern</a>). Hence also <span class="foreign">scead</span> (n.) "separation, distinction; discretion, understanding, reason;" <span class="foreign">sceadwisnes</span> "discrimination, discretion."</dd></dl></div></div>
