                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/register">register</a> 登记，注册</span><p>来自拉丁语 regerere,登记，记录，-st,过去分词格，来自 re-,向后，往回，-ger,携带，承载， 词源同 gesture,belligerent.</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/register">register</a></dt><dd><b>register: </b><span>[14] <i>Register </i>comes via Old French <i>registre </i>from late Latin <i>regestum </i>‘list’. This was a noun use of the past participle of <i>regerere </i>‘bring back’, hence ‘set down, record’, a compound verb formed from the prefix <i>re</i>- ‘back, again’ and <i>gerere </i>‘bring, carry’ (source also of English <i>congest</i>, <i>digest</i>, <i>gesture</i>, <i>jester</i>, <i>suggest</i>, etc).</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/congest" target="_blank">congest</a>, <a href="/etym/s/digest" target="_blank">digest</a>, <a href="/etym/s/gesture" target="_blank">gesture</a>, <a href="/etym/s/jester" target="_blank">jester</a>, <a href="/etym/s/suggest" target="_blank">suggest</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/register">register (n.1)</a></dt><dd>late 14c., from Old French <span class="foreign">registre</span> (13c.) and directly from Medieval Latin <span class="foreign">registrum</span>, alteration of Late Latin <span class="foreign">regesta</span> "list, matters recorded," noun use of Latin <span class="foreign">regesta</span>, neuter plural of <span class="foreign">regestus</span>, past participle of <span class="foreign">regerere</span> "to record; retort," literally "to carry back, bring back" from <span class="foreign">re-</span> "back" (see <a href="/etym/s/re-" target="_blank">re-</a>) + <span class="foreign">gerere</span> "carry, bear" (see <a href="/etym/s/gest" target="_blank">gest</a>).&#13; <br /><br />&#13; Also borrowed in Dutch, German, Swedish, Danish. Some senses influenced by association with Latin <span class="foreign">regere</span> "to rule." Meaning in printing, "exact alignment of presswork" is from 1680s. Musical sense is from 1811, "compass or range of a voice or instrument," hence "series of tones of the same quality" (produced by a voice or instrument). Sense "device by which data is automatically recorded" is 1830, from the verb; hence <span class="foreign">Cash register</span> (1875).</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/register">register (v.)</a></dt><dd>late 14c. (transitive), "enter in a listing," from Old French <span class="foreign">registrer</span> "note down, include" (13c.) and directly from Medieval Latin <span class="foreign">registrare</span>, from <span class="foreign">registrum</span> (see <a href="/etym/s/register" target="_blank">register</a> (n.)). Intransitive sense, of instruments, from 1797; of persons and feelings, "make an impression," from 1901. Meaning "to enter one's name in a list" for some purpose is from 1940. Related: <span class="foreign">Registered</span>; <span class="foreign">registering</span>. <span class="foreign">Registered nurse</span> attested from 1879.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/register">register (n.2)</a></dt><dd>"assistant court officer in administrative or routine function," 1530s, now chiefly U.S., alteration of <a href="/etym/s/registrar" target="_blank">registrar</a> (q.v) due to influence of <a href="/etym/s/register" target="_blank">register</a>.</dd></dl></div></div>
