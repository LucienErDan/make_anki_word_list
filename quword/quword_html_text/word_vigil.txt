                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/vigil">vigil</a> 守夜，值夜</span><p>来自拉丁语vigil,警醒，看守，来自PIE*weg,有活力的，积极的，词源同vigor,wake。引申词义守夜，值夜。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/vigil">vigil</a></dt><dd><b>vigil: </b><span>[13] Etymologically, to take part in a <i>vigil</i>, you have to be ‘alert’ and ‘awake’. The word comes via Old French <i>vigile </i>from Latin <i>vigilia</i>, which was derived from the adjective <i>vigil </i>‘awake, alert’, so the notion underlying it is of staying awake to keep watch. Another derivative of the Latin adjective was <i>vigilāre </i>‘keep watch’, which lies behind English <i>reveille </i>[17], <i>surveillance </i>[19], <i>vigilant </i>[15], and <i>vigilante </i>[19] (via Spanish). It came ultimately from the Indo-European base *<i>wog</i>-, *<i>weg</i>- ‘be lively or active’, which also produced English <i>vigour</i>, <i>wake </i>and <i>watch</i>.</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/reveille" target="_blank">reveille</a>, <a href="/etym/s/surveillance" target="_blank">surveillance</a>, <a href="/etym/s/vigilante" target="_blank">vigilante</a>, <a href="/etym/s/vigour" target="_blank">vigour</a>, <a href="/etym/s/wake" target="_blank">wake</a>, <a href="/etym/s/watch" target="_blank">watch</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/vigil">vigil (n.)</a></dt><dd>c. 1200, "eve of a religious festival" (an occasion for devotional watching or observance), from Anglo-French and Old French <span class="foreign">vigile</span> "watch, guard; eve of a holy day" (12c.), from Latin <span class="foreign">vigilia</span> "a watch, watchfulness," from <span class="foreign">vigil</span> "watchful, awake, on the watch, alert," from PIE root <span class="foreign">*weg-</span> (2) "be lively or active, be strong" (cognates: Old English <span class="foreign">wacan</span> "to wake up, arise," <span class="foreign">wacian</span> "to be awake;" Old High German <span class="foreign">wahta</span> "watch, vigil;" see <a href="/etym/s/wake" target="_blank">wake</a> (v.)). Meaning "watch kept on a festival eve" in English is from late 14c.; general sense of "occasion of keeping awake for some purpose" is recorded from 1711.</dd></dl></div></div>
