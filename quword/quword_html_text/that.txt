                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/that">that</a> 那，那个</span><p>来自古英语 thaet,那，那个，来自 Proto-Germanic*that,那，那个，来自 PIE*tod,指示代词， 扩展自 PIE*to,指示代词，词源同 the,they.词义演变比较同源词 here,he.</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/that">that</a></dt><dd><b>that: </b><span>[OE] <i>That </i>is a very ancient word, going right back to prehistoric Indo-European *<i>tad </i>(source also of Greek <i>tó </i>‘the’ and Russian <i>tot’</i>, <i>ta</i>, <i>to </i>‘that’). Its original function in English was as the neuter form of a demonstrative pronoun. This came to be used as the definite article (modern English <i>the</i>), but as the grammatical gender system began to break down in the early Middle English period, <i>that </i>broke away from the definite article and began to be used as a demonstrative adjective. Its use as a relative pronoun goes back to the Old English period.</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/the" target="_blank">the</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/that">that (pron.)</a></dt><dd>Old English <span class="foreign">þæt</span>, "that, so that, after that," neuter singular demonstrative pronoun ("A Man's a Man for a' that"), relative pronoun ("O thou that hearest prayer"), and &#13; demonstrative adjective ("Look at that caveman go!"), corresponding to masc. <span class="foreign">se</span>, fem. <span class="foreign">seo</span>. From Proto-Germanic <span class="foreign">*that</span>, from PIE <span class="foreign">*tod-</span>, extended form of demonstrative pronominal base <span class="foreign">*-to-</span> (see <a href="/etym/s/-th" target="_blank">-th</a> (1)). With the breakdown of the grammatical gender system, it came to be used in Middle English and Modern English for all genders. Germanic cognates include Old Saxon <span class="foreign">that</span>, Old Frisian <span class="foreign">thet</span>, Middle Dutch, Dutch <span class="foreign">dat</span> "that," German <span class="foreign">der</span>, <span class="foreign">die</span>, <span class="foreign">das</span> "the."&#13; <br /><br />&#13; Generally more specific or emphatic than <a href="/etym/s/the" target="_blank">the</a>, but in some cases they are interchangeable. From c. 1200 opposed to <a href="/etym/s/this" target="_blank">this</a> as indicating something farther off. In adverbial use ("I'm that old"), in reference to something implied or previously said, c. 1200, an abbreviation of the notion of "to that extent," "to that degree." Slang <span class="foreign">that way</span> "in love" first recorded 1929. <span class="foreign">That-a-way</span> "in that direction" is recorded from 1839. <span class="foreign">"Take that!"</span> said while delivering a blow, is recorded from early 15c.</dd></dl></div></div>

            <div class="row" id="yd-liju">

If you wait, all <b>that</b> happens is <b>that</b> you get older. </dt> <dd>如果你等待，发生的只有变老。</dd> <p>来自金山词霸 每日一句</p> </dl><dl><dt>3. When life gets hard and you want to give up, remember <b>that</b> life is full of ups and downs, and without the downs, the ups would mean nothing. </dt> <dd>当生活很艰难，你想要放弃的时候，请记住，生活充满了起起落落，如果没有低谷，那站在高处也失去了意义。</dd> <p>来自</p> </dl><dl><dt>4. Eventually, you'll learn to cry <b>that</b> on the inside. </dt> <dd>终有一天，你会学会让泪往心里流。</dd> <p>来自美剧《破产姐妹》</p> </dl><dl><dt>5.
