                        <div class="page-header"><h3>记忆方法</h3></div>
            <div class="row" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;">1. syrinx：【希腊神话】西琳克丝(阿卡狄亚水泽女神，被潘追求，为保贞洁变为芦苇，潘用此制成潘神箫)。<br />
2. syrinx => syringe.<br />
3. 绪任克斯（或西琳克丝）（Syrinx）是希腊神话里阿耳卡狄亚（Arcadia）的山林女神，狩猎女神阿耳忒弥斯Artemis的随从，以贞洁闻名。一天绪任克斯发现自己被潘（Pan）追逐，在即将被追上的时候，绪任克斯请求父亲河神拉冬（Ladon）把她变成芦苇。潘看到绪任克斯所追逐的女郎变成了芦苇，便折芦苇做成排箫来纪念他所爱恋的人。<br />
4. 谐音“射润剂”。</div>

                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/syringe">syringe</a> 吸管，注射器</span><p>来自希腊语 syringa,管子，芦苇，排箫，来自 syrizein,吹管，耳语，发沙沙声，来自 PIE*swer, 沙沙声，嗡嗡声，词源同 swarm,susurrate.后用于指吸管或注射器。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/syringe">syringe</a></dt><dd><b>syringe: </b><span>[15] Syringes get their name from their cylindrical shape. The word comes from late Latin <i>syringa</i>, an alteration of Latin <i>syrinx</i>, which in turn went back to Greek <i>súrigx </i>‘pipe, shepherd’s pipe’. The stems of both the mock orange shrub and the lilac were used for making such pipes – hence their alternative name <i>syringa </i>[17].</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/syringa" target="_blank">syringa</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/syringe">syringe (n.)</a></dt><dd>"narrow tube for injecting a stream of liquid," early 15c. (earlier <span class="foreign">suringa</span>, late 14c.), from Late Latin <span class="foreign">syringa</span>, from Greek <span class="foreign">syringa</span>, accusative of <span class="foreign">syrinx</span> "tube, hole, channel, shepherd's pipe," related to <span class="foreign">syrizein</span> "to pipe, whistle, hiss," from PIE root <span class="foreign">*swer-</span> (see <a href="/etym/s/susurration" target="_blank">susurration</a>). Originally a catheter for irrigating wounds; the application to hypodermic needles is from 1884. Related: <span class="foreign">Syringeal</span>.</dd></dl></div></div>
