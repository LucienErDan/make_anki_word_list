                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/idiom">idiom</a> 习语，习惯</span><p>来自希腊语idios,个人的，自己的，来自PIE*swe,个人，自己，词源同self,swain,custom.引申词义个人的生活习惯，个人的说话方式，后抽象化为习语，习惯。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/idiom">idiom (n.)</a></dt><dd>1580s, "form of speech peculiar to a people or place," from Middle French <span class="foreign">idiome</span> (16c.) and directly from Late Latin <span class="foreign">idioma</span> "a peculiarity in language," from Greek <span class="foreign">idioma</span> "peculiarity, peculiar phraseology," from <span class="foreign">idioumai</span> "to appropriate to oneself," from <span class="foreign">idios</span> "personal, private," properly "particular to oneself," from PIE <span class="foreign">*swed-yo-</span>, suffixed form of root <span class="foreign">*s(w)e-</span>, pronoun of the third person and reflexive (referring back to the subject of a sentence), also used in forms denoting the speaker's social group, "(we our-)selves" (cognates: Sanskrit <span class="foreign">svah</span>, Avestan <span class="foreign">hva-</span>, Old Persian <span class="foreign">huva</span> "one's own," <span class="foreign">khva-data</span> "lord," literally "created from oneself;" Greek <span class="foreign">hos</span> "he, she, it;" Latin <span class="foreign">suescere</span> "to accustom, get accustomed," <span class="foreign">sodalis</span> "companion;" Old Church Slavonic <span class="foreign">svoji</span> "his, her, its," <span class="foreign">svojaku</span> "relative, kinsman;" Gothic <span class="foreign">swes</span> "one's own;" Old Norse <span class="foreign">sik</span> "oneself;" German <span class="foreign">Sein</span>; Old Irish <span class="foreign">fein</span> "self, himself"). Meaning "phrase or expression peculiar to a language" is from 1620s.</dd></dl></div></div>

            <div class="row" id="yd-liju">

I like the <b>idiom</b> of modern popular music. </dt> <dd>我喜欢现代流行音乐的风格.</dd> <p>来自《简明英汉词典》</p> </dl><dl><dt>3. And nothing was so irritating as the confident way he used archaic <b>idiom</b>. </dt> <dd>没什么比他使用过时的方言时那种自负的神态更气人的了。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>4. McCartney was also keen to write in a classical <b>idiom</b>, rather than a pop one. </dt> <dd>麦卡特尼也热衷于古典风格而不是通俗风格的创作。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>5. She is, in fact, a perfect illustration of the French <b>idiom</b> "to be comfortable in one's own skin." </dt> <dd>实际上，她很好地诠释了“欣然接纳自我”这一法语习语的意思。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl>                 <dl><p>[ <a href="/liju/w/idiom">idiom 造句</a> ]</p></dl>

        if (word == '') {
            return false;
        } else if (word.search(/^[\w \-]+$/) == 0) {
