                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/straight">straight</a> 直的，正的，正直的，传统的，异性恋的</span><p>来自中古英语 streight,拉直的，过去分词格于 strechen,拉直，展开，来自古英语 streccan,拉 直，展开，词源同 stretch,伸展，延伸。拼写比较 bake,batch.引申词义正的，正直的，以及现 代词义传统的，异性恋的等。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/straight">straight</a></dt><dd><b>straight: </b><span>[14] <i>Straight </i>began life as the past participle of <i>stretch</i>. Nowadays this verb has a perfectly normal past form (<i>stretched</i>), but in Middle English it was <i>straught </i>(source of <i>distraught </i>[14], an alteration of <i>distract</i>) or <i>straight </i>– whence the adjective <i>straight</i>. The sense ‘not bent or curved’ derives from the notion of stretching something between two points. <i>Straightaway </i>[15] originally meant ‘by a straight path’; the temporal sense ‘immediately’ emerged in the 16th century.</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/distract" target="_blank">distract</a>, <a href="/etym/s/distraught" target="_blank">distraught</a>, <a href="/etym/s/stretch" target="_blank">stretch</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/straight">straight (adj.1)</a></dt><dd>late 14c., "direct, undeviating; not crooked, not bent or curved," of a person, "direct, honest;" properly "stretched," adjectival use of Old English <span class="foreign">streht</span> (earlier <span class="foreign">streaht</span>), past participle of <span class="foreign">streccan</span> "to stretch" (see <a href="/etym/s/stretch" target="_blank">stretch</a> (v.)). Related: <span class="foreign">Straightly</span>; <span class="foreign">straightness</span>.&#13; <br /><br />&#13; Meaning "true, direct, honest" is from 1520s. Of communication, "clear, unambiguous," from 1862. Sense of "undiluted, uncompromising" (as in <span class="foreign">straight whiskey</span>, 1874) is American English, first recorded 1856. As an adverb from c. 1300, "in a straight line, without swerving or deviating." Theatrical sense of "serious" (as opposed to <span class="foreign">popular</span> or <span class="foreign">comic</span>) is attested from 1895; vaudeville slang <span class="foreign">straight man</span> first attested 1923.&#13; <br /><br /><span class="foreign">Go straight</span> in the underworld slang sense is from 1919; <span class="foreign">straighten up</span> "become respectable" is from 1907. To <span class="foreign">play it straight</span> is from 1906 in theater, 1907 in sports ("play fair"), with figurative extension; later perhaps also from jazz. <span class="foreign">Straight arrow</span> "decent, conventional person" is 1969, from archetypal Native American brave name. <span class="foreign">Straight shooter</span> is from 1928. <span class="foreign">Straight As</span> "top grades" is from 1920.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/straight">straight (adj.2)</a></dt><dd>"conventional," especially "heterosexual," 1941, a secondary sense evolved from <a href="/etym/s/straight" target="_blank">straight</a> (adj.1), probably suggested by <span class="foreign">straight and narrow path</span> "course of conventional morality and law-abiding behavior," which is based on a misreading of Matt. vii:14 (where the gate is actually <a href="/etym/s/strait" target="_blank">strait</a>), and the other influence seems to be from <a href="/etym/s/strait-laced" target="_blank">strait-laced</a>.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/straight">straight (n.)</a></dt><dd>1640s, "a level position," from <a href="/etym/s/straight" target="_blank">straight</a> (adj.1). From 1864 as "straight part of a race track." Poker sense attested from 1841. Meaning "conventional person" is first recorded 1967, from <a href="/etym/s/straight" target="_blank">straight</a> (adj.2).</dd></dl></div></div>
