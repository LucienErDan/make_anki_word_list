                        <div class="page-header"><h3>记忆方法</h3></div>
            <div class="row" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;">thatch n. 茅草   vt. 用茅草盖→ hatch  “用茅草盖房，用来孵小鸡”<br />
1. deck => thatch.<br />
2. To thatch a building is etymologically to 'cover' it; the notion of 'straw' is a secondary development.<br />
3. The word goes back ultimately to the PIE base *tog-, *teg- 'cover' (source also of English detect, integument, protect, tile, and toga).<br />
4. deck, detect, protect, tile, toga => thatch.</div>

                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/thatch">thatch</a></dt><dd><b>thatch: </b><span>[OE] To <i>thatch </i>a building is etymologically to ‘cover’ it; the notion of ‘straw’ is a secondary development. The word goes back ultimately to the Indo-European base *<i>tog</i>-, *<i>teg</i>- ‘cover’ (source also of English <i>detect</i>, <i>integument</i>, <i>protect</i>, <i>tile</i>, and <i>toga</i>). Its Germanic descendant was *<i>thak</i>- (source of German <i>dach </i>‘roof’ and English <i>deck</i>). From this was derived *<i>thakjan</i>, which gave English <i>thatch</i>.</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/deck" target="_blank">deck</a>, <a href="/etym/s/detect" target="_blank">detect</a>, <a href="/etym/s/integument" target="_blank">integument</a>, <a href="/etym/s/protect" target="_blank">protect</a>, <a href="/etym/s/thug" target="_blank">thug</a>, <a href="/etym/s/tile" target="_blank">tile</a>, <a href="/etym/s/toga" target="_blank">toga</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/thatch">thatch (v.)</a></dt><dd>late 14c., <span class="foreign">thecchen</span>, from Old English <span class="foreign">þeccan</span> "to cover, cover over, conceal," in late Old English specifically "cover the roof of a house," related to <span class="foreign">þæc</span> "roof, thatching material," from Proto-Germanic <span class="foreign">*thakan</span> (cognates: Old Saxon <span class="foreign">thekkian</span>, Old Norse <span class="foreign">þekja</span>, Old Frisian <span class="foreign">thekka</span>, Middle Dutch <span class="foreign">decken</span>, Dutch <span class="foreign">dekken</span>, Old High German <span class="foreign">decchen</span>, German <span class="foreign">decken</span> "to cover"), from PIE <span class="foreign">*(s)teg-</span> (2) "to cover" (see <a href="/etym/s/stegosaurus" target="_blank">stegosaurus</a>).</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/thatch">thatch (n.)</a></dt><dd>Old English <span class="foreign">þæc</span> "roof, thatch, cover of a building," from the source of <a href="/etym/s/thatch" target="_blank">thatch</a> (v.). Compare Old Norse <span class="foreign">þak</span>, Old Frisian <span class="foreign">thek</span>, Swedish <span class="foreign">tak</span>, Danish <span class="foreign">tag</span>, Middle Dutch, Dutch <span class="foreign">dak</span> "roof," Old High German <span class="foreign">dah</span> "covering, cover," German <span class="foreign">Dach</span> "roof."</dd></dl></div></div>
