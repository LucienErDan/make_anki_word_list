                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/fix">fix</a> 固定</span><p>来自拉丁语figere, 固定，来自PIE*dhigw, 挖，固定，词源同dig, ditch.</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/fix">fix</a></dt><dd><b>fix: </b><span>[15] <i>Fix </i>comes ultimately from Latin <i>fīgere </i>‘fasten’. Its past participle <i>fīxus </i>made its way into English along two distinct routes, partly via the Old French adjective <i>fix </i>‘fixed’, and partly via the medieval Latin verb <i>fīxāre</i>. Derived forms in English include <i>affix </i>[15], <i>prefix </i>[17], <i>suffix </i>[18], and <i>transfix </i>[16], and also <i>fichu </i>‘scarf’ [19]: this came from the past participle of French <i>ficher </i>‘attach’, which is descended from Vulgar Latin *<i>figicāre</i>, another derivative of <i>figere</i>.</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/affix" target="_blank">affix</a>, <a href="/etym/s/prefix" target="_blank">prefix</a>, <a href="/etym/s/suffix" target="_blank">suffix</a>, <a href="/etym/s/transfix" target="_blank">transfix</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/fix">fix (v.)</a></dt><dd>late 14c., "set (one's eyes or mind) on something" (a figurative use), probably from Old French verb <span class="foreign">*fixer</span>, from <span class="foreign">fixe</span> "fixed," from Latin <span class="foreign">fixus</span> "fixed, fast, immovable; established, settled," past participle adjective from <span class="foreign">figere</span> "to fix, fasten, drive, thrust in; pierce through, transfix," also figurative, from PIE root <span class="foreign">*dhigw-</span> "to stick, to fix" (see <a href="/etym/s/dike" target="_blank">dike</a>).&#13; <br /><br />&#13; Sense of "fasten, attach" is c. 1400; that of "to make (colors, etc.) fast or permanent" is from 1660s. The meaning "settle, assign" evolved into "adjust, arrange" (1660s), then "repair" (1737). Sense of "tamper with" (a fight, a jury, etc.) is from 1790. As euphemism for "castrate a pet" it dates from 1930. Related: <a href="/etym/s/Fixed" target="_blank">Fixed</a>; <span class="foreign">fixing</span>.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/fix">fix (n.)</a></dt><dd>"position from which it is difficult to move," 1809, American English, from <a href="/etym/s/fix" target="_blank">fix</a> (v.). Meaning "dose of narcotic" is from 1934, shortened from <span class="foreign">fix-up</span> (1867, originally in reference to liquor). Meaning "reliable indication of the position of a ship, plane, etc." (by reference to fixed positions) is from 1902.</dd></dl></div></div>
