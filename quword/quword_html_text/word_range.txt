                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/range">range</a> 排列，范围，距离，山脉</span><p>来自古法语 range,排列，布置，成排，来自 reng,排，行，线，词源同 rank.引申诸相关词义。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/range">range</a></dt><dd><b>range: </b><span>[13] <i>Range </i>and <i>rank </i>come ultimately from the same source: Old French <i>ranc</i>. This was borrowed directly into English as <i>rank</i>, but it subsequently developed to <i>rang</i>, from which was derived the verb <i>rangier </i>‘set in a row’ (ancestor of English <i>arrange</i>). This in turn produced the noun <i>range </i>‘rank, row’.</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/rank" target="_blank">rank</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/range">range (v.)</a></dt><dd>c. 1200, <span class="foreign">rengen</span>, "move over a large area, roam with the purpose of searching or hunting," from Old French <span class="foreign">ranger</span>, earlier <span class="foreign">rengier</span> "to place in a row, arrange; get into line," from <span class="foreign">reng</span> "row, line," from a Germanic source (see <a href="/etym/s/rank" target="_blank">rank</a> (n.)). Sense of "to arrange in rows" is recorded from c. 1300; intransitive sense of "exist in a row or rows" is from c. 1600. Related: <span class="foreign">Ranged</span>; <span class="foreign">ranging</span>.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/range">range (n.)</a></dt><dd>c. 1200, "row or line of persons" (especially hunters or soldiers), from Old French <span class="foreign">range</span> "range, rank" (see <a href="/etym/s/range" target="_blank">range</a> (v.)). General sense of "line, row" is from early 14c.; meaning "row of mountains" is from 1705.&#13; <br /><br />&#13; Meaning "scope, extent" first recorded late 15c.; that of "area over which animals seek food" is from 1620s, from the verb. Specific U.S. sense of "series of townships six miles in width" is from 1785. Sense of "distance a gun can send a bullet" is recorded from 1590s; meaning "place used for shooting practice" is from 1862. The cooking appliance so called since mid-15c., for unknown reasons. Originally a stove built into a fireplace with openings on top for multiple operations. <span class="foreign">Range-finder</span> attested from 1872.</dd></dl></div></div>
