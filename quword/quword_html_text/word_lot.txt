                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/lot">lot</a> 抽签，份额，命运，块，批，许多</span><p>来自hlot,用于抽签的小物件，骰子，草，木条等，词源不详。可能来自PIE*kel,砍，切，词源同clone,cleric,clastic.并引申诸多词义。常见于词组a lot of,即一块，一批，大量的。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/lot">lot</a></dt><dd><b>lot: </b><span>[OE] <i>Lot </i>goes back to a prehistoric Germanic *<i>khlut</i>-, which appears to have denoted the use of objects to make decisions by chance (Old English <i>hlot </i>was used for such an object). The first inklings of the modern range of senses did not emerge until the 18th century, when <i>lot </i>began to be used for a ‘set of things’. ‘Large number, many’ followed in the 19th century. The Germanic word was borrowed into the Romance languages, and of its descendants English has acquired <i>allot </i>[16] (from Old French) and <i>lotto </i>[18] (from Italian). <i>Lottery </i>[16] comes from the Dutch derivative <i>loterij</i>.</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/allot" target="_blank">allot</a>, <a href="/etym/s/lottery" target="_blank">lottery</a>, <a href="/etym/s/lotto" target="_blank">lotto</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/lot">lot (n.)</a></dt><dd>Old English <span class="foreign">hlot</span> "object (anything from dice to straw, but often a chip of wood with a name inscribed on it) used to determine someone's share," also "what falls to a person by lot," from Proto-Germanic <span class="foreign">*khlutom</span> (cognates: Old Norse <span class="foreign">hlutr</span> "lot, share," Old Frisian <span class="foreign">hlot</span> "lot," Old Saxon <span class="foreign">hlot</span>, Middle Dutch, Dutch <span class="foreign">lot</span>, Old High German <span class="foreign">hluz</span> "share of land," German <span class="foreign">Los</span>; Old English <span class="foreign">hleotan</span> "to cast lots, to foretell"), of unknown origin. The object was placed with others in a receptacle, which was shaken, the winner being the one that fell out first. Hence, to <span class="foreign">cast lots</span>. In some cases the lots were drawn by hand. The word was adopted from Germanic into the Romanic languages (compare <a href="/etym/s/lottery" target="_blank">lottery</a>, <a href="/etym/s/lotto" target="_blank">lotto</a>). Meaning "choice resulting from the casting of lots" first attested c. 1200.&#13; <br /><br />&#13; Sense of "plot of land" is first recorded 1630s (distribution of the best property in new settlements often determined by casting lots), that of "group, collection" is 1725, from notion of auction lots. The generalized sense of "great many" is first attested in 1812. To <span class="foreign">cast (one's) lot with</span> another is to agree to share winnings.</dd></dl></div></div>
