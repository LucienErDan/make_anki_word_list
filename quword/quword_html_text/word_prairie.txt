                        <div class="page-header"><h3>记忆方法</h3></div>
            <div class="row" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;">伊利诺伊州：该州别名“内陆帝国” (The Inland Empire)或“草原之州” (The Prairie State)。州花是紫罗兰(Native Violet)。州鸟是北美红雀(Cardinal)。州树是白橡(White Oak)。<br />
<br />
普雷里县（Prairie County）<br />
<br />
1. 【记】praise(赞美) + air(空气) + -ie ( <= -y ) => 只有置身于空旷的、一望无际的大草原上你才会由衷地赞美新鲜的、清新的空气，仿佛空气中还带有一缕清香，那种沁人心脾的清新之气令人陶醉，因为这是令人心旷神怡的、一望无际的、空旷的大草原、那种感觉简直无法形容。<br />
2. 美国电影：牧场之家好作伴：A Prairie Home Companion.<br />
3. 护肤化妆品：La Prairie（蓓丽/莱珀妮）：一种产自瑞士的奢华的抗衰老的护肤产品。</div>

                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/prairie">prairie</a> 北美草原</span><p>来自法语prairie,草地，牧场，来自拉丁语pratum,草地。特指北美大草原。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/prairie">prairie</a></dt><dd><b>prairie: </b><span>[18] <i>Prairie </i>comes ultimately from Latin <i>prātum </i>‘meadow’ (source also of French <i>pré </i>‘meadow’). From it was derived Vulgar Latin *<i>prātāria</i>, which passed into English via French <i>prairie</i>. The word was from the start almost exclusively used with reference to the plains of North America.</span><br></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/prairie">prairie (n.)</a></dt><dd>tract of level or undulating grassland in North America, by 1773, from French <span class="foreign">prairie</span> "meadow, grassland," from Old French <span class="foreign">praerie</span> "meadow, pastureland" (12c.), from Vulgar Latin <span class="foreign">*prataria</span>, from Latin <span class="foreign">pratum</span> "meadow," originally "a hollow." The word existed in Middle English as <span class="foreign">prayere,</span> but was lost and reborrowed to describe the American plains. <span class="foreign">Prairie dog</span> is attested from 1774; <span class="foreign">prairie schooner</span> "immigrant's wagon" is from 1841. Illinois has been the <span class="foreign">Prairie State</span> since at least 1861. In Latin, <span class="foreign">Neptunia prata</span> was poetic for "the sea."</dd></dl></div></div>
