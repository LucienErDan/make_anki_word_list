                        <div class="page-header"><h3>记忆方法</h3></div>
            <div class="row" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;">1. PIE root *nem- "divide, distribute, allot; take" (cf. Greek nemein "deal out," nemesis "just indignation," Latin numerus "number") => nim- "take, grasp, seize" => OE: niman "take, grasp, seize".<br />
2. nim- "take, grasp, seize" + -le => -ble (see limb, 类比变形) => nimble "quick to grasp".<br />
3. => agile, light-footed.<br />
4. 同源词：nim, numb, nimble.<br />
5. 5. numb => nimble. 为什么能从 numb 推导出 nimble 的含义呢？因为他们两是同源词，也就是说这两兄弟是同一个爹生的，numb 的本义是“被抓住、被抱住”，因为人一但被抓住、被抱住后就动弹不得了，所以由此就引申为了“麻木”之意。nimble 的本义也就是“抓、抱”之意，只是拓展成了“快速地抓、抱(quick to grasp)”，当然只有很敏捷才会“快速地抓住”啊，所以就引申为“敏捷的、灵敏的、敏感的、机智的”之意。-le (diminutive suffix, frequentative suffix, 也许暗含表达了一种小巧灵活之意)。<br />
6. 谐音“灵吧” ---  灵活吧，灵巧吧！<br />
7. 谐音“凌波” --- 凌波微步 ---  想一想段誉练成凌波微步后，逃跑起来是不是很灵巧、敏捷啊！让敌人想打打不着，想追追不上。</div>

                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/nimble">nimble</a> 灵活的，敏捷的</span><p>来自PIE*nem,分开，分配，拿，带，词源同number,numb.引申词义灵活的，敏捷的。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/nimble">nimble (adj.)</a></dt><dd>"agile, light-footed," c. 1300, <span class="foreign">nemel</span>, from Old English <span class="foreign">næmel</span> "quick to grasp" (attested but once), related to <span class="foreign">niman</span> "to take," from Proto-Germanic <span class="foreign">*neman</span> (cognates: Old Saxon, Old Dutch, Gothic <span class="foreign">niman</span>, Old Norse <span class="foreign">nema</span>, Old Frisian <span class="foreign">nima</span>, German <span class="foreign">nehmen</span> "to take"), from PIE root <span class="foreign">*nem-</span> "to divide, distribute, allot," also "to take" (cognates: Greek <span class="foreign">nemein</span> "to deal out," <span class="foreign">nemesis</span> "just indignation," Latin <span class="foreign">numerus</span> "number," Lithuanian <span class="foreign">nuoma</span> "rent, interest," Middle Irish <span class="foreign">nos</span> "custom, usage"). With excrescent <span class="foreign">-b-</span> from c. 1500 (compare <a href="/etym/s/limb" target="_blank">limb</a> (n.1)). Related: <span class="foreign">Nimbleness</span>. In 17c., English had <span class="foreign">nimblechaps</span> "talkative fellow."</dd></dl></div></div>
