                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/device">device</a> 仪器</span><p>来自devise, 用作名词。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/device">device</a></dt><dd><b>device: </b><span>[13] A <i>device </i>is something which has been devised – which, etymologically speaking, amounts to ‘something which has been divided’. For ultimately <i>devise </i>and <i>divide </i>come from the same source. The noun <i>device </i>comes in the first instance from Old French <i>devis </i>‘division, contrivance’ and latterly (in the 15th century) from Old French <i>devise </i>‘plan’, both of which were derivatives of the verb <i>deviser </i>‘divide, devise’ (source of English <i>devise </i>[13]).<br><br>This in turn came from Vulgar Latin *<i>dīvisāre</i>, a verb based on the past participial stem of Latin <i>dīvidere</i>, source of English <i>divide</i>. The semantic development by which ‘divide’ passed to ‘contrive’, presumably based on the notion that dividing something up and distributing it needs some planning, happened before the word reached English, and English <i>device </i>has never meant ‘division’.<br><br>The sense ‘simple machine’ essentially evolved in the 16th century.</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/devise" target="_blank">devise</a>, <a href="/etym/s/divide" target="_blank">divide</a>, <a href="/etym/s/individual" target="_blank">individual</a>, <a href="/etym/s/widow" target="_blank">widow</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/device">device (n.)</a></dt><dd>late 13c., from Old French <span class="foreign">devis</span> "division, separation, disposition, wish, desire; coat of arms, emblem; last will," from <span class="foreign">deviser</span> "to divide, distribute" (see <a href="/etym/s/devise" target="_blank">devise</a>). Sense of "method by which something is divided" arose in French and led to modern meaning.</dd></dl></div></div>

            <div class="row" id="yd-liju">

The <b>device</b> exploded, throwing Mr Taylor from his car. </dt> <dd>那个装置爆炸了，把泰勒先生从车里抛了出去。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>3. The <b>device</b> can discriminate between the cancerous and the normal cells. </dt> <dd>这台仪器可以辨别癌细胞和正常的细胞。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>4. The built-in safety <b>device</b> compensates for a fall in water pressure. </dt> <dd>该内置式安全装置在水压下降时能提供保护。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>5. The <b>device</b> plugs into one of the laptop's USB ports. </dt> <dd>这个设备可以插入到笔记本电脑的一个通用串行总线接口。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl>                 <dl><p>[ <a href="/liju/w/device">device 造句</a> ]</p></dl>
