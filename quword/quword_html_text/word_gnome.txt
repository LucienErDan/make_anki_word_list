                        <div class="page-header"><h3>记忆方法</h3></div>
            <div class="row" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;">1. 哥布林与地精(Gnome)很相似，但却不是相同的生物。不过，由于传说的流传上的差异，使不少人都把这两种生命混淆。其实他们是有所不同的。<br />
2. 地精（Gnome，或音译为诺姆）是一种在欧洲的传说中出现的妖怪，身材矮小，头戴红色帽子，身穿伐木衣，经常在地下活动，成群结队出没。由于他的特性与其他传说生物很近似，他亦经常被错误当作是精灵、哥布林或矮人。Gnome语源：希腊语γνομη(gnome）知识，观点。欧美的家家户户皆有地精造型的装饰品。地精也被视为代表土的元素四精灵之一。<br />
3. 四大元素精灵: 火精灵、风精灵、水精灵、地精灵。</div>

                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/gnome">gnome</a></dt><dd><b>gnome: </b><span>[18] <i>Gnome </i>comes via French from Latin <i>gnomus</i>, a word coined by the 16thcentury Swiss physician Paracelsus for a type of being that lives in the earth, in the same way that fish live in water. It seems to have been a pure invention on his part, and is not based on or related to Greek <i>gnómē </i>‘opinion, judgment’ (source of English <i>gnomic </i>[19] and connected with <i>agnostic</i>, <i>diagnosis</i>, and <i>know</i>). The term <i>gnomes of Zürich </i>for ‘Swiss financiers’ is first recorded in the early 1960s.</span><br></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/gnome">gnome (n.1)</a></dt><dd>"dwarf-like earth-dwelling spirit," 1712, from French <span class="foreign">gnome</span> (16c.), from Medieval Latin <span class="foreign">gnomus</span>, used 16c. in a treatise by Paracelsus, who gave the name <span class="foreign">pigmaei</span> or <span class="foreign">gnomi</span> to elemental earth beings, possibly from Greek <span class="foreign">*genomos</span> "earth-dweller" (compare <span class="foreign">thalassonomos</span> "inhabitant of the sea"). A less-likely suggestion is that Paracelsus based it on the homonym that means "intelligence" (see <a href="/etym/s/gnome" target="_blank">gnome</a> (n.2)).&#13; <br /><br />&#13; Popularized in England in children's literature from early 19c. as a name for red-capped German and Swiss folklore dwarfs. Garden figurines of them were first imported to England late 1860s from Germany; <span class="foreign">garden-gnome</span> attested from 1933. <span class="foreign">Gnomes of Zurich</span> for "international financiers" is from 1964.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/gnome">gnome (n.2)</a></dt><dd>"short, pithy statement of general truth," 1570s, from Greek <span class="foreign">gnome</span> "judgment, opinion; maxim, the opinion of wise men" (see <a href="/etym/s/gnomic" target="_blank">gnomic</a>).</dd></dl></div></div>
