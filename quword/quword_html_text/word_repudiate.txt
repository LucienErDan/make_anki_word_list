                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/repudiate">repudiate</a> 拒绝，否认，断绝关系</span><p>来自拉丁语 repudiare,拒绝，否认，离婚，来自 repudium,离婚，断绝婚姻关系，来自 re-,向 后，离开，*pudium,击，打，来自 PIE*pu,击，打，词源同 pave,compute.也有学者认为该词 来自 ped,脚，踢，词源同 foot.</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/repudiate">repudiate</a></dt><dd><b>repudiate: </b><span>[16] <i>Repudiate </i>originally meant ‘divorce one’s wife’. It comes from Latin <i>repudiāre </i>‘divorce, reject’, a derivative of the noun <i>repudium </i>‘divorce’. It has been suggested that the ultimate source of this may be <i>pēs </i>‘foot’ (source of English <i>pedal</i>), in which case its underlying meaning would be virtually ‘kick out’.</span><br></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/repudiate">repudiate (v.)</a></dt><dd>1540s, "to cast off by divorce," from Latin <span class="foreign">repudiatus</span>, past participle of <span class="foreign">repudiare</span> "to cast off, put away, divorce, reject, scorn, disdain," from <span class="foreign">repudium</span> "divorce, rejection, a putting away, dissolution of marriage," from <span class="foreign">re-</span> "back, away" (see <a href="/etym/s/re-" target="_blank">re-</a>) + <span class="foreign">pudium</span>, which is probably related to <span class="foreign">pes/ped-</span> "foot" [Barnhart]. If this is so, the original notion may be of kicking something away, but folk etymology commonly connects it with <span class="foreign">pudere</span> "cause shame to." Of opinions, conduct, etc., "to refuse to acknowledge," attested from 1824. Earliest in English as an adjective meaning "divorced, rejected, condemned" (mid-15c.). Related: <span class="foreign">Repudiated</span>; <span class="foreign">repudiating</span>.</dd></dl></div></div>

He used his position to <b>repudiate</b> the charge. </dt> <dd>他利用自己的职位来否认指控.</dd> <p>来自《简明英汉词典》</p> </dl><dl><dt>3. Leaders urged people to turn out in large numbers to <b>repudiate</b> the violence. </dt> <dd>领导们力劝人们集体出面反对暴力。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>4. However, the Citizens opinion does not necessarily <b>repudiate</b> that proposition. </dt> <dd>但不管怎样, "公民"案的意见未必就否认了这一主张.</dd> <p>来自英汉非文学 - 行政法</p> </dl><dl><dt>5.             return false;
