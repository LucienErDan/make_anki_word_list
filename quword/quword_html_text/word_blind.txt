                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/blind">blind</a> 瞎的</span><p>来自PIE *bhel, 照耀，闪光。指闪光，炫目，使看不见的。瞎是词义发展的结果。词源同blend.</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/blind">blind</a></dt><dd><b>blind: </b><span>[OE] The connotations of the ultimate ancestor of <i>blind</i>, Indo-European *<i>bhlendhos</i>, seem to have been not so much ‘sightlessness’ as ‘confusion’ and ‘obscurity’. The notion of someone wandering around in actual or mental darkness, not knowing where to go, naturally progressed to the ‘inability to see’. Related words that fit this pattern are <i>blunder</i>, possibly from Old Norse <i>blunda </i>‘shut one’s eyes’, <i>blunt</i>, and maybe also <i>blend</i>.<br><br>By the time the word entered Old English, as <i>blind</i>, it already meant ‘sightless’, but ancestral associations of darkness and obscurity were retained (Pepys in his diary, for instance, writes of a ‘little blind [that is, dark] bed-chamber’ 1666), and traces of them remain in such usages as ‘blind entrance’.</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/blend" target="_blank">blend</a>, <a href="/etym/s/blunder" target="_blank">blunder</a>, <a href="/etym/s/blunt" target="_blank">blunt</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/blind">blind (adj.)</a></dt><dd>Old English <span class="foreign">blind</span> "blind," also "dark, enveloped in darkness, obscure; unintelligent, lacking mental perception," probably from Proto-Germanic <span class="foreign">*blinda-</span> "blind" (cognates: Dutch and German <span class="foreign">blind</span>, Old Norse <span class="foreign">blindr</span>, Gothic <span class="foreign">blinds</span> "blind"), perhaps, via notion of "to make cloudy, deceive," from an extended Germanic form of the PIE root <span class="foreign">*bhel-</span> (1) "to shine, flash, burn" (see <a href="/etym/s/bleach" target="_blank">bleach</a> (v.)). Compare Lithuanian <span class="foreign">blendzas</span> "blind," <span class="foreign">blesti</span> "to become dark." The original sense would be not "sightless" but rather "confused," which perhaps underlies such phrases as <span class="foreign">blind alley</span> (Chaucer's <span class="foreign">lanes blynde</span>), which is older than the sense of "closed at one end" (1610s).&#13; <blockquote>The twilight, or rather the hour between the time when one can no longer see to read and the lighting of the candles, is commonly called blindman's holiday. [Grose, 1796]&#13; </blockquote>&#13; In reference to doing something without seeing it first, by 1840. Of aviators flying without instruments or without clear observation, from 1919. Related: <span class="foreign">Blinded</span>; <a href="/etym/s/blinding" target="_blank">blinding</a>. <span class="foreign">Blindman's bluff</span> is from 1580s.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/blind">blind (v.)</a></dt><dd>"deprive of sight," early 13c., from Old English <span class="foreign">blendan</span> "to blind, deprive of sight; deceive," from Proto-Germanic <span class="foreign">*blandjan</span> (see <a href="/etym/s/blind" target="_blank">blind</a> (adj.)); form influenced in Middle English by the adjective. Related: <span class="foreign">Blinded</span>; <span class="foreign">blinding</span>.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/blind">blind (n.)</a></dt><dd>"a blind person; blind persons collectively," late Old Engish, from <a href="/etym/s/blind" target="_blank">blind</a> (adj.). Meaning "place of concealment" is from 1640s. Meaning "anything that obstructs sight" is from 1702.</dd></dl></div></div>

            <div class="row" id="yd-liju">

The road is a succession of hairpin bends, hills, and <b>blind</b> corners. </dt> <dd>这条路上急转弯、坡道和死拐角一个接一个。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>4. The Internet has proved a <b>blind</b> alley for many firms. </dt> <dd>事实证明，因特网对许多公司而言是一条行不通的路。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>5.
