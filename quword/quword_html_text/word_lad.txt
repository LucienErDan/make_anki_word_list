            <div class="row" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;">1. 谐音“男的”。<br />
2. 反义同源：lad <====> lass.<br />
3. lad 这货跟 lass 是天生一对儿，就跟 boy 和 girl 是天生一对儿一样的，所以这一对儿一定要一起记忆，所谓男女搭配干活不累嘛，不对应该是男女搭配记词不累。</div>

                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/lad">lad</a> 男孩，小伙</span><p>来自古英语ladde,步兵，年青的男仆，可能来自lead的过去分词，即被引导者，被带领的人，后用来指年轻的小伙。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/lad">lad</a></dt><dd><b>lad: </b><span>[13] <i>Lad </i>originally meant ‘male of low status or social rank’, and hence ‘male servant’, but by the 14th century its progression to the presentday ‘young male’ was well under way. It is not known where it came from, but there seems to be a strong likelihood of a Scandinavian origin (Norwegian has -<i>ladd </i>in compounds referring to ‘(male) persons’).</span><br></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/lad">lad (n.)</a></dt><dd>c. 1300, <span class="foreign">ladde</span> "foot soldier," also "young male servant" (attested as a surname from late 12c.), possibly from a Scandinavian language (compare Norwegian <span class="foreign">-ladd,</span> in compounds for "young man"), but of obscure origin in any case. OED hazards a guess on Middle English <span class="foreign">ladde</span>, plural of the past participle of <a href="/etym/s/lead" target="_blank">lead</a> (v.), thus "one who is led" (by a lord). Liberman derives it from Old Norse <span class="foreign">ladd</span> "hose; woolen stocking." "The development must have been from 'stocking,' 'foolish youth' to 'youngster of inferior status' and (with an ameliorated meaning) to 'young fellow.'" He adds, "Words for socks, stockings, and shoes seem to have been current as terms of abuse for and nicknames of fools." Meaning "boy, youth, young man" is from mid-15c. Scottish form <span class="foreign">laddie</span>, a term of endearment, attested from 1540s.</dd></dl></div></div>

He was a nice <b>lad</b> — bright and with a sunny disposition. </dt> <dd>他是个不错的小伙子——聪明伶俐而且性格开朗。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>3. He's a sensitive <b>lad</b> and some of the criticism has stung him. </dt> <dd>他是个敏感的小伙子，有些批评刺痛了他。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>4. Burns is a big <b>lad</b> with a rolling gait. </dt> <dd>伯恩斯是一个走起路来一摇一晃的高大小伙子。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>5. He's a mere slip of a <b>lad</b> compared to his brother. </dt> <dd>跟他哥哥一比，他不过是个小毛孩。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl>                 <dl><p>[ <a href="/liju/w/lad">lad 造句</a> ]</p></dl>

    document.getElementById('yd-search').onsubmit = function() {

        if (word == '') {
            return false;
        } else if (word.search(/^[\w \-]+$/) == 0) {
            top.location.href = '/w/' + encodeURIComponent(word.replace(/ /g, '_'));
            return false;

                var char_code = match.charCodeAt(0) - 0xfee0;
                return String.fromCharCode(char_code);
            });
        }
