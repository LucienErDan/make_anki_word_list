区分：raffle, rifle: 字母a是字母表中的首字母，可以理解为抽中头等奖，字母i很像一杆枪射出的子弹。<br />

                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/raffle">raffle</a> 抽彩游戏</span><p>来自古法语 rafle,骰子赌博，抢劫，劫掠，来自 Proto-Germanic*khrap,抢走，抓走，拔出，可 能来自 PIE*skerb,sker,砍，切，词源同 shear,sharp.比喻用法。拼写比较 ring,来自 PIE*sker, 弯，转，词源同 curve.</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/raffle">raffle</a></dt><dd><b>raffle: </b><span>[14] <i>Raffle </i>was originally the name of a game played with three dice; the modern application to a ‘prize draw’ did not emerge until the 18th century. The word was borrowed from Old French <i>raffle </i>‘act of snatching’, but where this came from is not known.</span><br></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/raffle">raffle (n.)</a></dt><dd>late 14c., "dice game," from Old French <span class="foreign">rafle</span> "dice game," also "plundering," perhaps from a Germanic source (compare Middle Dutch <span class="foreign">raffel</span> "dice game," Old Frisian <span class="foreign">hreppa</span> "to move," Old Norse <span class="foreign">hreppa</span> "to reach, get," Swedish <span class="foreign">rafs</span> "rubbish," Old High German <span class="foreign">raspon</span> "to scrape together, snatch up in haste," German <span class="foreign">raffen</span> "to snatch away, sweep off"), from Proto-Germanic <span class="foreign">*khrap-</span> "to pluck out, snatch off." The notion would be "to sweep up (the stakes), to snatch (the winnings)." Dietz connects the French word with the Germanic root, but OED is against this. Meaning "sale of chances" first recorded 1766.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/raffle">raffle (v.)</a></dt><dd>"dispose of by raffle," 1851, from <a href="/etym/s/raffle" target="_blank">raffle</a> (n.). Related: <span class="foreign">Raffled</span>; <span class="foreign">raffling</span>.</dd></dl></div></div>
