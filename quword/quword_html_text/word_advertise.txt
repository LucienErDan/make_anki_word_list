            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/advertise">advertise</a> 广告</span><p>前缀ad-, 去，往。词根vert, 转。吸引人们注意力的，广告。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/advertise">advertise</a></dt><dd><b>advertise: </b><span>[15] When it was originally borrowed into English, from French, <i>advertise </i>meant ‘notice’. It comes ultimately from the Latin verb <i>advertere </i>‘turn towards’ (whose past participle <i>adversus </i>‘hostile’ is the source of English <i>adverse </i>[14] and <i>adversity </i>[13]). A later variant form, <i>advertīre</i>, passed into Old French as <i>avertir </i>‘warn’ (not to be confused with the <i>avertir </i>from which English gets <i>avert </i>[15] and <i>averse </i>[16], which came from Latin <i>abvertere </i>‘turn away’).<br><br>This was later reformed into <i>advertir</i>, on the model of its Latin original, and its stem form <i>advertiss</i>- was taken into English, with its note of ‘warning’ already softening into ‘giving notice of’, or simply ‘noticing’. The modern sense of ‘describing publicly in order to increase sales’ had its beginnings in the mid 18th century. In the 16th and 17th centuries, the verb was pronounced with the main stress on its second syllable, like the <i>advertise</i>- in <i>advertisement</i>.</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/adverse" target="_blank">adverse</a>, <a href="/etym/s/adversity" target="_blank">adversity</a>, <a href="/etym/s/verse" target="_blank">verse</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/advertise">advertise (v.)</a></dt><dd>early 15c., "to take notice of," from Middle French <span class="foreign">advertiss-</span>, present participle stem of <span class="foreign">a(d)vertir</span> "to warn" (12c.), from Latin <span class="foreign">advertere</span> "turn toward," from <span class="foreign">ad-</span> "toward" (see <a href="/etym/s/ad-" target="_blank">ad-</a>) + <span class="foreign">vertere</span> "to turn" (see <a href="/etym/s/versus" target="_blank">versus</a>).&#13; <br /><br />&#13; Sense shifted to "to give notice to others, warn" (late 15c.) by influence of <a href="/etym/s/advertisement" target="_blank">advertisement</a>; specific meaning "to call attention to goods for sale, rewards, etc." had emerged by late 18c. Original meaning remains in the verb <a href="/etym/s/advert" target="_blank">advert</a> "to give attention to." Related: <a href="/etym/s/Advertised" target="_blank">Advertised</a>; <span class="foreign">advertising</span>.</dd></dl></div></div>
