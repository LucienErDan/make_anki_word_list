2. 谐音“死脾烂、死脾拧”------死脾气真烂。<br />
3. 谐音“是脾淋”-----脾脏是重要的淋巴器官，具有造血、滤血、清除衰老血细胞及参与免疫反应等功能。脾脏是机体最大的免疫器官，占全身淋巴组织总量的25%，含有大量的淋巴细胞和巨噬细胞，是机体细胞免疫和体液免疫的中心。</div>

                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/spleen">spleen</a> 脾，怒火，怒气</span><p>来自拉丁语 splen,来自希腊 splen,来自 PIE*spelgh,脾。因中世纪医学理论认为脾是人体怒火 和怒气郁积之所，因而引申词义怒火，怒气。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/spleen">spleen</a></dt><dd><b>spleen: </b><span>[13] <i>Spleen </i>comes via Old French <i>esplen </i>and Latin <i>splēn </i>from Greek <i>splén</i>, which may have been related to Latin <i>liēn </i>‘spleen’ and Greek <i>splágkhnon </i>‘entrails’ (source of English <i>splanchnic </i>‘of the viscera’ [17]). In medieval physiology many internal organs were held to be the seat of a particular emotion, and the spleen was no exception. It had several conflicting states of mind attributed to it, but the one which survives is ‘moroseness’ or ‘bad temper’, in the derived adjective <i>splenetic </i>[16].</span><br></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/spleen">spleen (n.)</a></dt><dd>c. 1300, from Old French <span class="foreign">esplen</span>, from Latin <span class="foreign">splen</span>, from Greek <span class="foreign">splen</span> "the milt, spleen," from PIE <span class="foreign">*spelgh-</span> "spleen, milt" (cognates: Sanskrit <span class="foreign">plihan-</span>, Avestan <span class="foreign">sperezan</span>, Armenian <span class="foreign">p'aicaln</span>, Latin <span class="foreign">lien</span>, Old Church Slavonic <span class="foreign">slezena</span>, Lithuanian <span class="foreign">blužnis</span>, Old Prussian <span class="foreign">blusne</span>, Old Irish <span class="foreign">selg</span> "spleen").&#13; <br /><br />&#13; Regarded in medieval physiology as the seat of morose feelings and bad temper. Hence figurative sense of "violent ill-temper" (1580s, implied in <span class="foreign">spleenful</span>); and thence <span class="foreign">spleenless</span> "free from anger, ill-humor, malice, or spite" (1610s).</dd></dl></div></div>
