                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/toy">toy</a> 玩具</span><p>来 自 中 古 英 语 toye, 玩 具 ， 玩 物 ， 可 能 来 自 荷 兰 语 toy, 工 具 ， 设 备 ， 装 饰 品 ， 来 自 Proto-Germanic*teuhana,拉出，引出，来自 PIE*deuk,拉，引导，词源同 tow,tie,duct.</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/toy">toy</a></dt><dd><b>toy: </b><span>[14] Nothing is known for certain about the origins of <i>toy</i>. It originally meant ‘amorous dalliance’ (‘So said he, and forbore not glance or toy, of amorous intent, well understood of Eve’, Milton, <i>Paradise Lost </i>1667), traces of which survive in the verbal sense ‘amuse oneself idly’. ‘Plaything’ first appeared towards the end of the 16th century.</span><br></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/toy">toy (n.)</a></dt><dd>c. 1300, "amorous playing, sport," later "piece of fun or entertainment" (c. 1500), "thing of little value, trifle" (1520s), and "thing for a child to play with" (1580s). Of uncertain origin, and there may be more than one word here. Compare Middle Dutch <span class="foreign">toy</span>, Dutch <span class="foreign">tuig</span> "tools, apparatus; stuff, trash," in <span class="foreign">speeltuig</span> "play-toy, plaything;" German <span class="foreign">Zeug</span> "stuff, matter, tools," <span class="foreign">Spielzeug</span> "plaything, toy;" Danish <span class="foreign">tøi</span>, Swedish <span class="foreign">tyg</span> "stuff, gear." Applied as an adjective to things of diminutive size, especially dogs, from 1806. <span class="foreign">Toy-boy</span> is from 1981.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/toy">toy (v.)</a></dt><dd>"deal carelessly (with), trifle," 1520s, from <a href="/etym/s/toy" target="_blank">toy</a> (n.) in its older sense.&#13; <blockquote>&#13; If he be merie and toy with any,&#13; <br />His wife will frowne, and words geve manye.&#13; <br />["Song of the Bachelor's Life," 16c.]&#13; </blockquote>&#13; Related: <span class="foreign">Toyed</span>; <span class="foreign">toying</span>.</dd></dl></div></div>
