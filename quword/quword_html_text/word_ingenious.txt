                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/ingenious">ingenious</a> 灵巧的，精巧的</span><p>来自拉丁语ingenium,天生的品质，能力，来自in-,进入，使，内在，-gen,生育，出生，词源同gene,engine.即出生就具有的，后引申词义陪明的，灵巧的，精巧的。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/ingenious">ingenious</a></dt><dd><b>ingenious: </b><span>[15] <i>Ingenious </i>used to be a more elevated term than it is today. To begin with it meant ‘highly intelligent’, but already by the 16th century it was starting to come down in the world somewhat to ‘cleverly inventive’. It comes, partly via French <i>ingénieux</i>, from Latin <i>ingeniōsus</i>, a derivative of <i>ingenium </i>‘natural talent, skill’ (a word which, like English <i>gene</i>, <i>generate</i>, <i>genital</i>, etc, goes back ultimately to Indo-European *<i>gen</i>- ‘produce’, and was also the source of English <i>engine</i>).<br><br>Its formal similarity to the distantly related <i>ingenuous </i>has led in the past to its being used for ‘honest, open, frank’, and indeed its semantic derivative <i>ingenuity </i>‘quality of being ingenious’ [16] belongs etymologically to <i>ingenuous</i>.</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/gene" target="_blank">gene</a>, <a href="/etym/s/general" target="_blank">general</a>, <a href="/etym/s/generate" target="_blank">generate</a>, <a href="/etym/s/genital" target="_blank">genital</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/ingenious">ingenious (adj.)</a></dt><dd>early 15c., "intellectual, talented," from Middle French <span class="foreign">ingénieux</span> "clever, ingenious" (Old French <span class="foreign">engeignos</span>), from Latin <span class="foreign">ingeniosus</span> "of good capacity, full of intellect; clever, gifted with genius," from <span class="foreign">ingenium</span> "innate qualities, ability," literally "that which is inborn," from <span class="foreign">in-</span> "in" (see <a href="/etym/s/in-" target="_blank">in-</a> (2)) + <span class="foreign">gignere</span>, from PIE <span class="foreign">*gen-</span> "produce" (see <a href="/etym/s/genus" target="_blank">genus</a>). Sense of "skillful, clever at contrivance" first recorded 1540s. In a sense of "crafty, clever, skillful" Middle English had <span class="foreign">enginous</span> (mid-14c.), from Old French <span class="foreign">engeignos</span>, also <span class="foreign">engineful</span> "skillful (in war)" (c. 1300). Related: <span class="foreign">Ingeniously</span>; <span class="foreign">ingeniousness</span>.</dd></dl></div></div>
