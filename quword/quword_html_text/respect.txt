                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/respect">respect</a> 尊敬，尊重</span><p>re-,再，-spect,看，词源同 specter,retrospect.即再看，反复看，引申词义尊敬，尊重。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/respect">respect</a></dt><dd><b>respect: </b><span>[14] <i>Respect </i>and <i>respite </i>[13] are ultimately the same word. Both go back to <i>respectus</i>, the past participle of Latin <i>respicere </i>‘look back at’, hence ‘look at, regard, consider’. This was a compound verb formed from the prefix <i>re</i>- ‘back’ and <i>specere </i>‘look’ (source of English <i>spectacle</i>, <i>speculate</i>, etc). <i>Respectus </i>passed into English, perhaps via Old French <i>respect</i>, as <i>respect</i>, in the sense ‘regard, relation’ (as in <i>with respect to</i>); the key modern meaning ‘deference, esteem’ developed towards the end of the 16th century.<br><br>An earlier borrowing of <i>respectus </i>into Old French produced <i>respit</i>, which preserved another meaning of the Latin word, ‘refuge’. This was the source of English <i>respite</i>.</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/inspect" target="_blank">inspect</a>, <a href="/etym/s/respite" target="_blank">respite</a>, <a href="/etym/s/spectacle" target="_blank">spectacle</a>, <a href="/etym/s/spectator" target="_blank">spectator</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/respect">respect (n.)</a></dt><dd>late 14c., "relationship, relation; regard, consideration," from Old French <span class="foreign">respect</span> and directly from Latin <span class="foreign">respectus</span> "regard, a looking at," literally "act of looking back (or often) at one," noun use of past participle of <span class="foreign">respicere</span> "look back at, regard, consider," from <span class="foreign">re-</span> "back" (see <a href="/etym/s/re-" target="_blank">re-</a>) + <span class="foreign">specere</span> "look at" (see <a href="/etym/s/scope" target="_blank">scope</a> (n.1)). Meanings "feeling of esteem excited by actions or attributes of someone or something; courteous or considerate treatment due to personal worth or power" are from 1580s, as is sense of "point, particular feature."</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/respect">respect (v.)</a></dt><dd>1540s, "to regard," from Middle French <span class="foreign">respecter</span> "look back; respect; delay," from Latin <span class="foreign">respectere</span>, frequentative of <span class="foreign">respicere</span> (see <a href="/etym/s/respect" target="_blank">respect</a> (n.). Meaning "treat with deferential regard or esteem" is from 1550s. Sense of "refrain from injuring" is from 1620s. Meaning "have reference to" is from 1560s. Related: <span class="foreign">Respected</span>; <span class="foreign">respecting</span>.&#13; <br /><br />&#13; To <span class="foreign">respect the person</span> was "show undue bias toward (or against) based on regard for the outward circumstances of a person;" hence <span class="foreign">respecter of persons</span>, usually with negative, from Acts x:34, in the 1611 translation.</dd></dl></div></div>

            <div class="row" id="yd-liju">
