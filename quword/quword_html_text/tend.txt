                        <div class="page-header"><h3>记忆方法</h3></div>
            <div class="row" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;">1. tend (v.1): a shortening of attend.<br />
2. tend (v.2): tend- => tend "move in a certain direction". 伸展到某个方向，但具体是哪个方向不确定，因此后面接to(towards)来指明具体的方向。</div>

                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/tend">tend</a></dt><dd><b>tend: </b><span>English has two distinct words <i>tend</i>, but they come from the same ultimate source. <i>Tend </i>‘look after’ [14] is short for <i>attend</i>, which goes back to Latin compound verb based on <i>tendere </i>‘stretch’ – source of English <i>tend </i>‘have an inclination’ [14]. <i>Tendere </i>itself was descended from the Indo-European base *<i>ten</i>- ‘stretch’ (source also of English <i>tenant</i>, <i>tenuous</i>, <i>thin</i>, <i>tone</i>, <i>tune</i>, etc), and its other contributions to English include <i>contend </i>[15], <i>distend </i>[14], <i>extend</i>, <i>intend</i>, <i>portend </i>[15], <i>pretend</i>, and also <i>ostensible</i>, <i>tendency </i>[17], <i>tense</i>, <i>tension</i>, and <i>tent</i>.</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/contend" target="_blank">contend</a>, <a href="/etym/s/distend" target="_blank">distend</a>, <a href="/etym/s/extend" target="_blank">extend</a>, <a href="/etym/s/intend" target="_blank">intend</a>, <a href="/etym/s/ostensible" target="_blank">ostensible</a>, <a href="/etym/s/portend" target="_blank">portend</a>, <a href="/etym/s/pretend" target="_blank">pretend</a>, <a href="/etym/s/tenant" target="_blank">tenant</a>, <a href="/etym/s/tense" target="_blank">tense</a>, <a href="/etym/s/tent" target="_blank">tent</a>, <a href="/etym/s/tenterhooks" target="_blank">tenterhooks</a>, <a href="/etym/s/tenuous" target="_blank">tenuous</a>, <a href="/etym/s/thin" target="_blank">thin</a>, <a href="/etym/s/tone" target="_blank">tone</a>, <a href="/etym/s/tune" target="_blank">tune</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/tend">tend (v.1)</a></dt><dd>"to incline, to move in a certain direction," early 14c., from Old French <span class="foreign">tendre</span> "stretch out, hold forth, hand over, offer" (11c.), from Latin <span class="foreign">tendere</span> "to stretch, extend, make tense; aim, direct; direct oneself, hold a course" (see <a href="/etym/s/tenet" target="_blank">tenet</a>).</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/tend">tend (v.2)</a></dt><dd>"attend to," c. 1200, a shortening of Middle English <span class="foreign">atenden</span> (see <a href="/etym/s/attend" target="_blank">attend</a>).</dd></dl></div></div>
