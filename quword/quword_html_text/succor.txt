                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/succor">succor</a> 救助，救援，帮助</span><p>suc-,在下，向上，-cor,跑，词源同 current,course.即跑过来，引申词义帮助，救助，救援等。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/succor">succor (n.)</a></dt><dd>c. 1200, <span class="foreign">socour</span>, earlier <span class="foreign">socours</span> "aid, help," from Anglo-French <span class="foreign">succors</span> "help, aid," Old French <span class="foreign">socors</span>, <span class="foreign">sucurres</span> "aid, help, assistance" (Modern French <span class="foreign">secours</span>), from Medieval Latin <span class="foreign">succursus</span> "help, assistance," from past participle of Latin <span class="foreign">succurrere</span> "run to help, hasten to the aid of," from assimilated form of <span class="foreign">sub</span> "up to" (see <a href="/etym/s/sub-" target="_blank">sub-</a>) + <span class="foreign">currere</span> "to run" (see <a href="/etym/s/current" target="_blank">current</a> (adj.)). Final <span class="foreign">-s</span> mistaken in English as a plural inflection and dropped late 13c. Meaning "one who aids or helps" is from c. 1300.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/succor">succor (v.)</a></dt><dd>late 13c., "help or relieve when in difficulty," from Old French <span class="foreign">succurre</span> "to help, assist" (Modern French <span class="foreign">secourir</span>), from Latin <span class="foreign">succerrere</span> "to help, assist" (see <a href="/etym/s/succor" target="_blank">succor</a> (n.)). Related: <span class="foreign">Succored</span>; <span class="foreign">succoring</span>.</dd></dl></div></div>
