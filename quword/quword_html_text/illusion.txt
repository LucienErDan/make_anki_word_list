                        <div class="page-header"><h3>记忆方法</h3></div>
            <div class="row" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;">1、在里面、内部玩(play)，其实就是感觉在自己的脑海中玩、在自己的心中玩，也就是在自己的想象中玩，这种玩当然就是一种幻觉、错觉了，只不过是自欺欺人罢了。<br />
2、通过连哄带骗的戏耍、玩弄(play, joke, jest)等方式，将你吸引进去，将(使)你置于一种幻觉、错觉、假象之中，让你信以为真而不自知。<br />
3、il- "in, into" + lus- + -ion.<br />
4. 谐音“一路淫”--- 一路上都在意淫 --- 意淫当然是幻觉了。</div>

                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/illusion">illusion</a> 虚幻</span><p>il-,进入，使，-lus,玩乐，玩耍，词源同ludic,elude,elusive.引申词义玩弄，欺骗，虚幻。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/illusion">illusion</a></dt><dd><b>illusion: </b><span>[14] The notion of ‘play’ is at the etymological heart of <i>illusion </i>(as indeed of its close relatives <i>allusion </i>[16], <i>delusion </i>[15], and <i>elude </i>[16]). It came via Old French from Latin <i>illūsiō</i>, a derivative of <i>illūdere </i>‘make fun of’. This was a compound verb formed from the prefix <i>in</i>- and <i>lūdere </i>‘play’ (source of English <i>ludicrous </i>[17]). In classical Latin <i>illūsiō </i>meant ‘mockery’, and no semantic shift seems to have taken place until post-classical times, when it moved to ‘deceit’ (a sense originally taken over by English).</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/allusion" target="_blank">allusion</a>, <a href="/etym/s/delusion" target="_blank">delusion</a>, <a href="/etym/s/elude" target="_blank">elude</a>, <a href="/etym/s/ludicrous" target="_blank">ludicrous</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/illusion">illusion (n.)</a></dt><dd>mid-14c., "act of deception," from Old French <span class="foreign">illusion</span> "a mocking, deceit, deception" (12c.), from Latin <span class="foreign">illusionem</span> (nominative <span class="foreign">illusio</span>) "a mocking, jesting, irony," from <span class="foreign">illudere</span> "mock at," literally "to play with," from assimilated form of <span class="foreign">in-</span> "at, upon" (see <a href="/etym/s/in-" target="_blank">in-</a> (2)) + <span class="foreign">ludere</span> "to play" (see <a href="/etym/s/ludicrous" target="_blank">ludicrous</a>). Sense of "deceptive appearance" developed in Church Latin and was attested in English by late 14c. Related: <span class="foreign">Illusioned</span> "full of illusions" (1920).</dd></dl></div></div>
                                    <div class="page-header"><h3><a href="/liju/w/illusion">权威例句</a></h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-liju">
                 <dl><dt>1. Sloping walls on the bulk of the building create an optical <b>illusion</b>. </dt> <dd>大楼主体的斜墙给人造成一种视错觉。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>2. She laboured under the <b>illusion</b> that I knew what I was doing. </dt> <dd>她有一个错觉，以为我知道自己在做什么。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>3. This eerie calm is an <b>illusion</b>. </dt> <dd>这种怪诞的平静是一种假象。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>4. The sun appears to go round the Earth, but it's an <b>illusion</b>. </dt> <dd>太阳看起来好像绕着地球转, 但这只是个错觉.</dd> <p>来自《简明英汉词典》</p> </dl><dl><dt>5. The mirrors all round the walls give an <b>illusion</b> of greater space. </dt> <dd>墙壁周围的镜子造成一种较大空间的假象.</dd> <p>来自《简明英汉词典》</p> </dl>                 <dl><p>[ <a href="/liju/w/illusion">illusion 造句</a> ]</p></dl>
