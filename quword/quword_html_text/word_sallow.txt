                        <div class="page-header"><h3>记忆方法</h3></div>
            <div class="row" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;">1. from PIE root *sal- "dirty, gray", *sal-有多重含义，其中之一就是此意，来源于原始德语、古德语。<br />
2. 由首辅音即音变而得到：sallow.<br />
3. 形近词：wallow, tallow, swallow, shallow, sallow, hallow, callow, fallow.<br />
4. salic- => sallow.<br />
5. 谐音“沙柳”。<br />
6. 谐音“沙喽、晒喽、色喽”-----所谓黄沙、黄沙，就是像黄沙一样的颜色。沙子都是呈现出一种土黄色的。<br />
7. 首字母s是颜色的色的声母，所以该词是表示颜色。<br />
8. soil + yellow => *sellow => sallow: 土黄色, sand + yellow => sallow.</div>

            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/sallow">sallow</a></dt><dd><b>sallow: </b><span>[OE] English has two distinct words <i>sallow</i>. The adjective goes back to a prehistoric Germanic *<i>salwa</i>-, which was also borrowed into French as <i>sale </i>‘dirty’. The underlying meaning appears to be ‘dark-coloured’. Its only surviving relative among the mainstream Germanic languages is Icelandic <i>sölr </i>‘yellow’. <i>Sallow </i>‘willow’ comes from a prehistoric Germanic *<i>salkhaz</i>, which also produced French <i>saule </i>‘willow’ and was distantly related to Latin <i>salix </i>‘willow’.</span><br></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/sallow">sallow (n.)</a></dt><dd>"shrubby willow plant," Old English <span class="foreign">sealh</span> (Anglian <span class="foreign">salh</span>), from Proto-Germanic <span class="foreign">*salhjon</span> (cognates: Old Norse <span class="foreign">selja</span>, Old High German <span class="foreign">salaha</span>, and first element in German compound <span class="foreign">Salweide</span>), from PIE <span class="foreign">*sal(i)k-</span> "willow" (cognates: Latin <span class="foreign">salix</span> "willow," Middle Irish <span class="foreign">sail</span>, Welsh <span class="foreign">helygen</span>, Breton <span class="foreign">halegen</span> "willow"). French <span class="foreign">saule</span> "willow" is from Frankish <span class="foreign">salha</span>, from the Germanic root. Used in Palm Sunday processions and decorations in England before the importing of real palm leaves began.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/sallow">sallow (adj.)</a></dt><dd>Old English <span class="foreign">salo</span> "dusky, dark" (related to <span class="foreign">sol</span> "dark, dirty"), from Proto-Germanic <span class="foreign">*salwa-</span> (cognates: Middle Dutch <span class="foreign">salu</span> "discolored, dirty," Old High German <span class="foreign">salo</span> "dirty gray," Old Norse <span class="foreign">sölr</span> "dirty yellow"), from PIE root <span class="foreign">*sal-</span> (2) "dirty, gray" (cognates: Old Church Slavonic <span class="foreign">slavojocije</span> "grayish-blue color," Russian <span class="foreign">solovoj</span> "cream-colored"). Related: <span class="foreign">Sallowness</span>.</dd></dl></div></div>
