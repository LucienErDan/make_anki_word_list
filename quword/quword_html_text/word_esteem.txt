                        <div class="page-header"><h3>记忆方法</h3></div>
            <div class="row" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;">1. estimate => esteem.<br />
2. Originally, esteem meant much the same as estimate does: 'evaluate, assess'. But later it had passed into 'think highly of' (a semantic development interestingly paralleled by rate).<br />
3. 由最初的本义“估计、评价”到后来引申为“很高的评价或估计”，既然对其评价很高自然就会很“尊敬，尊重，敬重”。<br />
4. from *aes-timos [ aes (genitive aeris) "brass, copper" ] "one who cuts copper, one who cuts ore", 可能就是通过切开矿石或铜来鉴别、判断矿石或铜质量如何。<br />
5. 谐音“爱死地母”-----爱死大地之神“地母”。</div>

                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/esteem">esteem</a> 尊重</span><p>来自拉丁语aestimare, 估计，评估，判定价值，来自aes, 铜，词源同ore, -tim, 砍，切，词源同anatomy. 原指铸造铜币，估计并判定币值，该词义见estimate.同时，引申义尊重，尊敬，即值得一看的，值得考虑的。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/esteem">esteem</a></dt><dd><b>esteem: </b><span>[15] <i>Esteem </i>and <i>estimate </i>[16] are fairly open about their relationship, but there is another, more heavily disguised member of the family: <i>aim</i>. All three come ultimately from Latin <i>aestimāre</i>. <i>Estimate </i>was a straightforward borrowing from the Latin past participle <i>aestimātus</i>, but <i>esteem </i>came via Old French <i>estimer</i>, and <i>aim </i>from the reduced Old French form <i>esmer</i>. Originally, <i>esteem </i>meant much the same as <i>estimate </i>does: ‘evaluate, assess’. But as early as the 16th century it had passed into ‘think highly of’ (a semantic development interestingly paralleled in the 20th century by <i>rate</i>).</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/aim" target="_blank">aim</a>, <a href="/etym/s/estimate" target="_blank">estimate</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/esteem">esteem (v.)</a></dt><dd>mid-15c., from Old French <span class="foreign">estimer</span> "to estimate, determine" (14c.), from Latin <span class="foreign">aestimare</span> "to value, determine the value of, appraise," perhaps ultimately from <span class="foreign">*ais-temos</span> "one who cuts copper," i.e. mints money (but de Vaan finds this "not very credible"). At first used as we would now use <span class="foreign">estimate</span>; sense of "value, respect" is 1530s. Related: <a href="/etym/s/Esteemed" target="_blank">Esteemed</a>; <span class="foreign">esteeming</span>.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/esteem">esteem (n.)</a></dt><dd>(also <span class="foreign">steem</span>, <span class="foreign">extyme</span>), mid-14c., "account, value, worth," from French <span class="foreign">estime</span>, from <span class="foreign">estimer</span> (see <a href="/etym/s/esteem" target="_blank">esteem</a> (v.)). Meaning "high regard" is from 1610s.</dd></dl></div></div>
