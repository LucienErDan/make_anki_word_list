                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/spoil">spoil</a> 脏物，战利品，破坏，糟蹋，变质，腐败，溺爱，娇惯</span><p>来自拉丁语 spoliare,抢劫，打劫，剥落，剥除衣服，来自 spolium,战利品，原义为剥皮，来 自 PIE*spel,分开，劈开，词源同 spill,split.引申词义破坏，糟蹋等，后用于指家长对小孩的 溺爱，娇惯，纵容，即糟蹋小孩。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/spoil">spoil</a></dt><dd><b>spoil: </b><span>[13] Latin <i>spolium </i>originally denoted ‘skin stripped from a killed animal’ (it went back ultimately to the Indo-European base *<i>spel</i>- ‘split, burst’, which also produced German <i>spalten </i>‘split’, and probably English <i>spill </i>and <i>split</i>). It broadened out metaphorically via ‘weapons stripped from a fallen enemy’ to ‘booty’ in general, which lies behind English <i>spoils</i>.<br><br>The word itself was borrowed from Old French <i>espoille</i>, a derivative of the verb <i>espoillier</i>, which in turn went back to Latin <i>spoliāre </i>‘despoil’ (source of English <i>spoliation </i>[14]), a derivative of <i>spolium</i>. The verb <i>spoil </i>came either from Old French <i>espoillier</i>, or is short for <i>despoil </i>[13], which went back via Old French <i>despoillier </i>to Latin <i>dēspoliāre</i>.<br><br>It used to mean ‘strip of possessions’, as <i>despoil </i>still does, but in the 16th century it moved across to take over the semantic territory of the similarsounding <i>spill </i>(which once meant ‘destroy, ruin’).</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/despoil" target="_blank">despoil</a>, <a href="/etym/s/spoliation" target="_blank">spoliation</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/spoil">spoil (v.)</a></dt><dd>c. 1300, "to strip (someone) of clothes, strip a slain enemy," from Old French <span class="foreign">espillier</span> "to strip, plunder, pillage," from Latin <span class="foreign">spoliare</span> "to strip, uncover, lay bare; strip of clothing, rob, plunder, pillage," from <span class="foreign">spolia</span>, plural of <span class="foreign">spolium</span> "arms taken from an enemy, booty;" originally "skin stripped from a killed animal," from PIE <span class="foreign">*spol-yo-</span>, perhaps from root <span class="foreign">*spel-</span> "to split, to break off" (see <a href="/etym/s/spill" target="_blank">spill</a> (v.)).&#13; <br /><br />&#13; From late 14c. in English as "strip with violence, rob, pillage, plunder, dispossess; impoverish with excessive taxation." Used c. 1400 as the verb to describe Christ's harrowing of Hell. Sense of "destroy, ruin, damage so as to render useless" is from 1560s; that of "to over-indulge" (a child, etc.) is from 1640s (implied in <span class="foreign">spoiled</span>). Intransitive sense of "become tainted, go bad, lose freshness" is from 1690s. To be <span class="foreign">spoiling for</span> (a fight, etc.) is from 1865, from notion that one will "spoil" if he doesn't get it.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/spoil">spoil (n.)</a></dt><dd>"booty, goods captured in time of war," mid-14c., <span class="foreign">spoils</span> (collective singular), from <a href="/etym/s/spoil" target="_blank">spoil</a> (v.) or else from Old French <span class="foreign">espoille</span> "booty, spoil," from the verb in French, and in part from Latin <span class="foreign">spolium</span>. Also from the Latin noun are Spanish <span class="foreign">espolio</span>, Italian <span class="foreign">spoglio</span>.&#13; <br /><br />&#13; Transferred sense of "that which has been acquired by special effort" is from 1750. <span class="foreign">Spoils</span> has stood cynically for "public offices, etc." aince at least 1770. <span class="foreign">Spoils system</span> in U.S. politics attested by 1839, commonly associated with the administration of President Andrew Jackson, on the notion of "to the victor belongs the spoils."</dd></dl></div></div>
                                    <div class="page-header"><h3><a href="/liju/w/spoil">权威例句</a></h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-liju">
                 <dl><dt>1. We all know that fats <b>spoil</b> by becoming rancid. </dt> <dd>我们都知道油脂变质后会发臭。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>2. They could not afford to <b>spoil</b> those maps by careless colouring. </dt> <dd>若是上色的时候不小心弄坏了那些地图，他们可承担不起。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>3. Her untimely return could <b>spoil</b> Miss Melville's entire programme for the evening. </dt> <dd>她提前回来可能会破坏掉梅尔维尔小姐今晚的整个计划。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>4. It's important not to let mistakes <b>spoil</b> your life. </dt> <dd>重要的是不要让错误毁了你的生活。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl><dl><dt>5. I had an uneasy feeling that he was going to <b>spoil</b> it. </dt> <dd>我有种不安的感觉，觉得他会把事情搞糟。</dd> <p>来自柯林斯例句</p> </dl>                 <dl><p>[ <a href="/liju/w/spoil">spoil 造句</a> ]</p></dl>
