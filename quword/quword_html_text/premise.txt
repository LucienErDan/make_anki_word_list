                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/premise">premise</a> 假设，前提，房屋，土地</span><p>来自古法语premisse,来自拉丁语premissa,前提，假设，在前面的论断，来自pre-,在前，早于，-miss,送出，提出，词源同mission,emit.在法律文件中意为前面陈述之事，描述物，通常指土地或房屋，因而引申该词义。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/premise">premise</a></dt><dd><b>premise: </b><span>[14] <i>Premise </i>comes via Old French <i>premisse </i>from medieval Latin <i>praemissa</i>, a noun use of the past participle of Latin <i>praemittere </i>‘send ahead’. This was a compound verb formed from the prefix <i>prae</i>- ‘before’ and <i>mittere </i>‘send’ (source of English <i>admit</i>, <i>commit</i>, <i>mission</i>, <i>transmit</i>, etc). It first entered English as a technical term in logic, in which its underlying meaning is of a proposition ‘set before’ someone.<br><br>But it was also used in the plural as a legal term, meaning ‘matters stated previously’. In a conveyance or will, such ‘matters’ were often houses or other buildings referred to specifically at the beginning of the document, and so the term <i>premises </i>came to denote such buildings.</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/admit" target="_blank">admit</a>, <a href="/etym/s/commit" target="_blank">commit</a>, <a href="/etym/s/mission" target="_blank">mission</a>, <a href="/etym/s/permit" target="_blank">permit</a>, <a href="/etym/s/submit" target="_blank">submit</a>, <a href="/etym/s/transmit" target="_blank">transmit</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/premise">premise (n.)</a></dt><dd>late 14c., in logic, "a previous proposition from which another follows," from Old French <span class="foreign">premisse</span> (14c.), from Medieval Latin <span class="foreign">premissa</span> <span class="foreign">(propositio</span> or <span class="foreign">sententia)</span> "(the proposition) set before," noun use of fem. past participle of Latin <span class="foreign">praemittere</span> "send forward, put before," from <span class="foreign">prae</span> "before" (see <a href="/etym/s/pre-" target="_blank">pre-</a>) + <span class="foreign">mittere</span> "to send" (see <a href="/etym/s/mission" target="_blank">mission</a>). In legal documents it meant "matter previously stated" (early 15c.), which in deeds or wills often was a house or building, hence the extended meaning "house or building, with grounds" (1730).</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/premise">premise (v.)</a></dt><dd>"to state before something else," mid-15c., from <a href="/etym/s/premise" target="_blank">premise</a> (n.). Related: <span class="foreign">Premised</span>; <span class="foreign">premising</span>.</dd></dl></div></div>
