                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>中文词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/economy">economy</a> 经济学</span><p>来自PIE*weik, 家，词源同vicinity, village. 字母w脱落。-nomy, 学说，管理。原指管理家的学问，后指经济学。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/economy">economy</a></dt><dd><b>economy: </b><span>[16] The underlying notion contained in the word <i>economy </i>is of ‘household management’. It comes, via French or Latin, from Greek <i>oikonomíā</i>, a derivative of <i>oikonómos</i>, a term for the ‘steward of a household’. This was a compound noun formed from <i>oikos </i>‘house’ (a word related to the -<i>wich </i>element in many English place-names) and <i>némein </i>‘manage’ (ultimate source of English <i>antinomian </i>and <i>nomad</i>).<br><br>The original sense ‘household management’ was carried through into English. It broadened out in the 17th century to the management of a nation’s resources (a concept at first termed more fully <i>political economy</i>), while the use of the derivative <i>economics </i>for the theoretical study of the creation and consumption of wealth dates from the early 19th century.</span><br><span class="cognate">=&gt; <a href="/etym/s/antinomian" target="_blank">antinomian</a>, <a href="/etym/s/ecology" target="_blank">ecology</a>, <a href="/etym/s/nomad" target="_blank">nomad</a></span></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/economy">economy (n.)</a></dt><dd>1530s, "household management," from Latin <span class="foreign">oeconomia</span> (source of French <span class="foreign">économie</span>, Spanish <span class="foreign">economia</span>, German <span class="foreign">Ökonomie</span>, etc.), from Greek <span class="foreign">oikonomia</span> "household management, thrift," from <span class="foreign">oikonomos</span> "manager, steward," from <span class="foreign">oikos</span> "house, abode, dwelling" (cognate with Latin <span class="foreign">vicus</span> "district," <span class="foreign">vicinus</span> "near;" Old English <span class="foreign">wic</span> "dwelling, village;" see <a href="/etym/s/villa" target="_blank">villa</a>) + <span class="foreign">nomos</span> "managing," from <span class="foreign">nemein</span> "manage" (see <a href="/etym/s/numismatic" target="_blank">numismatic</a>). Meaning "frugality, judicious use of resources" is from 1660s. The sense of "wealth and resources of a country" (short for <span class="foreign">political economy</span>) is from 1650s.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/economy">economy (adj.)</a></dt><dd>1821 as a term in advertising, at first meant simply "cheaper," then "bigger and thus cheaper per unit or amount" (1950). See <a href="/etym/s/economy" target="_blank">economy</a> (n.).</dd></dl></div></div>
