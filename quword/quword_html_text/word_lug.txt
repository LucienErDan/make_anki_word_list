            <div class="row" id="yd-ciyuan" style="font-family:SimSun,serif;"><span class="ciyuan-title"><a target="_blank" href="/ciyuan/s/lug">lug</a> 手柄，拖，拉</span><p>词源不详。</p></div>
                                                <div class="page-header"><h3>英语词源</h3></div>
            <div class="row"><div class="thumbnail" id="yd-etym"><dl><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/lug">lug</a></dt><dd><b>lug: </b><span>English has three words <i>lug</i>, two of them possibly connected. The verb, ‘pull’ [14], may be related to Swedish <i>lugga </i>‘pull someone’s hair’, suggesting a Scandinavian origin. And it has been pointed out that the various meanings of the noun <i>lug </i>[15], such as ‘ear’ and ‘projecting handle’, share a common semantic element ‘capable of being held (and pulled)’, so the noun may have been derived from the verb. The <i>lug</i>- of <i>lugworm </i>[17] may be of Celtic origin.</span><br></dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/lug">lug (v.)</a></dt><dd>late 14c., "to move (something) heavily or slowly," from Scandinavian (compare Swedish <span class="foreign">lugga</span>, Norwegian <span class="foreign">lugge</span> "to pull by the hair"); see <a href="/etym/s/lug" target="_blank">lug</a> (n.). Related: <span class="foreign">Lugged</span>; <span class="foreign">lugging</span>.</dd><dt><a target="_blank" href="/etym/s/lug">lug (n.)</a></dt><dd>1620s, "handle of a pitcher," from <span class="foreign">lugge</span> (Scottish) "earflap of a cap, ear" (late 15c.; according to OED, the common word for "ear" in 19c. Scotland), probably from Scandinavian (compare Swedish <span class="foreign">lugg</span> "forelock," Norwegian <span class="foreign">lugg</span> "tuft of hair"). The connecting notion is "something that can be gripped and pulled." Applied 19c. to mechanical objects that can be grabbed or gripped. Meaning "stupid fellow" is from 1924; that of "lout, sponger" is 1931, American English. Compare <span class="foreign">lug-nut</span> (1869), nut closed at one end as a cap.</dd></dl></div></div>

    document.getElementById('yd-search').onsubmit = function() {

        if (word == '') {
            return false;
        } else if (word.search(/^[\w \-]+$/) == 0) {
            top.location.href = '/w/' + encodeURIComponent(word.replace(/ /g, '_'));
            return false;

                var char_code = match.charCodeAt(0) - 0xfee0;
                return String.fromCharCode(char_code);
            });
        }
